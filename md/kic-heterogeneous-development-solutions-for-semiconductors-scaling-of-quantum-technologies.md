# KIC - Heterogeneous development solutions for semiconductors & scaling of quantum technologies

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/kic-heterogeneous-development-solutions-for-semiconductors-scaling-of-quantum-technologies

This call for proposals aims to fund fundamental and applied research in two areas: semiconductors and quantum technologies. This program, a collaboration between NWO and TKI-HTSM (Holland High Tech), aims to contribute to the development of these two key technologies from the National Technology Strategy (NTS).

### Purpose

This call for proposals aims to fund fundamental and applied research in two areas: semiconductors and quantum technologies. In the field of semiconductors, the focus is on heterogeneous development solutions, with challenges in the following areas: chiplets, advanced packaging, and the metrology required for this. In the field of quantum, the focus is on expanding knowledge in the field of manufacturing and scaling up quantum systems. This Call for Proposals also encourages crossovers between semiconductor and quantum technologies.

### Who can apply

Researchers may submit an application if they have a tenured position (and therefore a paid position for an indefinite period*) or a tenure track agreement at one of the following research organisations:

- universities and universities of applied sciences;- university medical centres;- institutes affiliated to the Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences (KNAW) or NWO;
- Netherlands Cancer Institute;
- the Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics in Nijmegen;
- Naturalis Biodiversity Center;
- Advanced Research Centre for NanoLithography (ARCNL);
- TO2 institutes**
- Princess Máxima Center.
1. * Researchers employed by a university of applied sciences may also apply as the main applicant with a fixed-term, paid contract.
2. ** Researchers from TO2 institutes can only apply as co-applicants.

### Consortium building

For an application in this Call for Proposals, a minimum of €600,000 and a maximum of €3,200,000 can be requested in NWO funding. NWO will fund a maximum of 80% of the total project size; the remainder of the project budget is provided through mandatory 20% co-funding (in-cash, in-kind or a mix thereof). NWO will never fund less than 50% of the total project size.

During the preparation phase, applicants must choose their preferred theme: "semiconductor technologies" or "quantum." Of the total budget, €5.925.000 is reserved for proposals related to semiconductors and €5.925.000 for quantum-related proposals.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this Call for Proposals will be assessed based on the following criteria:

1. Problem definition and analysis (20%)
2. Expected impact and route to impact (20%)
3. Quality of the consortium (30%)
4. Quality of the research (30%)

### Procedure

The application procedure consists of the following steps:

- matchmaking (optional);
- submission of the proposal;
- admissibility of the proposal;
- peer review by referees;
- rebuttal;
- initial advice from the assessment committee;
- interview selection;
- interview;
- assessment committee meeting;
- decision-making.
- An external, independent assessment committee will be assigned for this Call for proposals, consisting of representatives from science and practice with knowledge of the field.
