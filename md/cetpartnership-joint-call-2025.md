### Purpose

The Clean Energy Transition Partnership (CETPartnership) is a multilateral and strategic partnership of national and regional research, development and innovation (RDI) programmes in European Member States and Associated Countries. The CETPartnership aims to empower the clean energy transition and contribute to the EU’s goal of becoming the first climate-neutral continent by 2050, by pooling national and regional RDTI funding for a broad variety of technologies and system solutions required to make the transition.

In this 2025-call of the partnership NWO funds research on (1) multi-vector interactions between the integrated energy system and industrial frameworks and (2) hydrogen and renewable fuels.

Funded consortia bring together international partners from academia, companies and Industry organizations, local/regional governments, research organizations and NGO’s.

### Who can apply

The CETPartnership Joint Call 2025 is structured by the joint call text and nine Call modules describing specific topics and/or challenges that applicants shall address in their project proposal. Applicants must choose and apply to a specific call module when submitting their project proposal. 

Applications need to be submitted by research consortia including teams from a minimum of three countries participating in the call (including a minimum of two from EU Member States or EU Horizon Europe Associated Countries). Specific additional requirements apply for the 2 call modules that are eligible for NWO funding:

- CM2025-01 Multi-vector interactions between the integrated energy system and industrial frameworks
- CM2025-05 Hydrogen and renewable fuels
Full, associate and assistant professors, lectors and other researchers with a comparable position may submit an application.

An applicant may only request NWO funding for one project (part of a European consortium) in this call of the CETPartnership.

See the CETPartnership Call Documents for details on transnational eligibility criteria and participating countries. For scientists based in the Kingdom of the Netherlands, applying for NWO funding, the NWO eligibility criteria apply.

### What to apply for

For research proposals a maximum of €400.000 can be applied for from NWO. Funding can only be requested for research on the following call modules: CM2025-01, CM2025-05.

The maximum project duration is 3 years. No limit has been set on the total international consortium size (budget or partners), but per call module an indicative project size and minimum amount of consortium partners is described in the CETPartnership Call Documents per call module.

The budget modules that are available within this Call for proposals are: Research Leave, Postdoc, Non Scientific Staff, Personnel TO2 institutes and universities of applied sciences, Benchfee for Postdoc positions, Material Costs, Knowledge Utilisation and Internationalisation. The maximum amounts for each module are stated in the Funding Organisation rules (part NWO) in the Call Documents.

For further details, see the Call Documents at the [CETPartnership website](https://cetpartnership.eu/calls/joint-call-2025/documents).

### Assessment

### Criteria

Pre-proposals and full proposals will be reviewed on the basis of the following three criteria:

- Excellence
- Impact
- Quality and efficiency of the implementation
Please see the full CETPartnership Call Documents for the criteria including the sub-criteria.

### Procedure

The assessment in this call will be done in two stages. In the first stage consortia are invited to submit a pre-proposal. Only consortia that were successful in the first stage can submit a full proposal. Per individual call module each proposal will be evaluated by three independent experts. The proposals will be ranked per call module based on the criteria in the call text. Based on the ranking and the budget available, the national and regional funding agencies will take the funding decision. At NWO the Executive Board will take this decision regarding the dedicated NWO budget.
