# KIC Together for a better life with inherited heart disease: Innovations for early detection, risk prediction and therapy

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/kic-together-for-a-better-life-with-inherited-heart-disease-innovations-for-early-detection-risk-prediction-and-therapy

Hereditary heart diseases lead to severe symptoms at an early age and therefore have a major impact on patients and their surroundings. The NWO - Heart Foundation partnership funds inter- and transdisciplinary research into early detection, risk prediction and therapy in order to increase the number of life years with a good quality of life. The research also explicitly covers the social aspects involved.

## Explore

### Purpose

The aim of this call for proposals is to fund research that provides insights and results that relevant parties will use to improve early detection, risk prediction and new treatments. Within this call are three inextricably linked sub-themes: early detection, risk prediction and therapy.

- Early detection: Hereditary heart diseases can cause severe symptoms or death suddenly and at a young age. Obduction and DNA testing of young deceased people are not always performed in the Netherlands, which requires improvement. Family members should also be better detected and invited for genetic counselling, with an emphasis on interdisciplinary collaboration.
- Risk prediction: current knowledge is insufficient to properly predict who will develop symptoms of hereditary heart disease and when. This makes treatment decisions complex and may lead to overtreatment or undertreatment. Better risk prediction can improve quality of life and optimise care. Greater understanding of genetic and non-genetic causes is needed, as well as better data infrastructure at national level.
- Therapy: Current treatments include lifestyle advice, medication and/or ICDs, but have drawbacks and are not always effective. Improving quality of life and developing new treatments, including gene therapies, is necessary. Innovative research can lead to improved survival rates and prospects for a cure for a broader group of patients.

### Who can apply

Pre-proposals and full applications are submitted by a main applicant and one or more co-applicants. An application is set up by a consortium, in which other participants are involved in addition to the applicants. In this consortium the main applicant and co-applicants together represent at least two of the three research fields (humanities, natural sciences and social sciences), in which the natural sciences are always included. In addition, collaboration partners and potential co-funders should be included. For the full application, a consortium should consist of at least a main applicant, co-applicants, cooperation partners and at least two co-funders. In addition, at least two experts by experience, with relevant experience in the subject of the application, should always be part of the consortium.

### What to apply for

The available budget for this call totals €7,816,000. The grant amounts to a maximum of 90% of the total project size. The minimum co-financing requirement is 10% of the total project budget (in kind/in cash) from a private party.

### When to apply

- The deadline for submitting sub proposals is 24 September 2024, at 14:00:00 CEST.
- The deadline for submitting the full application is 27 May 2025, at 14:00:00 CEST.

### Assessment

## Criteria

The applications submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Problem posed and problem analysis, contribution to solution
2. Expected impact and route to impact
3. Quality of the consortium
4. Quality of the research
Criteria 1 and 2 each carry a 25% weighting in the overall assessment, criterion 3, a weighting of 30% and criterion 4, a weighting of 20%.

## Procedure

The call consists of 2 phases. Sub-proposals are submitted in phase 1. After assessment by an external committee, the consortia of several sub-proposals are selected and invited to jointly develop one full proposal (phase 2). Development of the full proposal is supported by workshops.

Details about the procedure are described in chapter 4 of the call text

### Write proposal

In phase 1, sub-proposals are submitted. This is a concise application that outlines which sub-themes the applicant, co-applicants and collaboration partners want to address and how these contribute to the objectives and impact described in the call.
