# KIC Collaborative business models for a circular economy

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/kic-collaborative-business-models-for-a-circular-economy

The transition to a circular economy requires organisations to find new forms of collaboration in and around a production chain. This call for proposals focuses on research into the organisation and financing of collaborative business models for circular partnerships that ensure a sustainable distribution of investments, returns and risks between parties.

## Explore

### Purpose

With this Call for Proposals, we invite interdisciplinary and transdisciplinary consortia to investigate how collaborative business models can reconfigure value creation, risk sharing and financing in such a way that systemic barriers in the transition from linear to circular production systems are overcome. Three aspects are central to this:

1. The creation of resilient business models and organisational forms for collaboration within circular production chains, networks and ecosystems.
2. The balance of risks and (non-)financial values that this collaboration delivers.
3. Financing models for collaborative business cases.
All three aspects are closely intertwined. Research proposals should address them in a comprehensive way, but may choose to focus on one or two aspects. Business and financing models can be tested in different scenarios: in a linear economic system, during the transition phase, and in a fully circular system.

### Who can apply

Applications are submitted by a main applicant and one or more co-applicants. An application is prepared by a consortium, which includes other participants in addition to the applicants. Four categories of participants in a consortium are distinguished:

1. Main applicant
2. Co-applicant(s)
3. Co-funder(s)
4. Cooperation partner(s)
All participants in the consortium must play an active role in formulating the research questions and in the design and implementation of the project.

### Assessment

### Criteria

Proposals will be assessed by the following four criteria:

1. Problem definition and analysis (20%)
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%)
3. Quality of the consortium (30%)
4. Quality of the research (30%)

### Procedure

The application procedure consists of the following steps:

- matchmaking;
- submission of a declaration of intent;
- submission of the proposal;
- admissibility of the proposal;
- pre-advice assessment committee;
- interview selection;
- interview;
- assessment committee meeting;
- decision-making.
