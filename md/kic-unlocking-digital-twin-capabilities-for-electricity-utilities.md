# KIC Unlocking Digital Twin Capabilities for Electricity Utilities

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/kic-unlocking-digital-twin-capabilities-for-electricity-utilities

In order to achieve energy objectives and carbon neutrality, network operators and stakeholders in the electricity system must innovate together. Digital Twin technology offers an important opportunity to tackle these challenges, but the technology is still in its infancy. This research programme, a collaboration between NWO, TenneT, and Energy Innovation NL, therefore stimulates knowledge development in Digital Twin technology.

## Explore

### Purpose

A Digital Twin (DT), or virtual representation of a process or system, can display the state and behaviour of its real-world counterpart in real time. This enables DTs to contribute to more efficient construction and a safer, more economical operation of the electricity system.

However, DT technology for energy systems is still in its infancy. The current state of the technology does not yet meet the needs of grid operators and other stakeholders. There is an urgent need for new knowledge, expertise, technologies, processes, and solutions.

This interdisciplinary research programme aims to increase knowledge and expertise in the field of DT technology. The focus is on the development of important building blocks, such as new methods, algorithms, architectures, and models. The intended result is to deliver working proof-of-concept solutions, which will ultimately be integrated into a demonstrable system.

### Consortium building

In this Call for proposals, consortium formation is encouraged through the use of a synergy workshop. The aim of the synergy workshop is to form a new inter- and transdisciplinary consortium that develops a single application from different areas of knowledge and expertise in order to achieve maximum results and impact.

Applicants submit a pre-proposal specified in one of the domains described in the Call for proposals. One application per domain will be selected. Lead applicants of selected pre-proposals are required to participate in synergy workshop. Co-applicants and collaboration partners of the selected pre-proposals are also invited to participate.

During the workshop, available knowledge on as many aspects of a complex issue as possible will be shared. Facilitators hired by NWO supervise the formation of the consortium and the integration of the pre-proposals into one application to be submitted. Selection for participation in the synergy workshop will take place on the basis of the pre-submissions, see Call for proposals for more information.

### Assessment

### Criteria

A preliminary -proposal should be focused on one of the six domains described in the Call for proposals. These proposals must clearly indicate which part of the issue outlined in the Call for Proposals they aim to address, while also leaving sufficient room for collaboration, adaptation, and synergy with the proposed research activities and insights from other preliminary -proposals. This flexibility is intended to enable the selected proposals to be further developed into a single, integrated application in phase 2. In phase 1, only a concise budget estimate is required, as the final project activities will be defined in phase 2.

### Procedure

Pre-proposals will be reviewed by an assessment committee, which advises the NWO Executive Board. Based on this advice, the Board will decide which proposals proceed to the synergy workshops, with decisions communicated in March 2026. The workshops take place from April to July 2026, after which one joint proposal will be submitted. This proposal will be assessed, and the consortium will be interviewed in September 2026 to clarify the application. Following the interview, the final funding recommendation will be made to the Executive Board. The funding decision will be announced in January 2027.

### Write proposal

Submitting a pre-proposal is mandatory for this Call for proposals. A pre-proposal is a concise application that outlines the main research topics the applicant and co-applicants intend to address, and how these contribute to the objectives and intended impact described in the Call for proposals. Each pre-proposal may address a maximum of one of the six domains outlined in chapter 2 of the Call for proposals. Both main and co-applicants may submit multiple pre-proposals, provided each addresses a different domain.

Main applicants of selected pre-proposals are required to participate in the synergy workshop. A full proposal may only be submitted by one of the main applicants who submitted a pre-proposal in phase 1 and whose proposal has been selected by the NWO Executive Board to proceed to phase 2.

### Mandatory consortium-building activities

In phase 2, synergy workshops will be used to form one large consortium. Participation in these workshops is mandatory for all pre-proposals selected in phase 1.

The aim of the synergy workshop is to establish a new inter- and transdisciplinary consortium that brings together diverse areas of knowledge and expertise to jointly develop a single proposal, with the goal of achieving maximum results and impact. Selected existing or newly formed collaborations that submitted a preproposal will be brought together in the workshop. During the workshop, available knowledge will be shared across as many aspects of the complex issue as possible. Facilitators appointed by NWO will guide the consortium-building process and the integration of the pre-proposals into one final proposal.
