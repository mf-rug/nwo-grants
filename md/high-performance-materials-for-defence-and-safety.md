# High performance materials for Defence and Safety

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/high-performance-materials-for-defence-and-safety

To stimulate the development of high-performance materials for Defence and Safety, seven million euros of research funding is available. This Call for Proposals addresses the major challenges facing the Ministry of Defence in the modernisation of their defence equipment. This Call was developed within the KIA Safety of the KIC Mission.

## Explore

### Purpose

The goal of this programme is to build knowledge on the development, applicability, and optimization of high-performance materials and advanced production technologies for defence and security-critical applications.

For materials, it must be clear how they can be integrated into a defence or security context. The focus is on new materials that can ultimately be used in both civilian and defence applications (dual-use). Materials that are still in an early stage of development and were not explicitly designed for defence and security applications but could be utilized in this context after further development are also within the scope of this Call. However, material developments originating from the defence sector for application in civilian contexts fall outside the scope of this Call for proposals.

### Who can apply

Proposals are submitted by the main applicant and one or more co-applicants. A proposal is written by a consortium in which besides the applicants other participants are involved as well. Four categories of participants can be distinguished in the consortium: 1. Main applicant, 2. Co-applicant(s), 3. Co-funder(s), 4. Cooperation partner(s). The co-funders jointly contribute a net minimum amount of 20% of the total budget for the proposal.

### Consortium building

On 22 May  2025, NWO will facilitate (online) matchmaking activities for this Call for proposals. Your consortium’s participation in these activities is recommended, but not mandatory. In the KIC, the aim of the matchmaking is to bring together and connect researchers from different scientific disciplines and organisations from everyday practice.

### What to apply for

For an application in this Call for proposals, a minimum of €700,000 and a maximum of €2,000,000 of NWO funding can be applied for in. With this, NWO finances maximally 80% of the total project budget; the rest of the funding must be contributed by the compulsory co-funding. NWO never funds less than 50% of the total project budget.

The applicant and co-applicants can include costs for personnel, equipment, investments and knowledge utilisation.

### Assessment

### Criteria

1. Problem definition and analysis (20%),
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%),
3. Quality of the consortium (30%),
4. Quality of the research (30%).

### Procedure

The submission of an initiative is mandatory for this Call for proposals. With an initiative you indicate that you have a potential contribution to or an idea for a (sub)project within this Call for proposals and intend to submit an application for this Call for proposals.

Applications will be provided with feedback by referees. Applicants will have the opportunity to submit a rebuttal to this. Subsequently, the applications are assessed by the assessment committee.

The assessment committee offers its recommendation to the NWO Executive Board for decision-making. In April 2026, the Executive Board determines the final assessment of the applications.
