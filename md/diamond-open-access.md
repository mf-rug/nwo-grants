## Explore

### Purpose

The aim of this programme is to support specifically Diamond Open Access for journals. Funding is for 2-year projects and aimed at existing journals that want to transition to Diamond Open Access. 

Editors, academic societies, foundations and associations that want to “flip” their existing academic journal(s) to a non-profit Diamond Open Access model can apply for funding in this Call for proposals.

### Who can apply

Applicants (main and co-applicants) must have a permanent or temporary contract (that covers at least the project duration) at one of the following organisations:

- universities and universities of applied sciences (UAS) as referred to in Article 1.8 paragraph 1 of the Higher Education and Scientific Research Act and universities listed in the Policy Rules for Universities located in the Kingdom of the Netherlands;
- university medical centres by which is meant academic hospitals as referred to in Article 1.13 paragraph 1 of the Higher Education and Scientific Research Act;- institutes affiliated to the Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences (KNAW) or NWO;
- Netherlands Cancer Institute;
- the Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics in Nijmegen;
- Naturalis Biodiversity Center;
- Advanced Research Centre for NanoLithography (ARCNL);
- Princess Máxima Center.
Personnel with a zero-hour appointment cannot apply.

There are two categories of participants:

1. Main applicant;
2. Co-applicant(s);
Additional conditions for participants:

the main applicant must already be a member of the editorial board of the journal for at least one year, for which the application is submitted;

- a Diamond Open Access application has one main applicant and up to four co-applicants;
- the main applicant submits the application on behalf of the consortium (if applicable);
- the main applicant cannot be involved as a main applicant or co-applicant in other applications for the Call for proposals Diamond Open Access;
- journals must have a publication history of more than three years at the time of submission;
- for journals that seek to relaunch, the publication history of the journal that is left behind must be at least three years;
- (Trade) journals must conduct a form of academic quality control (peer review) in their editorial process.
There are no co-financiers in this Call for proposals.

### What to apply for

Per project, a grant amount of € 50,000 can be applied for. The maximum duration of the proposed project is 24 months.

The grant will be transferred in the form of a lump sum and is intended to cover the costs related to the transition of an existing academic (peer reviewed) subscription-based (trade) journal to a non-profit Diamond Open Access model that ensures the sustainability of the journal after the project end date.

The main applicant can budget costs for personnel and material costs. Appendix 7.1 explains which costs are eligible for funding in this Call for proposals. The main applicant should draft a realistic budget and explain how the budgeted costs are relevant to the project.

### When to apply

- The deadline for **submitting applications** is **15 May 2025,** **before 14:00 CEST**.

### Assessment

## Criteria

The applications submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

| Alignment with the scope of this Call for proposals (30%). |
| --- |
| Feasibility of the proposal (40%): | How realistic is the project plan? |
|  | How relevant are the expertise and skills of the team for the execution of the project?) |
|  | Completeness of identified risk and adequacy of risk management. |
|  | Adequacy and substantiation of the budget. |
| Open Access publishing standards (10%): | To what extent have the applicants embraced and/or applied open access standards to the journal? |
| How relevant and convincing are the plans for sustaining the journal after the end date of the project (20%)? |

### Procedure

The application procedure consists of the following steps:

| Submission of the proposal | deadline 15 May 2025 |
| --- | --- |
| Admissibility of the proposals / eligibility check by NWO | May/June 2025 |
| Rebuttal by applicants | June/July 2025 |
| Assessment committee meeting | August 2025 |
| Decision-making | October 2025 |

An external, independent assessment committee will be assigned for this Call for proposals. The final decision on granting will be done by the NWO board, based on the advice of the assessment committee.
