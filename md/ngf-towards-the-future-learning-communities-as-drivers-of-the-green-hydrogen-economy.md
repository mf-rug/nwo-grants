# NGF: Towards the future Learning communities as drivers of the green hydrogen economy

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/ngf-towards-the-future-learning-communities-as-drivers-of-the-green-hydrogen-economy

This Call for proposals seeks to promote research into regional solutions and/or strategies for creating and shaping a responsive ecosystem of working, learning, and innovation through applied research. These regional ecosystems combine to create a nationwide ecosystem of working, learning, and innovation to drive the hydrogen transition. The objective of this Call for proposals is to help advance knowledge development for the benefit of the entire ecosystem. The available budget for this Call for proposals is a total of €26,800,000. Under this Call for proposals, a maximum of one proposal per above-mentioned region (corresponding to a maximum of seven proposals) will be awarded funding. Participation in the workshops is a mandatory part of the application procedure. The application deadline is Tuesday, October 15, 2024, before 2:00 PM CEST.

## Explore

### Purpose

The key question is ‘How can we forge a responsive ecosystem – in which working, learning, and innovating are tightly intertwined – that integrates knowledge and skills development for hydrogen professionals (both present and future), to reduce time-to-job?’

The success of the hydrogen transition hinges on acquiring a great deal of new knowledge. Therefore, GroenvermogenNL invests not only in human capital but also in R&D, demonstrations, and pilot projects. To implement this new knowledge in the labour market, GroenvermogenNL links knowledge development with regional learning communities where working, learning, and innovation converge. In these learning communities, research findings are implemented and further refined. In this way, GroenvermogenNL achieves skills valorisation by valorising new knowledge from research and innovations through the development of skills among professionals and students. GroenvermogenNL encourages applying consortia to seek collaboration with regional learning communities, to foster skills valorisation. In the regions, these learning communities play a key part in fostering a responsive ecosystem. Thus, establishing and/or expanding learning communities is a crucial component of proposals in this Call for proposals.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The proposals submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantively assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

- Criterion 1: Compliance with the R&D&I framework,
- Criterion 2: Problem definition and problem analysis (20%),
- Criterion 3: Quality of the consortium (40%),
- Criterion 4: Quality of the research plan (40%).

### Procedure

An external, independent assessment committee will be appointed for this Call for proposals, consisting of representatives from science and everyday practice with knowledge of the field. This assessment committee is tasked with assessing the submitted proposals and the associated documentation, both in relation to one another and on their own merits, based on the assessment criteria set out in this Call for proposals (see Section 4.3.1).

Given the expertise within the assessment committee, NWO has opted to use the provision in Article 2.2.4, paragraph 2, of the NWO Grant Rules 2017, to assess the proposals without involving referees.
