# Computing Time on National Computing Facilities

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/computing-time-on-national-computing-facilities-0

Through the Call for Proposals Computing Time on National Computer Facilities 2026, researchers and research groups can gain access to computing time and the associated data services and expertise on the national advanced computer systems Supercomputer Snellius, Data Processing (Grid/Spider), HPC Cloud (via SURF Research Cloud) and support from SURF. In addition, the Dutch allocation of the pre-exascale supercomputer LUMI is also part of this call. Please note that storage is only permitted during the allocation period and cannot be used for long-term storage.

## Explore

### Purpose

Advanced computer systems are used for technical-scientific research where major computational and/or data-intensive problems need to be solved. In the Netherlands, various computer systems are available for national use and managed by SURF: the Snellius supercomputer, Data Processing (Grid/Spider), HPC Cloud (via SURF Research Cloud). Through the call for proposals Computing Time on National Computer Facilities, researchers can apply for access to computing time and associated data services on these national computer facilities. Capacity can also be requested on the Dutch part of the pre-exascale supercomputer LUMI in consultation with SURF should the Snellius system no longer be sufficient.

### Who can apply

Researchers may submit an application if they have a tenured position (and therefore a paid position for an indefinite period) or a tenure track agreement at one of the following research organisations. Only for small computing time requests , the main applicant may also submit an application with a temporary employment contract, provided that

- his/her appointment lasts at least as long as the duration of the project for which an application is submitted; and
- The research organization guarantees the duration and completion of the computing time project.
- Universities and Universities of applied sciences, as referred to in section 1.8(1) of the Higher Education and Scientific Research Act (WHW) and the universities listed in the [Policy Rules for Universities in the Kingdom of the Netherlands](https://www.nwo.nl/sites/nwo/files/media-files/NWO%20Policy%20Rules%20for%20Universities%20in%20the%20Kingdom%20of%20the%20Netherlands.pdf);
- University medical centres, by which is meant academic hospitals as referred to in section 1.13 paragraph 1 of the Higher Education and Scientific Research Act (WHW);
- KNAW and NWO institutes;
- Netherlands Cancer Institute;
- the Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics in Nijmegen;
- NCB Naturalis;
- Advanced Research Centre for NanoLithography (ARCNL).
- Princess Máxima Center.
- TO2 federation: Deltares, Maritime Research Institute Netherlands (Marin), The Netherlands Organisation for Applied Scientific Research (TNO), Netherlands Aerospace Centre (NLR) and Wageningen University (WUR).
- National Institute for Public Health and the Environment (RIVM);
- Royal Netherlands Meteorological Institute (KNMI);
- IHE Delft Institute for Water Education.
Those with zero-hours contracts are excluded from submitting proposals

Additional requirements:
Applicants affiliated to other research organisations as referred to in Article 1.1(4) of the NWO Grant Rules, the organisation must meet cumulative conditions, see Call for proposals section 3.1 for more information.

### What to apply for

#### Within this call, these are applications for large amounts of computing time:

- More than 1,000,000 SBU on the Snellius supercomputer (CPU and/or GPU), whether or not in combination with terabytes of project space and/or Data Archive (offline tape storage).
- More than 500,000 CPU core hours and/or 14,000 GPU hours and/or 100,000 TB hours of storage on the pre-exascale supercomputer LUMI, whether or not in combination with terabytes of online storage and/or offline storage.
- More than 1,000,000 CPU core hours or 10,000 GPU hours of Data Processing (Grid and/or Spider) and/or more than 200 TB of online data storage and/or more than 300 TB of Grid storage - tape (offline tape storage).
- More than 50,000 CPU core hours or 5,000 GPU hours on HPC Cloud (via SURF Research Cloud), whether or not in combination with terabytes of online storage and/or Data Archive (offline tape storage).
The maximum duration of large computing time projects per application is two years. The maximum duration on the pre-exascale supercomputer LUMI is one year.

#### Small amounts of computing time:

These applications are intended primarily as short-term pilots to explore the system or to support small-scale studies, for example during the transition from local to national computer facilities. A researcher may submit a maximum of one computing time application per compute facility per calendar year directly to SURF. Furthermore, it is recommended that the research group's computing needs be combined as much as possible into one large group application.

Small computing time applications can be submitted directly to SURF via the request portal [https://www.surf.nl/en/small-compute-applications-nwo](https://www.surf.nl/en/small-compute-applications-nwo). These requests include:

- Up to 1,000,000 SBU on the Snellius supercomputer (CPU and/or GPU), possibly in combination with a maximum of 10 TB of project space and/or a maximum of 50 TB of data archive.
- Up to 500,000 CPU core hours and/or 14,000 GPU hours for pilot applications on the pre-exascale supercomputer LUMI, possibly in combination with up to 100,000 TB hours of storage.
- Up to 1,000,000 CPU core hours or 10,000 GPU hours of Data Processing (Grid and/or Spider), possibly in combination with up to 200 TB of online data storage and/or up to 300 TB of Grid storage - tape (offline tape storage).
- Up to 50,000 CPU core hours or 5,000 GPU hours on HPC Cloud (via SURF Research Cloud), possibly in combination with up to 2 TB online storage and/or up to 50 TB Data Archive (offline tape storage).
The maximum duration small of computing time projects is one year.

More information on how to apply can be found in the call for proposals under section 3.2.

### When to apply

You can submit your proposal at any time. There are no intermediate deadlines.

### Assessment

### Criteria

Applications will be assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

- Project organization.
- Scientific aspects of the project.
- Need to access computer facilities and technical expertise.
More information about the criteria can be found in the call for proposals

### Procedure

Applications are assessed in order of receipt based on a the application, referee reports and rebuttal. This will lead to an advice concerning the allocation or rejection by the permanent assessment Committee for the Scientific Use of Supercomputers (WGS) to the Board of NWO Domain Science. The WGS meets six times a year to consider the then completed application files. More information about the procedure can be found in the call for proposals.

For more information about the WGS-committee, see[the Computing Time programme page](https://www.nwo.nl/en/researchprogrammes/computing-time-national-computer-facilities)

### Fill in forms

To prepare your application, please go through the following steps:

- download the application form that can be obtained from the online application system ISAAC or from the NWO website (on the website for this programme); you are expressly requested to use the most recent 2025 application form; application forms from previous years are outdated and will not be considered. The application form for 2025 can be found on the funding page for this programme on the NWO website.
- first consult one of SURF's advisers on the technical aspects of the application at [servicedesk@surf.nl](mailto:servicedesk@surf.nl).
- complete the application form.
- select and complete the corresponding technical annexes of the application form; remove all irrelevant technical annexes of computer facilities that will not be used.
- save the form as a pdf file and submit it with any required annexe(s) in ISAAC.
- complete the information requested online in ISAAC.

#### **Mandatory attachment:**

- If the application is a follow-up application, also submit a report from the previous project. Reports from previous projects are taken to the evaluation committee as additional information in the evaluation of the follow -up application. The report form can be found on the funding page for this program on the NWO website.
The attachment should be prepared in accordance with the template provided by NWO. The attachment should be uploaded separately from the application in ISAAC. All attachments should be submitted as a pdf file (without security). Attachments other than those listed above are not permitted.

It is mandatory to write your application in English.

More information on the conditions for applicants can be found in section 3.3 of the call for proposals.
