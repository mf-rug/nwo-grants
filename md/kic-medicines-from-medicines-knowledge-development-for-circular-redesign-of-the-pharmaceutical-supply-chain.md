# KIC Medicines from medicines - knowledge development for circular redesign of the pharmaceutical supply chain

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/kic-medicines-from-medicines-knowledge-development-for-circular-redesign-of-the-pharmaceutical-supply-chain

This call for proposals invites researchers from the humanities, natural sciences, and social sciences to collaborate with industry and societal organizations in forming an interdisciplinary and transdisciplinary consortium aimed at developing knowledge on circular adaptation of the pharmaceutical supply chain, anabling the acceptance of returned and/or discarded medications for reintegration.

## Explore

### Purpose

This call for proposals focuses on inter- and transdisciplinary knowledge development that contributes to redesigning the pharmaceutical supply chain. The emphasis is on developing business cases and use cases that involve the entire chain. The intended societal impact is a change among all key stakeholders to achieve a circular pharmaceutical supply chain that meets the same high standards for quality, efficacy, and safety as the current linear chain.

### Who can apply

Proposals are submitted by the main applicant and one or more co-applicants. Researchers may submit an application if they have a tenured position (and therefore a paid position for an indefinite period*) or a tenure track agreement at a research organisation. A proposal is written by a consortium in which besides the applicants two or more co-funders, and potentially one or more cooperation partners, are involved as well. All consortiumpartners should play an active role in the formulation of the research questions and in the design and implementation of the project.

For more information: see section 3.1 in the Call for Proposals.

### Consortium building

This Call for proposals stimulates the formation of consortiums by means of collaborative workshops. Attending the collaborative workshops is mandatory for main applicants.

The objective of the collaborative workshops is to bring together potential applicants and their ideas, to encourage collaboration within broad, innovative consortia, and to limit the number of proposals.

### What to apply for

For an application in this Call for proposals, a minimum of €750.000 and aa maximum of €2.300.000 of NWO funding can be applied for. With this, NWO finances maximally 80% of the total project budget; the rest of the funding must be contributed by the compulsory co-funding (see Section 3.5.6). NWO never funds less than 50% of the total project budget.

The maximum duration of the proposed project is 6 years.

### When to apply

- The dates of the collaborative workshops are **23 September 2025 and 7 October 2025**.
- The deadline for submission of full applications is **10 February , 2026, by 14:00:00 CET**.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Problem definition and analysis (20%)
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%)
3. Quality of the consortium (30%)
4. Quality of the research (30%)

### Procedure

For this Call for proposals, attending the collaborative workshops by main applicant (or substitute) is mandatory.

Full applications will be provided with peer review by referees. Applicants will be given the opportunity to submit a rebuttal to this. The applications will then be assessed by the assessment committee. The assessment committee will present its advice to the NWO Executive Board for decision-making. In September 2026, the NWO Executive Board will adopt the final assessment of the applications.

### Fill in forms

In addition to filling in the application form when submitting the complete application, we also ask you to include a signed statement from the co-financiers. If applicable, a confirmation of contribution to investments (see section 7.1 in the call for proposals) and a guarantee for continuity in the project management (see section 3.1 in the call for proposals) must also be sent.

At the time of submission, the enclosed letters of support must have pledged the full required co-financing in accordance with the conditions described in sections 3.1.2 and 3.5.6 in the call for proposals.

The appendix "confirmation of contribution to investments" is mandatory if the application requests funding for investments of € 150,000 or more (see also section 7.1 in the call for proposals).

Other types of annexes shall are not allowed at the stage of submission of complete applications. Attachments must be uploaded separately from the application in ISAAC. All attachments, with the exception of the budget form, must be uploaded as a pdf file. The budget form can be uploaded as an Excel file in ISAAC.
