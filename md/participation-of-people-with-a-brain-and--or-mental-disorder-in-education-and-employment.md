# Participation of people with a  brain and/or mental disorder in education and employment

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/participation-of-people-with-a-brain-and/or-mental-disorder-in-education-and-employment

This Call for proposals calls on knowledge institutions and societal stakeholders to jointly develop scientific and practice-oriented research proposals aimed at aimed at advancing participation for people with brain and/or mental disorders in education and (un)paid work, specifically on the transition between these domains. The consortia are interdisciplinary and knowledge chain-wide composed and work in a disorder-transcending and integrated manner.

## Explore

### Purpose

This programme focuses on 1) gaining insight into factors that influence participation in education and work for people with brain and/or mental disorders – including people with a lower socio-economic position (SEP) or migration background; 2) developing effective support methods and action perspectives for this group of people; and 3) increasing awareness and cooperation between stakeholders to ultimately contribute to a positive image (reducing taboo and combating stigmatisation).

### Who can apply

An application must be submitted by a consortium. A consortium should consist of at least one main applicant, co-applicant and collaboration partner, of which at least one collaboration partner must come from the healthcare sector. 
All participants should play an active role in formulating the research questions and in the design and implementation of the project.
The conditions per participant are further explained in the Call for proposals.

### Consortium building

This Call for proposals entails three phases: the submission of a research idea and participation in two collaborative workshops (phase I), submitting the full application submission (phase II), and the submission of the overarching application submission (phase III). Following the submission of the research idea, two consecutive collaborative workshops will be organised by NWO. The person who submits the research idea will automatically be registered for the collaborative workshops.

The workshops will take place Thursday 13 February and Tuesday 4 March 2025. Participation in the workshops is mandatory for the main applicant who wants to submit an application in phase II. Should this person be unable to attend, a replacement must be arranged, working within the same organisation.

All parties involved in a submitted research idea can apply for the workshops. In addition, the workshops are open to other interested parties. Parties not yet affiliated with a research idea before the workshop can view the submitted ideas on the NWO website. This allows them to determine whether affiliation with a research idea might be useful. Registration for the workshops is possible via the form on the programme page on the NWO website. Deadline for registration for the workshops is Thursday 6 February2025 before 14:00:00. Civil society organisations are specifically invited to submit a research idea and/or participate in the workshops.

### What to apply for

The available budget for this Call for proposals is €4,400,000. This budget will be distributed as follows: 4x €800,000 for phase II proposals and €1,200,000 for the phase II overarching proposal. The intended duration of all projects together (phase II and phase III) is 5 years, with phase II covering the first 4 years and phase III the last 3 years. This means that phase II and phase III will overlap for two years, allowing for interaction between phases II and III.

The applicant and co-applicants can include costs for personnel, equipment, investments and knowledge utilisation. The available budget modules (including maximum amounts) are listed in the Call for proposals. Apply only for what is essential to carry out the project. The rates and an explanation of these budget modules are given in Annex 7.1 of the Call for proposals.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Problem posed and problem analysis, contribution to solution,
2. Expected impact and route to impact,
3. Quality of the consortium,
4. Quality of the research.
Criteria 1 and 2 each carry a weighting of 20% in the overall assessment, and criteria 3 and 4 each carry a weighting of 30%.

### Procedure

The deadline for submitting proposals in phase II is Thursday 12 June 2025, before 14:00:00 CEST. For the assessment of the proposals, the committee will make use of pre-advices and interviews will be conducted. A final decision on funding by the NWO Executive Board is expected in November 2025.

The deadline for submitting the overarching proposal is Tuesday 27 April 2027, before 14:00:00 CEST. For the assessment of the proposal, the committee will make use of pre-advices and an opportunity for response. A final decision on funding by the NWO Executive Board is expected in June 2027.
