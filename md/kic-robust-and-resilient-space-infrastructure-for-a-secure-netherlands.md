# KIC Robust and resilient space infrastructure for a secure Netherlands

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/kic-robust-and-resilient-space-infrastructure-for-a-secure-netherlands

Space infrastructure is of vital importance, and it is therefore crucial to be well prepared and to protect this infrastructure through innovative technologies, supported by solid legal, ethical, and strategic security frameworks. This call invites knowledge institutions, industry, and societal partners to form a consortium for research to strengthening a robust and resilient space infrastructure for a secure Netherlands.

### Purpose

Space infrastructure is essential for communication, remote sensing, positioning, and time information, but it is also vulnerable to sabotage, espionage, and collisions with space debris from decommissioned or non-functioning satellites, as well as to natural disruptions. To strengthen the resilience of space infrastructure, it is important that the Dutch research community, industry, and government are well prepared and protect this infrastructure through innovative technologies, based on solid legal, ethical, and strategic security frameworks.

This Call for proposals focuses on strengthening a robust and resilient space infrastructure for a secure Netherlands. This Call for proposals specifically invites research proposals addressing the following themes:

- Critical technologies to enhance the resilience and protection of space infrastructure (consortia are required to address this aspect in their proposal).
- Associated legal, ethical, and strategic security frameworks (consortia must explicitly describe in their proposal how these frameworks are integrated into the design, implementation, and application of their research).

### Who can apply

Proposals are submitted by the main applicant and one or more co-applicants. A proposal is written by a consortium in which besides the applicants other participants are involved as well.

Four categories of participants can be distinguished in the consortium:

1. Main applicant
2. Co-applicant(s)
3. Co-funder(s)
4. Cooperation partner(s)
A consortium must consist of at least one main applicant, one co-applicant, and two co-funders, possibly supplemented by one or more cooperation partners. The conditions for each participant are explained in more detail in the following sections. All consortium partners must play an active role in formulating the research questions and in the design and implementation of the project.

### Consortium building

At 4th of February 2026, NWO will organize a matchmaking event for this Call for Proposals. Participation in this event is recommended but not mandatory.

### What to apply for

For a proposal submitted in this Call for proposals, applicants can request a minimum of €1.000,000 and a maximum of €2,000,000 in NWO funding. NWO will fund up to 80% of the total project budget; the remaining portion must be covered through mandatory co-funding (see section 3.5.6). NWO will never fund less than 50% of the total project budget. Applicants and co-applicants may include costs for personnel, materials, investments, knowledge utilization, and project management.

### When to apply

- The deadline for submitting proposals is **2 April 2026**, before 14:00:00 CEST.
- The deadline for submitting a request for the assessment of foreign organizations (see section 7.1.1) is **19 March 2026**, before 14:00:00 CEST.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The pre-proposals submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Problem definition and analysis (20%)
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%)
3. Quality of the consortium (30%)
4. Quality of the research (30%)

### Procedure

The application procedure consists of the following steps:

- submission research idea (not obligatory)
- matchmaking;
- submission of the proposal;
- admissibility of the proposal;
- peer review by referees;
- rebuttal;
- pre-advice assessment committee;
- interview selection;
- interview;
- assessment committee meeting;
- decision-making.
An external, independent assessment committee will be assigned for this Call for proposals, consisting of representatives from the research field and society with knowledge of (partial aspects of) the topic.

### Write proposal

These conditions are:

- the main applicant and co-applicant(s) meet the conditions stated in Section 3.1 and Section 7.1.1;
- the applying consortium contains two or more co-funders;
- the application complies with the DORA guidelines as described in Section 4.1;
- the application is submitted via the main applicant’s ISAAC account;
- the application is received before the deadline;
- the application is written in English;
- the proposed project has a duration of at most 6 years;
- all of the required annexes and forms are, after a possible request to make additions or changes, have been completed and submitted completely and according to the instructions and in accordance with the terms of this Call for proposals.
- the budget in the application has been prepared according to the terms of this Call for proposals (using the format made available which includes the most recent rates);
- the required co-financing has been correctly and fully committed through co-financing statements.

### Fill in forms

Compulsory annexes:

- budget form;
- declarations co-funding from co-funders (see Section 3.1.2 and 3.5.6);
- form ‘Statement and signature’;
- letter guaranteeing the continuity of project supervision (if applicable, see Section 3.1 of the Call for Proposals);
- confirmation of own contribution to investments (only if applicable, see section 7.1 of the Call for Proposals);
- Knowledge security statement.
The proposal and the appendix(es) must be drawn up in accordance with the template provided by NWO. Annexes must be uploaded in ISAAC separately from the application. All of the annexes, except for the budget, must be submitted as PDF files (without encryption). The budget must be submitted in ISAAC as an Excel file.

When submitting the proposal, the attached declarations co-funding should guarantee the full required amount of co-funding in line with the conditions of Section 3.5.6.

Any annexes other than those above-mentioned are not permitted.
