# KIC Future Plastics: Development of safe plastics for a sustainable future

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/kic-future-plastics-development-of-safe-plastics-for-a-sustainable-future

In 2050, the Netherlands aims to be fully circular. This includes, among other things, a fully circular plastic chain. To enable the transition to a circular and environmentally friendly plastic chain, new plastics need to be developed. This Call focuses on the development and transition to these plastics.

## Explore

### Purpose

This programme aims to build knowledge about a new generation of bio-based or CO2-based plastics that are non-persistent and can no longer lead to harmful leakage. The definition of plastics is not limited to plastics but also includes, among other things, coatings, composites, paint, and rubbers. Additionally, knowledge is needed about the pathways that can be taken to introduce these plastics into society, and this knowledge should be incorporated into the design of new materials. Interdisciplinary collaboration is of great importance in this regard.

### Who can apply

Proposals are submitted by the main applicant and one or more co-applicants. A proposal is written by a consortium in which besides the applicants other participants are involved as well.

The main and co-applicant(s) should conduct the proposed research as a consortium that always includes, beside themselves, two or more co-funders, and potentially one or more cooperation partners. All consortiumpartners should play an active role in the formulation of the research questions and in the design and implementation of the project.

### Consortium building

In the period prior to the deadline for submitting pre-proposals NWO will facilitate matchmaking activities for this Call for proposals. For this call, matchmaking will take place on May 13. Your consortium’s participation in these activities is recommended, but not mandatory.

### What to apply for

For an application in this Call for proposals, a minimum of €750,000 and a maximum of €2,000,000 of NWO funding can be applied for in. With this, NWO finances maximally 70% of the total project budget; the rest of the funding must be contributed by the compulsory co-funding. NWO never funds less than 50% of the total project budget.

The applicant and co-applicants can include costs for personnel, equipment, investments, knowledge utilization and project management. The available budget modules (including the maximum amounts) are listed below. Apply only for funding that is vital to realise the project.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Problem definition and analysis (20%)
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%)
3. Quality of the consortium (30%)
4. Quality of the research (30%)

### Procedure

The application procedure consists of the following steps:

- information session;
- matchmaking;
- submission of a statement of intent;
- submission of the proposal;
- admissibility of the proposal;
- peer review by referees;
- rebuttal;
- pre-advice assessment committee;
- interview selection;
- interview;
- assessment committee meeting;
- decision-making.

- ### [Research data management](/en/research-data-management)

### Write proposals

NWO will assess your application on the following conditions:

- the main applicant and co-applicant(s) meet the conditions;
- the main applicant has submitted a statement of intent in ISAAC before the set deadline;
- the applying consortium contains two or more cofunders;
- the application complies with the DORA guidelines as described in the Call for proposals;
- the required co-funding has been, after any request for addition or change, correctly and fully committed through declarations co-funding;
- the application is submitted via the main applicant’s ISAAC account;
- the application is received before the deadline;
- the application is written in English;
- the application budget of the proposal is drawn up in accordance with the conditions for this Call for proposals;
- the proposed project has a duration of at most 6 years;
- all of the required annexes are, after a possible request to make additions or changes, have been completed and submitted completely and according to the instructions and in accordance with the terms of this Call for proposals.
