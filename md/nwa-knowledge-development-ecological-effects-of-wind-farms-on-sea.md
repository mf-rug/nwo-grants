# NWA Knowledge development ecological effects of wind farms on sea

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/nwa-knowledge-development-ecological-effects-of-wind-farms-on-sea

This Call for proposals invites knowledge institutions and societal parties to apply for the Sandpit. During the Sandpit, a scientific and practice‐oriented research proposal will be developed together, focusing on the theme of ‘Knowledge development of the ecological effect of offshort wind farms. A consortium is then formed.

## Explore

### Purpose

This NWA programme focuses on effects of wind farms in the North Sea on ecological systems above and below water. With the Wozep and MONS programmes, the government is looking at these effects (for information on Wozep and MONS). Within this NWA programme, innovative and deeper understanding of essential and steering processes is being sought. Existing research with models will be transcended by further exploring assumptions made so far and adding new perspectives. This programme is complementary to the pathways ongoing within the MONS and Wozep multi‐year programmes. Insights from the research can form the basis for better‐informed, more integrated and future‐proof policy and decision‐making.

### Who can apply

Proposals are to be submitted by the main applicant on behalf of the consortium. The main applicant has participated in the Sandpit (see Section 2.4.1, 3.3.1 and 4.2.2).

There are four categories of participants within a consortium:

1. Main applicant;
2. Co-applicant(s);
3. Cooperation partner(s);
4. Co-funder(s) (optional).
A consortium should consist of at least a main applicant, a co-applicant and cooperation partner.

### Consortium building

In this Call for proposals, consortium formation is encouraged through the use of a Sandpit. The aim of a Sandpit is to establish cooperation in new collaborations and joint research proposals. Sandpits focus on collaboration in a single consortium consisting of the selected parties. During a Sandpit, the selected participants work jointly on site to develop a research project, under the guidance of external facilitators and content supporters. For participation in a Sandpit, selection takes place through an Expression of Interest, see the Call for proposals.

### What to apply for

A maximum grant amount of €5.200.000,‐ can be applied for. The applicant and co‐applicants may/can include costs for personnel, equipment, investments and knowledge utilisation among other things. Co-funding is not compulsory within this Call for proposals.

### Assessment

### Criteria

Assessment criteria Expression of Interest:

1. Academic/professional attributes (40%),
2. Personal competences and motivation (60%).
Assessment criteria proposal:

1. Problem definition and analysis (20%),
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%),
3. Quality of the consortium (30%),
4. Quality of the research (30%).

### Procedure

The application procedure consists of the following steps:

- Submission of the Expression of Interest;
- Admissibility of the Expression of Interest;
- Selection of participants for Sandpit;
- Decision‐making;
- Participation in Sandpit;
- Submission of the proposal;
- Admissibility of the proposal;
- Pre-advicing assessment committee;
- Assessment committee meeting including interview;
- Decision‐making.
