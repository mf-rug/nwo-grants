# Reducing drug harm: towards effective societal interventions

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/reducing-drug-harm-towards-effective-societal-interventions

This call invites researchers and societal organisations to form a consortium that researches societal innovations aimed at reducing drug(-related) harm. The consortium will investigate the mechanisms through which drug(-related) harm arises. It will also develop and improve policy interventions to reduce drug-related harm.

## Explore

### Purpose

Drug use, production and trafficking affect health, the economy, safety and the living environment, and these domains continuously influence one another. The Netherlands has a high availability of a wide array of drugs, which are rapidly distributed, driven by its strong logistical hubs, advanced digital infrastructure and a stable level of consumer demand. Public attitudes towards drug use and drug(-related) harm are varying and highly malleable, depending on age, personal experiences with drugs, (marketing) trends, news consumption and (online) social influence.

Reducing drug(-related) harm and developing integrated policies is complex. Due to this, it remains unclear which policy instruments and interventions are effective in reducing the harms caused by drugs. This Call aims to contribute to the development of knowledge about the (societal) mechanisms through which drug(-related) harm arises and how it can be reduced in a sustainable way. The knowledge generated through this Call must be able to adapt to temporal and societal fluctuations.

### Who can apply

The Call for Proposals invites knowledge institutions, societal organisations and citizens to jointly develop research proposals focused on the theme of drug(-related) harm. Pre-proposals and applications are submitted by a main applicant. The main applicant (or their replacement) must have participated in all synergy workshops of the second phase.

Within the consortium, four categories of participants are distinguished:

1. Main applicant
2. Co-applicant(s)
3. Cooperation partner(s)
4. Co-funder(s) (not mandatory)
The consortium must consist of at least a main applicant, a co-applicant and a cooperation partner.

Researchers may act as main applicants. Furthermore, two types of co-applicants are possible: co-applicants affiliated with research organisations and co-applicants affiliated with societal organisations.

The requirements are explained in more detail in the Call for Proposals.

### Consortium building

To increase the potential impact of the research, collaboration between researchers, with researchers from other disciplines, and with partners from society – such as companies and societal organisations – is required. In this Call for Proposals, the Dutch Research Council (NWO) supports the formation of a consortium by organising synergy workshops. These synergy workshops will take place in November 2026, January 2027 and February 2027, under the guidance of external, independent facilitators appointed by NWO.

Participation in all synergy workshops in phase 2 is mandatory for the main applicants of the selected pre-proposals if they wish to act as main applicant of the full proposal. Additional participants, including relevant stakeholders, may be invited if the selected applicants decide to do so.

If during or after the synergy workshops it appears that essential expertise or consortium partners are still needed, cooperation partners or co-funders may be added to the consortium, provided this is well substantiated in the full application.

### What to apply for

The Call for Proposals follows a two-phase structure. In the first phase, pre-proposals can be submitted for two themes. In the second phase, a single consortium is formed through externally facilitated synergy workshops.

Within this Call for Proposals, a maximum of four pre-proposals will be selected in phase 1. This corresponds to a maximum planned budget of €1,067,737.75 per pre-proposal.

The funding limit for the joint application in phase 2 amounts to a total of €4,270,951. The maximum duration of the proposed project is five years. The applicant and co-applicants may budget costs for personnel, material, investments and knowledge utilisation.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The assessment is based on substantive evaluation criteria and starts once an application has been taken into consideration. The application is assessed according to the following criteria:

Phase 1 – Pre-proposal:

- Problem definition and analysis (40%)
- Envisaged impact and route to impact (40%)
- Quality of the potential consortium partners (20%)
Phase 2 – Full proposal:

- Problem definition and analysis (20%)
- Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%)
- Quality of the consortium (30%)
- Quality of the research (30%)

### Mandatory consortium-building activities

In phase 2, the development and submission of a single application will take place by a consortium consisting of the selected main applicants and other involved parties from the selected pre-proposals. The content of the pre-proposals serves as the starting point and is integrated into a single application using the Impact Plan approach as the guiding framework.

The structure of the collaboration and the integration of the pre-proposals are expanded upon through synergy workshops. These consist of a series of workshops organised in the period from November 2026 up to and including February 2027, under the supervision of an external, independent facilitator appointed by NWO. Participation in all synergy workshops in phase 2 is mandatory for the main applicants of the selected pre-proposals if they wish to act as main applicant of the full proposal. Additional participants, including stakeholders, may be invited if the selected applicants decide to do so.

Additional information on consortium formation can be found in the Call for Proposals.
