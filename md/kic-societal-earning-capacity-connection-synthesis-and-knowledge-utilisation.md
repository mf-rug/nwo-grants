# KIC Societal Earning Capacity: Connection, Synthesis and Knowledge Utilisation

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/kic-societal-earning-capacity-connection-synthesis-and-knowledge-utilisation

This top-up call calls for consortiums from the previous KIC Mission calls to request extra budget intended for connection, synthesis and knowledge utilisation from the knowledge that is gained during their research. This call hopes to contribute to bring together, integrate and make knowledge available in other contexts and has as its purpose to accelerate mission-driven innovation and to create Societal Earning Capacity.

## Explore

### Purpose

The KIC Societal Earning Capacity Top-up Call: Connection, Synthesis and Knowledge Utilisation aims to contribute to bring together, integrate and make knowledge available gained from current and/or completed KIC projects. Its purpose is to accelerate mission driven innovation and to create Societal Earning Capacity. This gained knowledge is not always fully integrated outside certain consortiums and ecosystems. Knowledge about mission-driven innovation is also still often scattered.

This call is only open for current or completed projects from the following programmes:

- Mission-Driven Innovation Systems in a Regional Context KIC (2020);
- Collaboration in Mission-Driven Innovation KIC (2021);
- Realisation, Acceleration and Scaling-up of Mission-Driven Innovation KIC (2022)

### Who can apply

The project leads of a current or a completed project funded under the calls below are encouraged to submit an application. The ‘project lead’ is the person who took on the role of ‘project lead’ at the time when a proposal was submitted in the NWO administration system ISAAC for the current project.

Nine projects which were funded under the following original three calls for proposals are invited to apply for this top-up call:

- Mission-Driven Innovation Systems in a Regional Context KIC (2020);
- Collaboration in Mission-Driven Innovation KIC (2021);
- Realisation, Acceleration and Scaling-up of Mission-Driven Innovation KIC (2022).
The project lead of a current project funded under one of the three above-mentioned calls will take the role of main applicant when submitting a proposal. 

**Additional conditions:**
Co-applicants are permitted. For the purposes of this top-up call, a consortium consists of a main applicant, one or more co-applicants and a partnering organisation. Consortiums who wish to submit a proposal to this top-up call may also collaborate and jointly request a higher grant.

### Consortium building

NWO facilitates physical workshops for this call during the Societal Earning Capacity programme days in October 2024, 2025 and 2026. The aim of the workshops is to bring together the consortiums of current projects to foster an exchange of ideas about their current findings.

### What to apply for

Proposals submitted to this call can apply for a minimum amount in NWO funding of € 55,555 twice or a maximum of € 111,111 once. Every current project funded under an already allocated Societal Earning Capacity call may request up to a maximum of € 111,111; this may be applied for all at once or split between two different proposals valued at € 55,555. If two or more projects make a joint top-up application together, the allowable grant for a single project will be multiplied by the number of projects making the joint application. For more information see paragraph 3.2 ‘What can be applied for’ and annex 7.1 of the call text. 

The proposed project has a duration of at most 1 year.

The grant will not cover activities that are already planned in the scope of allocated projects. Instead, the activities in this call for proposals must be distinct, newly initiated efforts.

### When to apply

This Call for proposals has a specific lead time during which applications can be submitted continuously. During the term, three deadlines/collection times have been determined when applications will be assessed. Applications can be submitted continuously until January 21, 2027, before 2:00:00 PM CET.

**The pick-up times are:**

- January 23, 2025, before 2:00:00 PM CET
- January 22, 2026, before 2:00:00 PM CET
- January 21, 2027, before 2:00 PM CET (also closing date of this Call for proposals)

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following (equally weighted) criteria:

1. Consortium and collaboration (33%)
2. Impact (33%)
3. Plan (33%)
For more information see paragraph 4.3 of the call text.
