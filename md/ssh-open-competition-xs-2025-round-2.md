# SSH Open Competition XS 2025 - round 2

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/ssh-open-competition-xs-2025-round-2

The NWO Domain Social Sciences and Humanities (SSH) has three different funding programmes in the Open Competition. These grants are for ideas that hold great promise (XS) and for large-scale grants (M, L). The SSH-XS grants are available for projects with a maximum budget of 50,000 euro to enable proposals for curiosity-driven, innovative research in the research fields covered by the NWO Domain SSH. The Call for proposals of 2025 has 3 rounds. This information concerns the second round.

## Characteristics

**Status:** Closed  
**Closing date full application:** 13 May 2025 14:00 hrs.  
**Finance type:** Curiosity Driven Research  
**Budget:** €4,000,000  
**Research programme:** XS Open Competition SSH  

## Explore

### Purpose

The SSH Open Competition XS Call for proposals is specifically intended to encourage curiosity-driven and innovative research that involves the relatively rapid exploration of a promising idea.

### The characteristics of SSH-XS research are:

- the proposed research is innovative;
- it is not certain in advance whether the intended objectives will be achieved;
- the result of each project contributes to the advancement of existing science and new scientific insights.

### Who can apply

Researchers with a promising idea who obtained their PhD between 5 and 10 years ago may submit an application if they have a paid appointment for at least the duration of the application process and the duration of the proposed project.

The submission deadline for the round is the reference date of the graduation date. The graduation date on the PhD diploma (degree) is leading.

For round 2, the applicant must have obtained their PhD between 9 May 2015 and 8 May 2020.

### Extra conditions:

- an SSH-XS application has a single applicant and no co-applicants;
- an applicant may submit a maximum of one application in the SSH-XS, call round 2025;
- an applicant - when using the extension scheme - may not submit an application for multiple types of funding instruments within the NWO Open Competition programme during the same calendar year (2025);
- applicants may not be part of an XS project for which the end date has not yet passed at the time of submission.
Please note: the extension scheme applies to the SSH Open Competition XS in 2025. For more information and the online application form for extension see: [https://www.nwo.nl/en/extension-clause](https://www.nwo.nl/en/extension-clause).

### What to apply for

For an application in this Call for proposals a maximum of 50,000 euro can be applied for. The minimum duration of the proposed project is 6 months and the maximum duration is 12 months.

Applicants are required to draw up a realistic budget and explain why the spending choices are relevant to the project. This explanation must cover all of the personnel requested. 

Please only apply for funding that is vital to realise the project. A more detailed explanation of the budget modules can be found in the annexe to this Call for proposals (Section 7.1).

### When to apply

For 2025, 3 deadlines for submitting applications are specified:

- Round 1: 6 March 2025, before 14:00:00 CET (closed)
- Round 2: 8 May 2025, before 14:00:00 CEST
- Round 3: 18 September 2025, before 14:00:00 CEST
When you submit your application in ISAAC, you will also need to enter some details online. Therefore, please start submitting your application at least one day before the deadline of the round. Applications that are submitted after the deadline will not be taken into consideration.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The assessors who rank the applications will focus on the following criteria. These criteria are not weighted. At the discretion of an assessor, both of these aspects may be used for the purposes of ranking. However, that ranking must be accompanied by a short written explanation to substantiate the given rank based on the assessment criteria listed below.

##### - Innovative aspect

Is the proposed research innovative and groundbreaking? Does the proposed research contribute to existing and new scientific insights? Does the research proposal potentially have a scientific impact?

##### - Feasibility of the research plan

The objective and work plan must be clearly described. Does the methodology seem suitable to achieve the indicated project goal? Is the planning of the research plan appropriate and are the activities included in the planning with sufficient clarity?

Applications must be drawn up with a wide scientific audience in mind so that assessors from the relevant panel will be able to properly evaluate the research plan and the assessment criteria described above. The SSH disciplines and associated (sub)disciplines are listed on the NWO website: [https://www.nwo.nl/en/overview-panels-domain-social-sciences-and-humani…](https://www.nwo.nl/en/overview-panels-domain-social-sciences-and-humanities). The assessors may award a lower ranking to any applications that are drawn up in a way that is not accessible to those in other disciplines.

### Procedure

The applicant determines by which panel the application will be processed by means of the SSH main discipline specified in the information form (see Section 4.2.3 of the Call for proposals).

Applications processed per panel are divided into 2 groups, so that each panel consists of Group A and Group B. In principle, the classification takes place at the time of submission to NWO, taking into account the personal interests declared by the applicants.

The assessors and applicants associated with a particular package of proposals do not meet. NWO will decide the final rankings of the applications, based on the rankings submitted by the assessors. Per round, 6 final rankings will be established – per panel one for Group A and one for Group B. There will be no opportunity to dispute the rankings.

The SSH Domain Board will base its decision about awarding or rejecting the applications on the results of the 6 final rankings, the assessment reports and the available funding.

## Prepare

Applications must be submitted through the Isaac application and reporting system. If you do not have an Isaac account yet, then please create one at least one workday in advance. More information about writing a proposal and which forms you need to use can be found in Isaac. Please download and use the forms in Isaac. Below you will find examples of the required forms.

### Write proposal

The research idea needs to be drawn up anonymously, which means the application form meets the following conditions:

- applicants should not include their own name nor the name of any individuals in their research group;
- applicants should not include details of any affiliations with institutes or knowledge institutions;
- applicants should not include references to any publications that they have authored or co-authored;
- applicants should not include the names of any (potential) partners.
Applications must be drawn up with a wide scientific audience in mind so that assessors from the relevant panel will be able to properly evaluate the research plan and the assessment criteria.
