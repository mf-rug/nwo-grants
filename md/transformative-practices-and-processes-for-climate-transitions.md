# Transformative practices and processes for climate transitions

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/transformative-practices-and-processes-for-climate-transitions

This grant invites employees of societal organisations and knowledge institutions to develop, test or scale up transformative practices and processes for just climate transitions. Individuals or consortia can apply for up to €50,000 for projects that will advance systemic change by facilitating transformative, transdisciplinary collaborations.

### Purpose

This call for proposals supports projects that validate, further develop or make methods and processes more widely accessible in order to accelerate systemic and just climate transitions. The focus is on practices that promote transdisciplinary collaboration between science and practice, support transformative change, integrate sustainability principles and pursue climate justice. It is important that the practice forms the starting point and that methods are scalable and transferable. Initiatives that address less -explored aspects of transitions – such as transition pain, grief, chaos or imagination – are also very welcome. The call is part of KIN's Science in Transition programme and offers space, time and resources to strengthen and embed experimental forms of collaboration in practice. KIN aims to stimulate a new scientific practice in which knowledge production contributes to just system change and societal impact. Projects contribute to strengthening a transition mindset and ensure that valuable methods become more widely applicable for a climate-neutral and resilient society.

### Assessment

### Criteria

Proposals are assessed on two aspects:

1. Relation to the call and quality
2. easibility of the activity plan
Please note, for this call for proposals, the main applicants are also the assessors. For the full assessment aspects, please read chapter 4.2 of the call for proposals.

### Procedure

This call for proposals for proposals encourages the acceleration of climate transitions through open knowledge sharing and peer review. Main applicants assess each other's proposals in two groups, with consideration for conflict of interests and diversity. The assessment is based solely on the proposal, which must be widely accessible and clearly written. The chosen methodology utilises the expertise of applicants and promotes diverse feedback. Each assessor ranks the proposals they have received for assessment based on the assessment aspects (see section 4.2). The KIN bureau draws up a final ranking based on the priorities set by each group. The steering group reviews the procedure and decides on the allocation of grants. There is no possibility of appeal. If the number of proposals is too low or if conflicts of interest impede peer review, the assessment will be carried out by an independent committee.

### Write proposal

The grant “Transformative practices and processes for climate transitions” focuses on validating, further developing and expanding the accessibility of experimental collaboration methods, facilitating practices, processes, in-between spaces and tools that:

- promote transdisciplinary collaboration between practice and science,
- contribute to system change,
- and strengthen the transition mindset among researchers and practitioners by empowering them and providing them with tools.
Clearly describe what you will do, why, and how the results will be shared.

- Demonstrate transformative ambition: KIN is looking for methods and processes that truly change things, not just improve them.
- Co-creation & knowledge development: Show how different types of knowledge come together in the methods and processes.
- Be creative and concrete: Design-oriented, activist or creative practices with clear impact are welcome.
- Write accessibly: The main applicants are also the assessors and come from academia and practice with different areas of expertise.
