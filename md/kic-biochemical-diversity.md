### Purpose

The increasing pressure on natural resources due to the rapidly growing global population necessitates fundamental changes to production processes to ensure the continued viability of life on our planet. This Call for proposals focuses on increasing the sustainability of production processes by developing and refining key enabling technologies. This will involve expanding and improving sustainable production processes by exploring the vast range of biochemical substances and biochemical pathways in nature and making them accessible for production processes. The many biochemical substances and processes in nature offer opportunities to achieve this objective. However, the existing technological capabilities are insufficient to effectively explore, analyse and utilise that diversity. This Call for proposals therefore focuses not just on knowledge development, but first and foremost on developing and refining technologies that facilitate the exploration, analysis and application of biochemical diversity.

### Who can apply

This Call for proposals is intended to promote collaboration between different scientific fields, social partners and business. As such, proposals are invited from researchers from both academic institutes and universities of applied sciences. Close collaboration with private and public organisations is essential.

Proposals are submitted by the main applicant and one or more co-applicants. Researchers may submit an application if they have a tenured position (and therefore a paid position for an indefinite period*) or a tenure track agreement. A proposal is written by a consortium in which besides the applicants other participants are involved as well. Details on who can apply can be found in section 3.1 of the call text.

### Consortium building

In this Call for proposals, consortium formation is encouraged through the use of matchmaking. Participation in the matchmaking meeting is encouraged, but not mandatory. The objective of matchmaking is to bring together potential applicants, researchers, collaborators and stakeholders from various fields and sectors to exchange ideas about, or the need for, research related to the topic and impact described in the Call for proposals.

### What to apply for

For an application in this Call for proposals, a minimum of €600,000 and a maximum of €1,360,000 of NWO funding can be applied for. With this, NWO finances maximally 80% of the total project budget; the rest of the funding must be contributed by the compulsory co-funding. NWO never funds less than 50% of the total project budget.

The applicant and co-applicants can include costs for personnel, equipment, investments, knowledge utilization and project management.

### When to apply

When you submit your application in ISAAC, you will also need to enter some details online. Therefore please start submitting your application at least one day before the deadline of this Call for proposals. Applications that are submitted after the deadline will not be taken into consideration.

- The deadline for submitting full proposals is **12 February 2026, before 14:00:00 CET**.
- The deadline for submitting a request for review of foreign organisations (see section 7.1.1) is **26 January 2026, by 14:00:00 CET**.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Problem definition and analysis
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact
3. Quality of the consortium
4. Quality of the research
Criteria 1 and 2 will each count for 20% of the final verdict, criteria 3 and 4 will each count for 30%.

### Procedure

The application procedure consists of the following steps:

- matchmaking; around September (optional);
- submission of the proposal; Thursday 12 February 2026, before 14:00 CET;
- admissibility of the proposal;
- peer review by referees; February-April 2026;
- rebuttal; May 2026;
- pre-advice assessment committee;
- interview selection (if neccesary);
- interview; June/July 2026
- assessment committee meeting; June/July 2026
- decision-making by the Board of directors of NWO; September 2026.

## Prepare

Applications must be submitted through the Isaac application and reporting system. If you do not have an Isaac account yet, then please create one at least one workday in advance. More information about writing a proposal and which forms you need to use can be found in Isaac. Please download and use the forms in Isaac. Below you will find examples of the required forms.

### Write proposal

The application should indicate how the proposed research relates to the Call for proposals and on which societal problem/challenge and which key technology(ies) the application focusses. It should also indicate the goals, hypotheses and scientific feasibility of the project. In addition, the application should show how the consortium is composed and will work together to increase the applicability of the research results. For this, the Impact Plan approach should be followed and described in the proposal.

### Fill in forms

- declarations co-funding from co-funders (required);
- budget form (required);
- letter guaranteeing the continuity of project supervision (if applicable);
- Research Organisation Declaration (if applicable).
