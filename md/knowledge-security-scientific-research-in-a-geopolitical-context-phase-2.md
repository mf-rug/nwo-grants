# Knowledge security: scientific research in a geopolitical context – Phase 2

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/knowledge-security-scientific-research-in-a-geopolitical-context-phase-2

International research collaboration is of great societal and economic value, but at the same time makes the Dutch knowledge sector vulnerable to unwanted transfer, interference, and unethical use of scientific knowledge and research. This NWA programme promotes scientific and applied research into developments around knowledge security and inherent dilemmas related to knowledge security policies.

### Purpose

The NWA programme 'Knowledge security: scientific research in a geopolitical context' promotes research into developments around knowledge security and inherent dilemmas in the context of an evolving geopolitical landscape. The programme aims to contribute to the development of a conceptual framework for the study of knowledge security and yield insights into the international context and threats, the organisation and vulnerability of the knowledge sector, and the balance between opportunities and risks in knowledge security policies. These insights, together with tangible action perspectives, should help relevant stakeholders within the knowledge sector and government to develop balanced policies and deal with dilemmas. In this way, this programme aims to increase the resilience of the Dutch knowledge sector and bolster national security, in such a way that core academic values are being protected and promoted and society can continue to benefit from international cooperation.

### Who can apply

**Please note:** this is a closed application. Only one of the main applicants who submitted a preproposal that was selected by NWO may submit a proposal.

The application has to be submitted by a main applicant, on behalf of a consortium. A consortium should include at least a main applicant, co-applicant and cooperation partner.

This Call for proposals consists of two phases. In phase 1, consortia submit a preproposal that addresses one of the four themes identified in the Call. In phase 2, the applicants of the selected preproposals jointly develop a proposal by participating in synergy workshops, after which the proposal is submitted by a main applicant who has been appointed in phase 2.

All consortium members should play an active role in formulating the research questions and in preparing and executing the project. The requirements per consortium member are explained in further detail in paragraph 3.1 of the Call for proposals.

### What to apply for

In this Call for proposals, a total of €6,600,000 is available, and a maximum of one proposal will be awarded funding. In phase 2, the applicants of the four preproposals selected in phase 1 of the procedure jointly submit one application, composed of an overarching work package and four thematic work packages.

At least €2,000,000 is available for the overarching work package. A maximum of €1,150,000 is available for each thematic work package. Depending on the number of preproposals selected, any remaining budget will be added to the overarching work package (phase 2). The budget for the preproposals (phase 1) is released  once the research proposal of the application (phase 2) has been approved. The proposed project has a duration of a minimum of 48 months/4 years and a maximum of 72 months/6 years.

### Write proposal

**Please note:** only main applicants of preproposals that were selected by NWO may submit a proposal in phase 2 of the procedure.

In this Call for proposals, four complementary themes have been identified within which knowledge gaps exist with regards to knowledge security:

1. International context and threats
2. Organisation and vulnerability of knowledge practice
3. Balance (between opportunities and risks) and policy
4. Action perspectives: increasing resilience
In phase 1 of this Call for proposals, it is mandatory to submit a preproposal. A preproposal is a concise application addressing one of the four themes described above. The preproposal describes which knowledge questions the applicant and co-applicants want to address and how this contributes to the overall objectives and impact described in the Call for proposals. In addition, the preproposal describes which opportunities and leads the applicants anticipate for cooperation with the consortia working on the other themes.

In phase 2, one application is developed and submitted by a consortium consisting of parties that have submitted the selected preproposals. In this phase, the selected preproposals serve as starting point and are integrated into a single proposal. The proposal consists of an overarching work package in which an interdisciplinary conceptual framework is developed, which forms the basis to coherently address the four themes and the objectives and impact described in the Call for proposals.

NWO will assess your application against all the conditions set out in the Call for proposals, including the conditions listed below. Your application will only be admitted to the assessment procedure if it meets these conditions. After submitting your application, NWO requests you to be available to implement any possible administrative corrections so that you can (still) meet the conditions for submission. These conditions are:

- the main applicant and co-applicant(s) meet the conditions stated in Section 3.1;
- the application complies with the DORA guidelines as described in Section 4.1;
- the application is submitted via the main applicant’s ISAAC account;
- the application is received before the deadline;
- the application is written in English;
- the application budget is drawn according to the terms of this Call for proposals (using the format made available which includes the most recent rates);
- the proposed project has a duration of at least 48 months/4 years and maximum 72 months/6 years;
- all of the required annexes, after a possible request for additions or changes, have been completed and submitted completely and in accordance with the terms of this Call of proposals;
- if applicable: the co-financing has been correctly and fully committed through co-financing statements, after any supplement or modification request.
