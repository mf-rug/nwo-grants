# BrainHealth Call 2026-1 - Biological, social and environmental factors in brain health

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/brainhealth-call-2026-1-biological-social-and-environmental-factors-in-brain-health

This call of the European Partnership BrainHealth invites applications to combine a holistic approach to brain health with in-depth biological understanding. The aim of the call is to facilitate multinational, collaborative and interdisciplinary research that addresses critical translational questions. The call is open for international collaborations comprised of at least 3 organisations and a maximum of 6 organisations. Funding is provided by NWO in collaboration with the Brain Foundation Netherlands.

## Explore

### Purpose

Brain disorders are a leading cause of disability and mortality globally, placing a great burden on people living with brain disorders, their families, social relations, professional and informal carers, as well as healthcare systems and national economies as a whole. It is thus crucial to identify the factors that influence brain health throughout life, from prenatal stages to advanced age, as well as methods to monitor them, influence them, or both. Brain health is dynamic and shaped by biological, social and environmental factors at every stage of life. Advancing the scientific knowledge about these factors may enable positive changes in brain health trajectories and lead to opportunities for prevention and recovery along the life-course. Applications for this call, should address how biological, social and environmental factors affect the trajectory of neurological, mental and sensory disorders across the lifespan.

### Who can apply

Each proposal must include minimal three(in their country or region) admissible legal entities from at least three different countries. Maximum three consortium partners can be from the same country. Maximum total number of partners in a consortium is six, excluding partners participating with own expenses. Researchers from Dutch universities, universities of applied sciences and other knowledge institutions can submit applications to NWO. See the NWO Annex and the call for proposals for further information.

A grant application (i.e. the Dutch part of a consortium) has a single main applicant (i.e. Dutch partner or coordinator). National co-applicants may be part of the application. An applicant can only apply for NWO funding for one project (as part of a European consortium).

### What to apply for

NWO and the Brain Foundation Netherlands, will provide a total of 2 million euro in funding for this call. 

A maximum of 400,000 euro can be applied for at NWO for a project proposal in this call. The maximum project duration is 3 years. Available NWO budget modules are: Personnel (Postdoc, Non-Scientific Personnel, Research leave), Material costs, Knowledge utilisation and Project management.

### When to apply

- Deadline pre-proposal (obligatory): **10 March 2026, 14:00 CET**.
- Deadline for full proposals: **30 June 2026, 14:00 CEST**.

### Assessment

### Criteria

Applications are assessed on the following criteria:

- Excellence
- Impact
- Quality and efficiency of the implementation
A full description of the assessment criteria can be found in the Call for proposals.

### Procedure

Eligible pre-proposals will be reviewed using the evaluation criteria via a written peer review process. Based on that, a ranking list will be established according to which the Call Steering Committee will decide on full-proposal invitations.

An international interdisciplinary Peer Review Panel will evaluate the eligible full proposals based on the above-mentioned evaluation criteria and establish a ranking list. In this evaluation, the Peer Review Panel will take into account the assessments of patient reviewers, as well as recommendations provided by the ethics committee.

Based on the advice of the panel and the available budget, a financing decision is taken by the participating national and regional financers. For NWO, the Executive Board subsequently decides on the allocation of Dutch projects.

## Prepare

The pre-proposal and the full proposal must be submitted by the transnational Project Coordinator through the [online submission portal](https://www.brainhealth-partnership.eu/calls/jtc2026-neurological-mental-sensory-disorders/).

### Write proposal

The application process will be in two stages: pre-proposals and full-proposals. At both stages, one joint proposal document shall be prepared by the consortium (in English) and submitted by the coordinator to the transnational online application portal, hosted by the EP BrainHealth Joint Call Secretariat. The individual research partners in a consortium will be funded by the respective national/regional funding organisation(s).

Eligibility criteria are the matter of individual partner funding organisations and additional national/regional regulations, requirements and processes may apply. The national annex of NWO can be found on this page (Tab 2. Prepare).

### Fill in forms

**Proposal form**

The proposal (prep-proposal and full proposal) is submitted through the online submission portal of the partnership.  The proposal does not need to be sent to NWO separately.

**Budget form**

It is recommended to use the NWO budget template in the pre-proposal stage to confirm eligibility of budget items. For full proposals, it is mandatory to submit the NWO budget form on a national level to NWO before the deadline for full proposals (share per e-mail: [brainhealt@nwo.nl](mailto:brainhealt@nwo.nl)).
