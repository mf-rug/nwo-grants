### Purpose

Digital interconnectedness is an integral part of Dutch society. Citizens, governments and businesses are closely intertwined in complex digital ecosystems, in which information, processes and services are continuously exchanged. This interconnectedness offers major advantages, but also makes society vulnerable. Disruptions, incidents or cyber attacks within a digital ecosystem can lead to serious societal disruption.

The aim of this call is to develop socio-technical innovations and courses of action that increase the security and resilience of digital ecosystems. A security by design and security by default approach is strongly encouraged. Ecosystems in which the right technological and social choices are made from the outset enable participants to  make use of the ecosystem’s possibilities in a natural way and with minimal security risks.

The Call for Proposals has a two-phase structure. In the first phase, research into sector‑specific digital ecosystems is funded. In the second phase, these insights are translated into cross‑sector guidelines for action for stakeholders within sectors and within government, brought together in one overarching project. The outcomes can also be used to scale up interventions and to shape new digital ecosystems that are secure and resilient by design.

### Who can apply

Applications in the first phase of this call are submitted by a main applicant on behalf of a consortium. Within

the consortium, four categories of participants are distinguished:

1. Main applicant
2. Co-applicant(s)
3. Cooperation partner(s)
4. Co-funder(s) (optional)
A consortium should consist of at least a main applicant, a co-applicant and cooperation partner.

In the second phase of this call, the main applicants of the research projects awarded in phase 1 will be invited

to form a consortium for an overarching research project. In addition, co-applicants from the underlying

research projects may be included in the consortium, insofar as their involvement contributes to the

objectives of the overarching project. In this phase, the consortium may be

expanded to include new cooperation partners and co-funders who were not involved in phase 1.

The conditions are explained in more detail in the Call for Proposals.

### Consortium building

In order to increase the impact of the research, collaboration between researchers, researchers from other

disciplines and partners from society, such as companies and societal organisations, is necessary. In this call, NWO supports the formation of a consortium by organising the following meetings:

- Matchmaking event (phase 1): on 23 April 2026, NWO will organise a matchmaking event for this call. Participation in this activity is recommended but not mandatory.
- Facilitating workshops (phase 2):  For the overarching project in phase 2, NWO is organising workshops to stimulate the development of the overarching application and the formation of the associated consortium. Participation in these facilitating workshops is mandatory: each phase 1 project must be represented by at least one project leader or work package leader. NWO will communicate in a timely manner with the phase 1 project leaders regarding the organization of these workshops.
Additional information about consortium formation can be found in the Call for proposals.

### What to apply for

The available budget for this Call for proposals is €8,702,072 in total. An amount of €7,500,000 is available for the first phase. For each phase 1 project, a grant amount of at most  €1,875,000 can be applied for. Four or five applications are expected to be allocated within this phase. For the second phase, one overarching connection project can be

awarded. The available budget for this phase 2 project is €1,202,072.

More information about the available budget can be found in the Call for proposals.

### Assessment

### Criteria

Applications submitted under this call will be assessed in both phases on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Problem definition and analysis (20%)
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%)
3. Quality of the consortium (30%)
4. Quality of the research (30%)

### Write proposal

An application can only be submitted via the web application ISAAC. Applications that are not submitted via ISAAC will not be taken into consideration.

As the main applicant, you are required to submit your application via your own personal ISAAC account.

###### To prepare applications in phases 1 and 2, follow these steps:

- download the application form from the NWO web application ISAAC or from the NWO web page (on the grant page of the funding instrument concerned);
- complete the application form;
- save the application form in ISAAC as a PDF file and upload it with any compulsory annexes;
