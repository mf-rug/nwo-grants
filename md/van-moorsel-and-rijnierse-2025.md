# Van Moorsel and Rijnierse 2025

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/van-moorsel-and-rijnierse-2025

The funding instrument Van Moorsel and Rijnierse supports innovative art historical and archaeological research on medieval Christianity in Egypt and the Nile Valley.

## Characteristics

**Status:** Closed  
**Closing date full application:** 4 March 2025 14:00 hrs.  
**For specific groups:** International Collaboration Researchers from Abroad Starting Researchers  
**Budget:** €345,000  
**Research programme:** Foundation Van Moorsel en Rijnierse  

## Explore

### Purpose

The Call for proposals Van Moorsel and Rijnierse is aimed at postdoctoral researchers within five years of obtaining their PhD. With this Call for proposals, NWO and the Van Moorsel and Rijnierse Foundation promote new and pioneering research on medieval Coptic Orthodox Christianity in Egypt and the Nile Valley.

The Call for proposals stems from the bequest of Johanna Rijnierse (Sloten, 5 February 1917 - Leiden, 23 September 2013), administered by NWO and the Van Moorsel and Rijnierse Foundation.

### Who can apply

The Call for proposals is open to international applicants who defended their PhD between 4 March 2020 and 4 March 2025 (the deadline of the round). The research must be undertaken at a university or knowledge institution in the Kingdom of the Netherlands (see section 3.1. in the Call for proposals).

The applicant should have a permanent or temporary contract at the university, or the prospect of a temporary contract for the length of the project. To this end, the applicant should have a letter of support signed by the university in question.

### What to apply for

A maximum of € 345,000 can be requested in this Call for proposals. The maximum duration of the proposed project is 3 years.

### When to apply

- The deadline for submitting applications is the **4 March 2025, 14:00 CET**.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The application will be assessed based on the following two assessment criteria:

1. Scientific quality of the research proposal (50%)
2. Quality of the researcher (50%)

### Procedure

The application process consists of the following steps:

- submission of the application;
- consideration of the application;
- pre-selection (optional);
- preliminary assessment committee;
- interview;
- meeting of the assessment committee;
- decision-making.
