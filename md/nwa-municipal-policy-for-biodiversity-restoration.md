# NWA Municipal policy for biodiversity restoration

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/nwa-municipal-policy-for-biodiversity-restoration

This Call for proposals invites knowledge institutions and societal parties to form consortia for the development of scientific and practice-oriented research proposals aimed at the theme nature-inclusive municipal policy for biodiversity restoration in the built environment. The consortia are required to work in an interdisciplinary and transdisciplinary manner and should have a knowledge-chain-wide composition. Knowledge-chain-wide implies that proposals connect fundamental, applied and practice-oriented research and that connection is sought with the knowledge needs of societal parties.

### Purpose

Biodiversity loss is an immense challenge for both science and society. Therefore, it is of great importance that biodiversity is not only taken into account, but also prioritised in policy and decision-making at national, provincial and municipal levels where necessary. The Call focuses on the development of knowledge that supports transformative change aimed at biodiversity restoration and conservation.

### Who can apply

Pre-proposals and proposals are to be submitted by the main applicant on behalf of the consortium.

There are four categories of participants within a consortium:

1. Main applicant;
2. Co-applicant(s);
3. Cooperation partner(s);
4. Co-funder(s) (optional).
A consortium should consist of at least a main applicant, a co-applicant and cooperation partner. The conditions for each type of participant are explained in more detail in the Call for Proposals

### Consortium building

Research on complex and challenging issues requires collaboration between research disciplines, between practice-oriented and fundamental research, and with businesses and social organisations. That collaboration takes place in consortia. NWO encourages and supports the formation of consortia through meetings and workshops. In this Call for Proposals, consortium formation is encouraged through the use of matchmaking for pre-proposals and synergy workshops for a proposal. More information can be found in the Call for Proposals.

### What to apply for

Per project, a grant amount of at most € 3,820,000 can be applied for. The maximum duration of the proposed project is 5 years. The applicant and co-applicants can include costs for personnel, equipment, investments and knowledge utilisation. Within this Call three pre-proposals will be selected. In the pre-proposal, applicants propose living labs. The selected applicants together submit one full proposal, which consists of an overarching research project and three living labs. In total, a budget of € 3,820,000 is available.

### Write proposal

The submission of a pre-proposal is compulsory for this Call for proposals.

In phase 1, pre-proposals have to be submitted via ISAAC. The pre-proposal covers the living labs as described in subsection 2.2.3. In addition, the pre-proposal should describe the method that will be used for the research, and outline what opportunities exist for collaboration with consortia working on the other research contexts. Finally, the pre-proposal describes which potential consortium members, such as collaboration partners and co-financiers, are still envisaged in phase 2.

Prior to the submission deadline for pre-proposals, NWO will organise a matchmaking meeting on 6 November 2025 (see subsection 4.2.1 and the timeline in subsection 4.2.14). Additional information on the matchmaking meeting will be announced at a later date on the programme page of the NWO website.

A pre-proposal must leave sufficient room for collaboration, adjustments and synergy with other pre-proposals so that in phase 2, the pre-proposals selected can be developed into one single integrated project. During phase 1, no detailed budget specifications will be requested, given that the definitive proposed project activities will only be finalised in phase 2. The main applicant and co-applicants of the selected pre-proposals will be invited to develop one single full proposal for phase 2.

### Mandatory consortium-building activities

The main applicants whose pre-proposals have been selected are obliged to take part in the synergy workshops. If a main applicant has been unable to attend, for example in the event of force majeure as referred to in the NWO Force Majeure Extension Scheme, a replacement participant must have taken part in the synergy workshops. Co-applicants and collaboration partners of the selected pre-proposals are also invited to participate.

### Fill in forms

The pre-proposal should be drawn up in the template provided by NWO.

The full proposal should be drawn up in the template provided by NWO. For a full proposal the following annexes are compulsory:

- budget;
- declarations of commitment of cooperation partners;
- Declaration of de-minimis support (mandatory if applicable, see paragraph 3.1.1 and 3.5.8);
- declaration co-funding from co-funders (mandatory if applicable);
- letter guaranteeing the continuity of the project supervision (compulsory if applicable, see paragraph 3.1);
- form 'Statement and signature'.
