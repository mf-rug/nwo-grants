# Caribbean Research – PhD Scholarships 2025

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/caribbean-research-phd-scholarships-2025

This Call for proposals is a PhD Scholarship programme developed for graduates (with a master’s degree or equivalent) and offers scholarships to conduct PhD research related to the Caribbean part of the Kingdom of the Netherlands and current challenges and knowledge questions in this region. This is a science-wide programme with a multidisciplinary character, not limited to any scientific or scholarly field of research, and therefore offers space for a wide range of topics and research areas related to the Caribbean region. PhD candidates must submit an application themselves. The procedure consists of a Statement of intent, pre-proposal, a full proposal and interview.

### Purpose

This PhD scholarship programme aims to contribute to the overarching goals of the programme [Caribbean Research: a multidisciplinary approach](https://www.nwo.nl/en/researchprogrammes/caribbean-research-a-multidisciplinary-approach), to strengthen the local knowledge infrastructure and to stimulate scientific research focused on societal issues. More specifically, to increase and strengthen scientific and scholarly knowledge production on and about the Caribbean region. The knowledge and research experience gained should directly benefit local research organisations and society in the Caribbean region.

In order to stimulate and support the main goals of this Call for proposals, there is a priority treatment for a maximum of three PhD positions meant for applicants with an (intended) main PhD trajectory at either the University of Aruba, University of Curaçao or University of St. Martin.

### Who can apply

PhD candidates are applicants themselves and submit a Statement of intent, pre-proposal and full application, in agreement with the intended responsible PhD supervisor and other supervisor(s) and intended research organisation.

For applications the following general requirements apply:

- The applicant holds a university master degree (or equivalent), at the time of submitting the pre-proposal.
- An application can only be submitted by one applicant (not for another applicant, duos or (research) groups).
- The applicant does not already have a PhD title.
- At the time of submission of the Statement of intent, the applicant does not already hold a fully paid PhD position or receive full-time funding for PhD research, regardless of the subject of the research.
There are two categories of scholarships that applicants can apply for, which are determined by the main conditions below:

- Category A – meant for applicants with an (intended) main PhD trajectory at either the University of Aruba, University of Curaçao or University of St. Martin.
- Category B – meant for applicants with an (intended) PhD trajectory at another research organisation than mentioned in category A.
** Requirement category B: If you intend to obtain a PhD at a research organisation in the Netherlands, you must be born on one of the following islands: Aruba, Bonaire, Curaçao, Saba, Sint Eustatius, Sint Maarten (and currently reside outside of the above-mentioned islands). Or you must reside/be registered on the above-mentioned islands.*

### What to apply for

In total a maximum of € 350.000 can be requested. This includes salary with a maximum duration of the proposed project between 48 months (1 fte) or equivalent of that up to a maximum of 72 months (0,65 fte). A maximum of 15% of the NWO grant amount allocated for salary of the main applicant can be requested for all project-specific material costs. And a maximum of 10% of the grant amount for material costs can be requested for research organisations abroad (i.e. outside the country of the main PhD appointment and outside the Kingdom of the Netherlands).

### Assessment

### Criteria

The pre-proposal will be assessed using the following:

- Profile of the applicant:
University education, Research experience, Motivation for PhD research, Embedding of the research, Training plan.
The full application is assessed using the following criteria:

- **Potential contribution and relevance of the research to science (40%)**- Scientific relevance of the research and the extent to which the proposed research has the potential to make an important contribution to the advancement of scientific knowledge and/or theory;
- The extent to which the proposed research contains (elements of) scientific innovation in terms of theory, methods and/or topic;
- Proposed scientific question (clarity of the hypothesis, research question(s), objective(s) and discussion).
- **Research design: approach and methodology (30%)**- Research design is appropriate to the research question(s) and objective(s)
- Effectiveness of the research approach and methodology;
- Feasibility in terms of: work plan, available research time, available infrastructure and supervision;
- Embedding of the research(er): supervision and quality of the research group, applicants must be guided by an adequate research team, containing the relevant expertise, which will be frequently contacted.
- **Potential impact and contribution to knowledge questions and current societal challenges in the Caribbean part of the Kingdom of the Netherlands in the short and long term (30%)**- Value and benefits of the research for the Caribbean region and positioning of the intended research in a wider societal context;
- Embedding of the research(er): effectiveness of the research (team) in relation to societal impact in the Caribbean region.
- Long-term vision of the applicant to ensure durability of the research in the Caribbean region.

### Procedure

A science-wide assessment committee assesses and prioritizes the pre-proposals and full applications, without making use of external reviewers. The pre-proposals will be assessed based on a written assessment and committee meeting. All applicants that are invited to submit a full proposal on 24 February 2026 will be invited for an interview in March 2026. The assessment committee advises the Executive Board of NWO that will make the decision on the pre-proposals and full proposals in respectively October 2025 and the end of June 2026.

### Statement of intent

## What is a Statement of intent?

An application starts with a Statement of intent, followed by a pre-proposal phase, a full proposal phase (if invited) and an interview. With a Statement of intent, you indicate that you intend to submit an application for this Call for proposals. The Statement of intent allows NWO to establish the expected number of applications. This Statement of intent is not part of the assessment procedure, however is compulsory and must be submitted before the submission deadline.

In this Statement of intent we ask further information about the intended research organisation and supervision (responsible PhD supervisor (promotor), co-promotor and/or other (daily) supervisors) in relation to the intended research proposal. Please note that you are allowed to make adjustments in your intended research and supervisory team in the pre-proposal.

**Please note!** When submitting the Statement of intent, you must complete two steps before the deadline:

1. You must fill in details in ISAAC (title of the proposal and a short summary of the PhD research),
2. You must send the required forms by email to [phd-caribbean@nwo.nl](mailto:phd-caribbean@nwo.nl)
You can find the forms for the Statement of intent under ‘Downloads for your application’.
If you have not submitted a Statement of intent (and compulsory annex), it is not possible to participate in the application process.

### Write proposal

The pre-proposal focuses on the profile of the applicant. In the narrative CV, the applicant is asked to describe their own qualities and motivation for conducting PhD research and the research in particular. In addition, the applicant is asked for key output, which can include both scientific and non-scientific activities that the applicant has developed. The activities should not be a summary or list, but they are asked to provide context.

The full application focuses on an elaboration of the research idea. How does the proposed research potentially contribute to science, what is the potential impact and contribution to society in the Caribbean part of the Kingdom of the Netherlands? In addition, the research design is requested to be described in a realistic work plan, explaining step by step how the approach and methodology of the project is carried out, as well with respect to supervision of the research project.

### Fill in forms

For this Call for proposals, we ask the applicant to complete the compulsory annex in addition to completing a Statement of intent. In the Statement of intent, we ask for additional information about the intended research organisation and supervision (promoter, co-promoter and/or other (daily) supervisors) in relation to the intended research proposal, which must also be signed by all parties. In the annex, we ask for additional personal information about, for instance, the applicant's country of birth and current place of residence. If you have not submitted a Statement of intent (and compulsory annex), it is not possible to participate in the application procedure.

In the pre-proposal phase, in addition to completing the pre-proposal application form, we also ask you to complete two compulsory annexes: Supervision declaration and University declaration. Here, we ask for additional information about the structure of the supervision, such as the division of tasks and structure of the contact moments, and for additional information about the agreements made with the university, respectively.

In the full application phase, in addition to the application form, we also ask you to complete a compulsory financial plan (Budget) of the project.

When submitting, if necessary, the annex Statement appointment and project supervision can also be added, in which the intended institute/university indicates who will take over the supervision if the (co)promoter has to be replaced.
