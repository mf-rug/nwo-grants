# KIC Reducing critical raw materials use in the energy transition

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/kic-reducing-critical-raw-materials-use-in-the-energy-transition

The energy transition requires rapid upscaling of technologies. This transition is currently dependent on the availability of so-called 'critical raw materials'. ## Explore

### Purpose

The energy transition requires a rapid upscaling of technologies such as wind turbines, electrolysers, solar panels, and batteries. This transition currently depends on the availability of so-called ‘critical raw materials’, which are necessary to utilize these technologies.

The aim of this program is to reduce the dependence on these scarce and geopolitically risky critical raw materials for the energy transition. As a result, this theme will contribute to greater certainty in the availability of materials and the progress of the energy transition by redesigning (parts of) the entire value chain. The focus is on reducing or replacing critical materials in the value chain.

For this program, we consider both critical raw materials and strategic raw materials to be relevant, as both groups of materials face the same societal challenge. Specifically, we follow the most recent (2023) definition and lists from the [CRMA](https://single-market-economy.ec.europa.eu/sectors/raw-materials/areas-specific-interest/critical-raw-materials_en#fifth-list-2023-of-critical-raw-materials-for-the-eu) to determine which materials belong to these groups.

### Who can apply

The main applicant can submit the application together with one or more co-applicants. The public-private partnership consortium prepares the application, which involves not only the applicants but also other participants.

The main and co-applicant(s) may carry out the proposed research within a consortium that includes, in addition to themselves, at least two or more co-financiers, possibly supplemented by one or more collaboration partners. All consortium partners are expected to play an active role in formulating the research questions as well as in the design and implementation of the project.

### Consortium building

NWO supports the formation of a consortium by organising a matchmaking meeting. The aim of matchmaking is to bring together potential applicants, researchers, collaboration partners and stakeholders from various fields and sectors to exchange ideas for, or needs for, research on the theme and impact described in the Call for Proposals. For this call, the matchmaking meeting will take place on 29 January 2026. Participation in this matchmaking is recommended but not mandatory.

### What to apply for

For an application in this Call for proposals, a minimum of €750,000 and a maximum of €1,300,000 of NWO funding can be applied for in. With this, NWO finances maximally 80% of the total project budget; the rest of the funding must be contributed by the compulsory co-funding. NWO never funds less than 50% of the total project budget.

The applicant and co-applicants may include costs for personnel, equipment, investments, knowledge utilisation and project management. The available budget modules (including the maximum amounts) are listed in paragraph 3.2 of the call text. Apply only for funding that is vital to realise the project.

### Line of Research

This Call for Proposals requests proposals that focus on reducing the use of critical raw materials in the following areas of application of the energy transition:

1. Electrolysis devices and electrolysis plants
2. Wind turbines
3. Photovoltaic energy
4. Battery systems
5. Other areas relevant to the energy transition, such as network infrastructure such as cables and transformers, power electronics, or the production of alternative fuels, etc.
Applicants are asked to focus primarily on one area of application. Sufficient funding is available for at least one project in each of the five areas of application.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications that NWO receives for this call will be assessed based on the following criteria:

1. Problem definition and analysis (20%)
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%)
3. Quality of the consortium (30%)
4. Quality of the research (30%)

### Procedure

The application procedure consists of the following steps:

- matchmaking (not mandatory);
- submission of the proposal;
- admissibility of the proposal;
- peer review by referees;
- rebuttal;
- pre-advice assessment committee;
- interview selection;
- interview;
- site visit;
- assessment committee meeting;
- decision-making

### Write proposal

In your application, please describe how your proposed research aligns with the objectives of this call for proposals. Clearly outline the societal challenge your project aims to address, along with its goals, hypotheses, research methods, and scientific feasibility.

We also invite you to showcase how your consortium is composed and how the partners will collaborate to maximize the practical relevance and impact of the research outcomes. To support this, the proposal should follow and describe the Impact Plan approach, ensuring a thoughtful strategy for translating research into realworld solutions.
