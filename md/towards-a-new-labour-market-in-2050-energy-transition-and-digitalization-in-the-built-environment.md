# Towards a new labour market in 2050: Energy transition and digitalization in the built environment

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/towards-a-new-labour-market-in-2050-energy-transition-and-digitalization-in-the-built-environment

This call is focused on developing knowledge on what the labour market of the built environment will look like in 2050. This research provides insight into the future-oriented challenges posed by the digital and energy transitions to the labour market of the built environment. Insights and knowledge from this call should contribute to the development of a future framework for the labour market of the built environment.

### Purpose

This call is focused on developing knowledge on what the labour market of the built environment will look like in 2050. This research provides insight into the future-oriented challenges posed by the digital and energy transitions to the labour market of the built environment. Insights and knowledge from this call should contribute to the development of a future framework for the labour market of the built environment.

### Who can apply

The main applicant submits the application on behalf of the consortium.

There are four categories of participant within a consortium:

1. Main applicant;
2. Co-applicant(s);
3. Cooperating partner(s);
4. Co-funder(s) (optional).
More information about the four categories of participants as well as specific requirements regarding who can apply, can be found in paragraph 3.1 of the call for proposals.

### What to apply for

The main applicant and co-applicants can include costs for personnel, equipment, investments and knowledge utilisation. The available budget modules (including maximum amounts) are listed in the Call for proposals. Apply only for what is essential to carry out the project. The rates and an explanation of these budget modules are given in Annex 7.1 of the Call for proposals.

### When to apply

- Deadline proposal: Thursday 25 September 2025 by 14:00:00 CEST

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

- Problem definition and analysis, contribution to a solution
- Envisaged impact and route to impact
- Quality of the consortium
- Quality of the research
Each assessment criterion consists of several subcriteria. More information the criteria can be found in paragraph 4.3 of the call for proposals.

### Procedure

The application procedure consists of the following steps:

- Registering for and participating in collaborative workshops;
- submission of the proposal;
- admissibility of the proposal;
- preliminary advice assessment committee;
- interview selection;
- interview;
- meeting of the assessment committee;
- decision-making.
Detailed information about the procedure can be found in the Call for Proposals, Section 4.2.

An application is prepared (and submitted) after the collaborative workshops, where research ideas were brought together, possible collaborations with other workshop participants were explored and where crucial initial agreements for joint development of the application were made.

The (eventual) lead applicant must have participated in both collaborative workshop.

### Fill in forms

To prepare your application, please follow the steps below:

- download the application form from the online application system ISAAC or from th
- NWO website (on the website of the relevant financing instrument);
- complete the application form;
- save the form as a PDF file and submit it to ISAAC with any mandatory attachments;
- complete the information requested online in ISAAC.
Mandatory attachments:

- budget;
- support statements from collaboration partner(s) (mandatory if applicable);
- statement from co-financier(s) (mandatory if applicable);
