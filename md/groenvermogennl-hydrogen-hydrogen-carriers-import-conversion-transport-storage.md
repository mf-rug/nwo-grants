# GroenvermogenNL: Hydrogen & Hydrogen Carriers: Import, Conversion, Transport & Storage

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/groenvermogennl-hydrogen-hydrogen-carriers-import-conversion-transport-storage

This call is part of the National Growth Fund GroenvermogenNL. A well-developed infrastructure for hydrogen and hydrogen carriers is necessary to exploit the Netherlands' potential in the hydrogen market. This call invites researchers and companies to form a consortium for research into import, conversion, transport, and storage of hydrogen (carriers).

## Explore

### Purpose

The aim of this Call for Proposals is to implement the second funding phase of GroenvermogenNL Work Package 2: Hydrogen & Hydrogen Carriers: Import, Conversion, Transport & Storage. This Call for Proposals focuses on breakthroughs and innovations with technical and economic potential for large-scale import, conversion, transport, and storage of hydrogen and hydrogen carriers. One consortium will be established through workshops.

Research areas are:

1.            Liquid Hydrogen (LH2) import, transport & storage,

2.            Hydrogen carrier import, conversion, transport & storage,

3.            Development and optimization of port infrastructure, supply chains, and conversion hubs.

### Who can apply

The proposal can be submitted by a consortium existing of:

1. Researchers from universities, universities of applied science, TO2 institutions and other research organisations.
2. Employees of businesses and other public and private organisations provided that:
- They have a tenured position for at least 0.6 fte;- Their organisation has demonstrable Research & Development (R&D) activities in the Netherlands;
- They hold a master’s degree.

### Consortium building

Consortium building takes places during two multiple-day workshops. The workshops are mandatory for the main applicant of the consortium. Co-applicants, cofinanciers and collaborating partners preferably also take part in the workshops.  Signing up for the workshops can be done via ISAAC.

### What to apply for

A maximum total of €13,000,000 can be requested from NWO for the grant application in this Call for Proposals. NWO will fund a maximum of 80% of the project, and a minimum of 51%.

You will therefore need to arrange other funding for this call. This can consist of an own contribution by enterprises and civil society organisations and cash or in-kind co-funding from private parties. See chapter 3.2 of the call for proposals for more conditions.

Projects may last for a maximum of six years.

### When to apply

- Workshop registration deadline: Tuesday 10 March 2026, 14:00 CET.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The proposal will be assessed on the basis of three criteria:

1. Comply with the Framework for State aid for research and development and innovation for research organisations,
2. Quality of the consortium,
3. Quality of the research plan.

### Procedure

The procedure fort his Call for proposals consists of 3 steps:

1. Registration of organisations for the workshops (deadline 10 March 2026, 14:00 CET),
2. Workshops (1 – 3 April/5 – 7 May 2026),
3. Submission of the grant proposal (deadline 9 July 2026, 14:00 CEST).
After submission, the proposal will be assessed by the selection committee, which will advise the NWO Domain Science Board about whether or not to award funding.

The decision of the Domain Science board is expected in the fourth quarter of 2026.

Organisations that want to participate in the grant proposal within this Call for proposals are required to submit an Application form Workshops to participate in the procedure.
