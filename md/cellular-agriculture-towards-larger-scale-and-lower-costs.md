# Cellular Agriculture - towards larger scale and lower costs

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/cellular-agriculture-towards-larger-scale-and-lower-costs

Cellular Agriculture is a technology to produce animal products merely using animal cells and micro-organisms, without the actual animal. This call is part of the National Growth Fund Project Cellular Agriculture and aims to reduce costs and scale up this technology. Specifically, in the research lines precision fermentation and cultivated meat. Research proposals for this call should address advancements in process and/or product technology and biology.

## Explore

### Purpose

This call aims to address key topics needed to cost-effectively and successfully produce at scale. Specifically, we seek research proposals that focus on advances in process and/or product technology and biology and fall within one of the two research lines: (A) precision fermentation, (B) cultured meat & seafood. A proposal should focus on and be limited to the topics indicated in the Call for proposals (see paragraph 2.1).

### Who can apply

Researchers may act as main or co-applicants if they are employed by a Dutch research organisation such as universities, universities of applied sciences, TO2 institutes or other institutes. For research proposals, consortia must be formed consisting of at least one main applicant and at least one co-funder.

### Consortium building

The consortia envisaged here must consist of at least one main applicant and at least one co-funder. Co-applicants and/or cooperation partners can join the consortium.

To facilitate consortium formation, at 25 June 2025 a matchmaking meeting is organised, for which interested parties can register. The registration form as well as more information about the meeting can be found on the corresponding website. The matchmaking meeting is optional and therefore not a mandatory part of the application procedure.

### What to apply for

A maximum of €1,371,836 NWO budget can be applied for per project. At least 3 research positions must be applied for per project, of which at least 1 is a PhD student and at most 1 is an Engineering Doctorate (EngD). A postdoc position counts as a research position if it has a minimum duration of 2 years, 1.0 fte (or equivalent). The applicant and co-applicants can include costs for personnel, equipment and knowledge utilisation. The maximum duration of the proposed project is 5 years.

The co-funders jointly contribute at least 20% of the total project budget; this mandatory co-funding may be provided either in cash or in kind.

### Assessment

### Criteria

Applications submitted within this Call for Proposals will be assessed according to the following criteria:

- Criterion 1: Quality of the problem statement (20%),
- Criterion 2: Quality of the proposed research (30%),
- Criterion 3: Expected impact and route to impact (20%),
- Criterion 4: Quality of the consortium (30%).

### Procedure

An external, independent assessment committee will be set up for this Call for proposals, consisting of representatives from science and practice with knowledge of the field. The task of the assessment committee is to assess the submitted applications and related documents in relation to each other and on their own merits on the basis of the assessment criteria given in the Call for proposals.

Because of the expertise present in the assessment committee, the assessment procedure will be carried out without engaging referees.

The procedure consists of the following steps:

- optional matchmaking event,
- submission of the proposal,
- admissibility of the proposal,
- initial advice from the assessment committee, including the possibility for interview selection,
- interview,
- assessment committee meeting,
- decision-making.

### Write proposal

For this call, it is mandatory to prepare your proposal in English. To prepare your proposal, please go through the following steps:

- download the application form from the ISAAC online application system or from this website,
