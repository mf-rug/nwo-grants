# NWA Digital Technology and Work

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/nwa-digital-technology-and-work

This Call for proposals urges knowledge institutions, organisations and community stakeholders to jointly develop scientific and practice-oriented research proposals that focus on the theme of ‘Digital Technology and Work: Understanding the impact and deploying applications in a human-centric manner’. The consortia will work in a multidisciplinary and interdisciplinary way and consist of organisations from across the knowledge chain. In this context, ‘across the knowledge chain’ means that the programme seeks to connect and integrate fundamental, applied and practice-oriented research and responds to the knowledge needs of societal stakeholders.

### Purpose

This Call for proposals aims to develop new knowledge on the impact of digital technology on work and workers, and on the collaboration and interaction that emerges between humans, technology and organisations when technology is deployed in the workplace. Such knowledge will help respond to opportunities and prevent or mitigate negative consequences. The programme aims to develop knowledge and action perspectives for the benefit of policymakers and organisations that want to deploy digital technologies in a healthy, safe, human-centric and inclusive manner. For organisations, the programme will offer insight into the process of deploying digital technologies and their impact, as well as an understanding of opportunities for the continuous development of technology in order to improve the suitability of work (and work processes). For policymakers, the programme will yield knowledge to formulate policies that will discourage the undesirable implementation of technology and encourage the desirable and effective implementation of technology.

### Who can apply

An application must be submitted by the main applicant on behalf of the consortium. There are four categories of participants within a consortium:

1. Main applicant;
2. Co-applicant(s);
3. Cooperation partner(s);
4. Co-funder(s) (optional).
A consortium should consist of at least a main applicant, a co-applicant and cooperation partner. The conditions for each type of participant are explained in more detail in the Call for Proposals.

### Consortium building

Collaborative workshops aim to bring together ideas and stimulate collaboration and submission of a limited number of research proposals by broadly-based (and possibly innovative) consortiums. The workshops also offer participants insights into the balance of forces around a theme. The respective expertise of the participants and their contribution to the call’s theme constitute the substantive basis of the workshops. There are two consecutive workshops in which NWO actively supports mutual exploration. In the end, however, the parties themselves decide whether they want to initiate a collaboration and, if so, with what other parties. After the workshops, the participants will have gained an overview of the possibilities and opportunities of clustering and collaboration. They will then elaborate their proposals. Further information on the collaborative workshops will be published on the NWO call page.

Participation in **both** collaborative workshops is mandatory for main applicants.

- The collaborative workshops will be held on **22 April 2025** and**8 May 2025**.
- The deadline for registering for the collaborative workshops is **15 April 2025, before 14:00:00 CEST**.

### What to apply for

Per project, a grant amount of at least €2.750.000 and at most €2.865.000 can be applied for. The maximum duration of the proposed project is 72 months/ 6 years. The applicant and co-applicants can include costs for personnel, equipment, investments and knowledge utilisation. The available budget modules are listed in the Call for proposals. Apply only for funding that is vital to realise the project.

### When to apply

- The deadline for **registering for the collaborative workshops** is **15 April 2025, before 14:00:00 CEST**.
- The deadline for **submitting proposals** is **16 September 2025, before 14:00:00 CEST**.

### Assessment

### **Criteria**

The applications submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Problem definition and analysis (weight 20%),
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (weight 20%),
3. Quality of the consortium (weight 30%),
4. Quality of the research (weight 30%).

### **Procedure**

The deadline for submitting proposals is **16 September 2025, before 14:00:00 CEST**. For the assessment of the proposals, the committee will make use of pre-advices and interviews will be conducted. A final decision on funding by the NWO Executive Board is expected in December 2025.
