# Isaac Newton Group Telescopes 26b

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/isaac-newton-group-telescopes-26b

Astronomers in the Netherlands are invited to apply for observation time at the Isaac Newton Group of Telescopes on La Palma (Canary Islands, Spain).

## Characteristics

**Status:** Open calls Open for applications  
**Closing date full application:** 24 March 2026 14:00 hrs.  
**Research programme:** Observation time on the Isaac Newton Group of Telescopes  

## Explore

### Purpose

Astronomers can apply for observing time at the Isaac Newton Group of Telescopes (ING) on La Palma. The ING consists of the 4.2 m William Herschel Telescope (WHT) and the 2.5 m Isaac Newton Telescope (INT).

### Who can apply

Researchers in astronomy with a Dutch affiliation.

### What to apply for

- Dutch observing time at WHT for observations with WEAVE to be carried out between 1 November 2026 and 30 April 2027.
or
- Dutch observing time at INT for observations with HARPS3 to be carried out between 1 August 2026 and 31 January 2027.

### WEAVE

Proposals are invited for observations with WEAVE at WHT. In semester 2026B all three WEAVE observing modes are offered: MOS (multi-object spectroscopy), LIFU (large integral-field unit) and mini IFUs (mini integral-field units). MOS and mini-IFUs are available for the first time; mini-IFUs on a shared risk basis. Target of Opportunity and non sidereal observations can be applied for with WEAVE LIFU.

The effective available Dutch observing time for open time proposals with WEAVE is approximately 90 hours per semester.

### HARPS3

Proposals are invited for the HARPS3 spectrograph at INT. HARPS3 is offered for the first time and on a shared-risk basis. Please note that in this semester the polarimetric mode is not offered.

Approximately 14% of HARPS3 science time will be available for Dutch open-time proposals; 60% of HARPS3 science time will be devoted to the Terra Hunting Experiment (THE).

For more information, see [https://www.ing.iac.es/astronomy/instr.html](https://www.ing.iac.es/astronomy/instr.html) .

### When to apply

The next deadline is 24 March 2026. This is the deadline for:

- observing time with WEAVE@WHT to be carried out between 1 November 2026 and 30 April 2027.
- observing time with HARPS3@INT to be carried out between 1 August 2026 and 31 January 2027.
**Note**: The ING issues two calls for proposals per year. The associated deadlines for Dutch open time proposals are yearly expected in the second half of March and in the second half of September.

### Assessment

### **Criteria**

Proposals will be assessed by the ING Programme Committee of NWO. Because proposals for observing time come from many different fields of research, we recommend you to keep in mind that not all members of the Programme Committee are experts in your specific area of research. Please include the necessary background information and clearly formulate the relevance of your science question and how the requested data will contribute to answering this science question.

### **Procedure**

The Programme Committee of NWO assesses proposals for Dutch observing time. International proposals are assessed by a different committee.

In general the telescope organisation will inform you on the success of your proposal approximately three months after submission. Around the same time, you will also receive feedback from the Programme Committee.
