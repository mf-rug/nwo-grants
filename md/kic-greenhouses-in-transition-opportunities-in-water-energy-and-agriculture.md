# KIC Greenhouses in transition: opportunities in water, energy and agriculture

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/kic-greenhouses-in-transition-opportunities-in-water-energy-and-agriculture

The horticultural sector is subject to many changes. New innovations are needed to future-proof horticulture. Technological development must go hand in hand with trans- and interdisciplinary research that crosses sectors within the water-energy-food nexus to achieve major innovations. The KIC call Greenhouses in transition focuses on developing future-proof innovations in Controlled Environment Agriculture (CEA) within the Netherlands and the United States, also taking into account the ecological, social, societal and economic impact.

## Explore

### Purpose

The call Greenhouses in transition aims to accelerate the transition from Controlled Environment Agriculture (CEA) to a circular and sustainable industry. As CEA is strongly linked to the water-energy-food nexus, the intended transition must go beyond sector boundaries on a technological and societal level. A focus on exclusively technological innovation in one sector is therefore not enough for this call. Inter- and transdisciplinary research is needed to arrive at cross-sectoral solutions. A regional, social and economic perspective is therefore required in addition to a technical perspective.

### Who can apply

The full proposal in phase 2 is submitted by a consortium consisting of consortium partners based in the Netherlands and the United States, whereby the following conditions apply:

- the consortium exists of consortium partners who have been selected in phase 1 to submit an integrated full proposal;
- at least two main applicants, of which one NWO applicant NWO and one FFAR applicant (Dutch main applicant submits the application at NWO);
- at least one co-applicant at NWO and at least one cooperation partner and co-funder based in the Netherlands;
- at least one co-applicant with FFAR, and one cooperation partner and co-funder based in the United States;
- the consortium must be balanced in terms of participation consortium members who are based in the Netherlands and consortium members based in the United States.
**Applicants based in the Netherlands:**
Persons working at Dutch universities, KNAW and NWO institutes, and universities of applied sciences may act as applicants. Collaboration partners have an interest in the research results and can consist of governments, companies and other social parties. Cooperation partners cannot receive a subsidy for their own employees.

**Applicants based in the United States:** 
A researcher working in a permanent position in a recognized academic institution or a publicly funded R&D institution/laboratory based in the U.S.

See the call text for all terms and conditions.

### Assessment

## Criteria

The full proposal in phase 2 will be assessed against the following four criteria:

1. Problem definition and analysis (20%)
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%)
3. Quality of the consortium (30%)
4. Quality of the research (30%)
Each criterion consists of multiple sub-criteria which are listed in paragraph 4.3.1 of the call text.

### Write proposal

In fase 2 an integrated full proposal will be submitted by a joint consortium consisting of the sub-applicants which were selected in phase 1 to develop a full integrated proposal. This full proposal integrates the three themes of the call:

**Themes:**

- Theme 1: Coupling and designing technologies that bridge CEA, energy, water or urban planning sectors;
- Theme 2: Theme 2: Understanding societal complexity of embedding new cross-sectoral technologies/innovative approaches to enable sustainable social innovation
- Theme 3: Investigating the potential ecological effects of a new cross-sectoral technology/innovative approach
The full proposal includes an impact plan, research plan, information on the consortium and a detailed budget (separate forms are used for the FFAR subsidy budget and the NWO subsidy budget).
