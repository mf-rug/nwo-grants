# Weave with NWO as partner agency 2024/2025

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/weave-with-nwo-as-partner-agency-2024/2025

Part of Open Competition ENW-M. NWO acts as partner agency when a Weave-proposal with Dutch co-applicant is submitted to the Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft (DFG) or the Research Foundation – Flanders (FWO). Via this page the Dutch co-applicant submits the administrative submission to NWO.

## Explore

### Purpose

As part of the Open Competition ENW-M, researchers from the Netherlands can be involved in a bilateral research proposal that is submitted to DFG or FWO. Together with partners from Germany or Flanders they can submit a Weave proposal. This foreign research funder will act as lead agency and is responsible for the scientific evaluation. In such case, NWO will acts as partner agency. The bilateral application and the foreign researchers must meet the conditions of the funding instrument designated by DFG or FWO. The applicant from the Netherlands and the Dutch part of the requested budget must meet the conditions of the call OC ENW-M (the Dutch designated funding instrument).

Note! For Weave applications in which the Dutch applicant acts as main applicant, NWO will act as the lead agency. In such cases, the application should be [submitted as an ENW-M-1-WEAVE application](https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/open-competition-domain-science-m-2024/2025).

### Who can apply

Full, associate and assistant professors and other researchers with a comparable position may submit an Weave-application if they have a tenured position (and therefore a paid position for an indefinite period) or a tenure track agreement at one of the organisations mentioned in the Call for Proposals. Researchers with a one-year or a two-year employment contract who are expected to be offered a permanent employment contract after the initial one-year or two-year period has elapsed, are also eligible to submit a proposal.

An additional condition is a.o. that a main or co-applicant may only submit an ENW-M proposal, including a Weave proposal, regardless of whether NWO or DFG/FWO is lead agency, for substantive consideration once every twelve months.

For a description of this and other conditions, please refer to the Call for Proposals OC ENW-M, round 2024-2025 (Annex 7.3).

### What to apply for

The Dutch component of a Weave-application corresponds to the equivalent of an ENW-M-1 grant application. Hence, the budget requested from NWO has a maximum size of 400,000 euro and must be used for a single temporary scientific position in combination with material budget, a small investment (equipment) and/or non-scientific support, as needed. The modules Investment (between 150,000 and 500,000 euros) and Money follows Cooperation are not available. Apply only for funding that is vital to realise the project.

### When to apply

- **Applications with DFG as lead agency and NWO** as partner agency may be submitted to DFG at any time during the period from **6 August 2024 to 31 July 2025**.
- If a proposal is **submitted to DFG**, the **deadline** for submitting the administrative submission (see section 7.3.3) to NWO is**five working days after submission to DFG, at 14:00:00 CE(S)T**.
- The deadline for **submitting applications with FWO** as lead agency and NWO as partner agency is **1 April 2025, at 17:00:00 CEST**.
- If an application is**submitted to FWO**, the **deadline** for submitting the administrative submission (see section 7.3.3) to NWO is **five working days after submission to FWO, at 14:00:00 CE(S)T**.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The lead agency assessment procedure is followed, as set out in the designated Weave funding channels at the respective lead agencies. NWO has no further role in the assessment.

### Procedure

The foreign lead agency submits the advice of their assessment committee to the NWO domain Science board. The domain Science board decides whether to accept or reject the Dutch part of the proposal.

It follows from the agreed Weave principles that Weave applications submitted abroad must be among the top 20% of proposals to be eligible for allocation. Funding a Weave proposal is only possible if the foreign lead agency and NWO as partner agency reach the same decision. If the lead agency rejects the proposal, the domain Science board will also reject the proposal.

### More information

The Weave proposal must fit within the Science domain and hence needs to be submitted to one of the selected review boards (DFG)/expert panels (FWO). If in doubt about the suitability of the content, you are advised to contact the programme's contact person at NWO, DFG or FWO well before the deadline. This person can advise you on the substantive fit of your application to this call.

For more information about [Weave](https://www.nwo.nl/en/researchprogrammes/open-competition-enw/weave-research-funding-for-international-collaboration)

## Prepare

The administrative applications must be submitted through the ISAAC application and reporting system. If you do not have an ISAAC account yet, then please create one at least one workday in advance. More information about which forms you need to use can be found in ISAAC.

### Write proposal

The proposal should be drafted according to the terms and conditions of DFG or FWO, respectively.

- [DFG](http://www.dfg.de/nwo)
- [FWO](//www.fwo.be/en/support-programmes/all-calls/senior-researchersresearch-teams/weave-fwo-lead/)

### Fill in forms

The lead applicant from Germany or Flanders submits the joint proposal to DFG or FWO according to the terms and conditions of DFG or FWO. To prepare the administrative submission to NWO, the 
Dutch co-applicant goes through the following steps:

- submits the original application as submitted to DFG or FWO, saved as pdf, with the attachments required at NWO in ISAAC;
- enters data requested online in ISAAC.
