# Museum Grants

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/museum-grants-0

Museum employees may apply for funding for short-term in-depth scientific research related to the museum or its collection. The results should benefit museum research practice and must be included in an open access publication.

### Purpose

The Museum Grants programme funds research to promote talent development and scientific research in the museum field. Research results should directly benefit museum research practice. The programme focuses on personal development, which is made possible by creating space for research by museum employees who are motivated to conduct scientific research. This also contributes to scientific research into museum collections and the development of the field of museum studies. The programme is open to all museum employees who identify with this and who meet the requirements to apply. The programme is therefore also open to museum employees who are not curators. The Museum Grants programme not only strengthens the position of museological research within the scientific domain, but also promotes collaboration and knowledge sharing between museums and research institutions.

### Who can apply

Employees of a museum institution or the Netherlands Institute for Art History (RKD) may act as the main applicant. Each application must involve one researcher affiliated with a research organisation as a supervisor of the research.

The main applicant must meet the following requirements:

- have a permanent or temporary position at the museum from which the application is being made, at least for the duration of the research;
- hold a master's degree (HBO or WO).
The museum of the main applicant must be registered in the Museum Register, or be in the process of registration with the Museum Register, if the institution is located in the European part of the Netherlands. For museums in the Caribbean part of the Kingdom of the Netherlands, registration in the Museum Register does not apply, but the condition described in section 3.1.1.1 applies.

### What to apply for

Applicants may apply for a grant of 10.000 to 50.000 euros. The research project has a maximum duration of 18 months.

The museum finances at least 20% of the total project budget in cash or in kind.

### When to apply

The deadline for submitting an application is 12 May 2026, 14:00 CEST.

### Assessment

### Criteria

Applications submitted under this Call for Proposals will be assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Scientific quality (50%),
2. Relevance to society and museums (25%),
3. Quality of the applicant (25%).
The criteria are specified in the Call for Proposals, Section 4.3.

### Procedure

An external, independent review committee will be set up for this Call for Proposals. After you have submitted your proposal, NWO will determine whether it will be considered based on a number of administrative and technical criteria. Your proposal will then be submitted to several members of the review committee (the preliminary advisors) for their comments. The committee will weigh up the arguments of the preliminary advisers in a meeting. Following the discussion, the committee will draw up a written recommendation to the board of the NWO Social Sciences and Humanities domain on the quality and prioritisation of the proposals.

## Prepare

Applications must be submitted through the Isaac application and reporting system. If you do not have an Isaac account yet, then please create one at least one workday in advance. More information about writing a proposal and which forms you need to use can be found in Isaac. Please download and use the forms in Isaac. Below you will find examples of the required forms.

### Write proposal

To prepare your application, please follow these steps:

- Download the application form from the online application system ISAAC or from the NWO website (on the website of the relevant funding instrument);
- Complete the application form;
- Save the form as a PDF and submit it to ISAAC with the required attachments;
- Enter the information requested online in ISAAC.

### Fill in forms

To prepare your application, follow these steps:

- download the application form from the ISAAC online application system or from the NWO website (on the website of the relevant funding instrument).
- complete the application form;
- save the form as a PDF and submit it with the required attachments in ISAAC;
- enter the information requested online in ISAAC.
Obligatory attachments consist of:

- completed budget form;
- letter of recommendation from the supervising co-applicant;
- employer's statement;
- if applicable: statement of Algemene Voorwaarden Algemene Groepsvrijstellingsverordening (AGVV);
- if applicable: proof of involvement in the Museum Register registration process;
- if applicable: documentation supporting the assessment of the museum institution in the Caribbean Netherlands;
