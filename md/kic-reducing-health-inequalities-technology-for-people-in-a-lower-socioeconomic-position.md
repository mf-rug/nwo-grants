# KIC Reducing health inequalities – Technology for people in a lower socioeconomic position

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/kic-reducing-health-inequalities-technology-for-people-in-a-lower-socioeconomic-position

(Further) development of health technology with a clear strategy for structural, sustainable implementation for people in a lower socioeconomic position aiming to reduce health inequalities. Alpha, beta and gamma researchers from universities of applied sciences, universities and other knowledge institutions are invited to join forces with industry, healthcare organisations, insurers and other civil society organisations to submit a joint application.

### Purpose

Society is marked by large health inequalities among different groups of people, where lower socioeconomic position (SEP) limits the likelihood of good health. Health technology can help reduce these health inequalities Current technology products and services do not adequately address needs, desires and behaviors of people in lower SEP. They are often not integrated into everyday life or into the existing healthcare infrastructure. The (further) development of technology takes a long time and financing for companies is difficult. This Call for proposals therefore focuses on acquiring and putting into practice an understanding of associated technological as well as systemic preconditions to (further) develop health technology for groups in a lower SEP. It is crucial that all parties work together to develop and scale up health technology solutions in a sustainable way with a clear future perspective.

### Who can apply

Full proposals are submitted by the main applicant and two or more co-applicants. The main applicant has participated in both collaborative workshops (see also sections 3.3.1 and 4.2.1) A proposal is written by a consortium in which besides the applicants other participants are involved as well. Besides the main applicant and co-applicant(s) a consortium always consists of two or more co-funders, possibly supplemented by one or more collaborating partners.

For more information see sections 3.1 and 4.2.1 in the NWO Call for proposals

### Consortium building

To encourage consortium formation, two consecutive collaborative workshops will be organised. These are mandatory for the main applicant to attend before submitting an application. Registration for these workshops should be done via the form on the programme page.

For more information please refer to section 2.3.1 and 3.3.1 of the NWO Call for proposals.

### What to apply for

For an application in this Call for proposals, a minimum of €2.800.000 and aa maximum of €3.500.000 of NWO funding can be applied for in. With this, NWO finances maximally 80%/70% of the total project budget; the rest of the funding must be contributed by the compulsory co-funding (see Section 3.5.6). NWO never funds less than 50% of the total project budget.

The applicant and co-applicants can include costs for personnel, equipment, investments, knowledge utilisation and project management. The rates and an explanation of these budget modules are given in annex 7 of the NWO call for proposals. The maximum duration of the proposed project is 6 years.

### Assessment

### Criteria

1. Problem definition and analysis (20%)
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%)
3. Quality of the consortium (30%)
4. Quality of the research (30%)

### Procedure

After submission deadline the full proposals will be provided with feedback by (international) referees. Applicants will be given the opportunity to submit a rebuttal to this. The applications will then be assessed by the assessment committee and applicants will be given the opportunity to answer questions from the assessment committee during an interview.

The assessment committee will present its advice to the NWO Executive Board for decision-making. In July 2026, the NWO Executive Board will adopt the final assessment of the applications.

All the documents you need for your proposal can be found here. The proposal is developed after the collaborative workshops. The steps involved in writing your proposal are:

- download the proposal application form from the NWO web application ISAAC or from the NWO web page (on the grant page of the funding instrument concerned);
- complete the application form;
- save the application form in ISAAC as a PDF file and upload it with any compulsory annexes;
- fill in the requested information online in ISAAC.

### Mandatory consortium-building activities

To promote consortium formation collaborative workshops will be organised. The collaborative workshops aim to promote optimal networking around the topic and encourage collaboration. The expertise of the participants and their contributions to the call topic form the basis for the conversation during the workshops. The workshops provide an opportunity to combine ideas and form broad innovative consortia (joining forces). It is then up to participants to collaborate in interdisciplinary, public-private and innovative consortia and jointly develop the application. More information about the workshops will also be announced on the program page on NWO's website.

The main applicant must have participated in both workshops. The collaborative workshops are explicitly open to companies and civil society parties as well. In this way, they will also have the opportunity to join consortia in the making that develop during the workshops.

### Fill in forms

In addition to filling in the application form when submitting the complete application, we also ask you to include a signed statement from the co-financiers as well as a complete budget form. If applicable, a guarantee for continuity in the project management (see section 3.1 in the call for proposals) must also be sent.

At the time of submission, the enclosed letters of support must have pledged the full required co-financing in accordance with the conditions described in sections 3.1.2 and 3.5.6 in the call for proposals.

Other types of annexes shall are not allowed at the stage of submission of complete applications. Attachments must be uploaded separately from the application in ISAAC. All attachments, with the exception of the budget form, must be uploaded as a pdf file. The budget form can be uploaded as an Excel file in ISAAC.
