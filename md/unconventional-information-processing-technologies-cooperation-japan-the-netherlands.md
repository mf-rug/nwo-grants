# Unconventional information processing technologies: Cooperation Japan – The Netherlands

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/unconventional-information-processing-technologies-cooperation-japan-the-netherlands

This Call for proposals aims to strengthen the relationship between Japan and the Netherlands in the high-tech sector and stimulate the formation of transnational networks in the fields of semiconductors, photonics, quantum and neuromorphic technologies. This call focuses on the development of unconventional information technologies for the advancement of computer science.

### Purpose

The programme aims to advance the frontiers of computational science by exploring unconventional approaches to information processing. The programme seeks to:

- Foster innovation in computational technologies that address complex global challenges,
- Strengthen research networks between the Netherlands and Japan, promote international talent mobility, and train early-career researchers who may become future leaders by connecting top researchers,
- Deliver societal and scientific impact by translating unconventional methods into practical solutions.
Example of scientific research directions are:

- Quantum information processing: Investigating new methods and applications for quantum algorithms and systems,
- Neuromorphic computing: Developing systems that emulate neural architectures for energy-efficient and scalable computation,
- Bio-inspired algorithms: Drawing inspiration from biological processes to design novel computational paradigms,
- Integrated approaches: Combining interdisciplinary insights to tackle challenges in data processing, communication, and machine intelligence.

### Who can apply

Proposals are prepared by a consortium consisting of at least one Japan-based applicant and one Netherlands-based applicant. The consortium works on one joint proposal, which the Netherlands-based applicant submits to the NWO web application ISAAC and the Japan-based main applicant submits to JST via e-Rad.

Five categories of consortium participants are distinguished:

1. Main applicant,
2. Co-applicants,
3. Research participants (Japan side only),
4. Cooperation partners,
5. Co-funders.
The terms and conditions for each participant are detailed in the Call for proposals.

### Consortium building

Consortium buildingApplications should be characterised by equitable partnership and sustainable collaboration among the partners in the consortium and with relevant stakeholders. Evidence of such active engagement will be an element in the assessment of project proposals. This includes inter-institutional cooperation, a balanced contribution to the proposed research, and frequent exchange between the partners, including exchange visits by both senior and junior researchers. The number of (co)applicants in both countries should be roughly equal. The workload on both sides should be equally balanced.

### What to apply for

Per project, the Netherlands-based applicant can apply for a grant amount of at least €800,000 and at most €1,150,000 from NWO. The Japan-based applicant can apply for a grant amount of at most 250,000,000 YP from JST. The main applicant and co-applicant(s) may apply for budget for personnel, material, knowledge utilization, and project management.

To strengthen the transnational networks between the Netherlands and Japan, all early-career researchers working on the project (PhDs and postdocs) are required to spend at least 20% of their total employment time on the project in Japan for an extended research stay. Appropriate budget needs to be reserved to fund these extended research stays.

The minimum duration of the proposed project is 48 months/ 4 years. The maximum duration of the project is 60 months/5 years.

The conditions on the budget are further explained in the Call for proposals.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Quality and relevance of the research content and plan,
2. Quality of the consortium,
3. Quality of stimulating researcher mobility and international collaborations,
4. Scientific and/or societal impact.
Criteria 1 and 3 each carry a weight of 30% and criteria 2 and 4 each carry a weight of 20%.
The assessment criteria are further operationalised in the Call for proposals.

### Procedure

An external, independent assessment committee will be assigned for this Call for proposals, consisting of representatives from science with knowledge of the field.
The task of the assessment committee is to assess the applications and the relevant documents that are submitted, in conjunction with each other and with regard to both the respective merits of each application and the assessment criteria outlined in this Call for proposals (see section 4.3.1.).

The Executive Board of NWO will decide on the submitted proposals.

## Prepare

Applications must be submitted through the NWO Isaac application and reporting system by the Netherlands-based main applicant, as well as through the JST application system e-Rad by the Japan-based main applicant. If you do not have an Isaac account yet, then please create one at least one workday in advance. Please ensure that new organisations that are not yet registered in Isaac are added to the system in time. More information about writing a proposal and which forms you need to use can be found in Isaac. Please download and use the forms in Isaac. Below you will find examples of the required forms.

### Write proposals

Aanvraag bij NWO

The Netherlands-based and Japan-based teams must submit their applications by parallel submission, using the designated application form to draft a joint proposal in English and submitting it to the application websites of their respective countries.
Application to NWO
The steps involved in writing your application are:

- Download the form for the declaration of intent from the website and fill it in (not compulsory). Send this form to [aspire-nl@jst.go.jp](mailto:aspire-nl@jst.go.jp) and [nwo-jst-joint-call@nwo.nl](mailto: nwo-jst-joint-call@nwo.nl),
- download the application form from the NWO web application ISAAC or from this NWO web page,
- complete the application form (adhere to the questions and word/page limits stated in the form),
- save the application form in ISAAC as a PDF file and upload it with any compulsory annexes,
- fill in the requested information online in ISAAC.
Compulsory annexes:

- budget form,
- statement by the applicant,
- in case of co-funding; a declaration from the co-funder,
- in case it is needed (as specified in the Call for proposals): embedding guarantee.

### Fill in forms

The steps for writing and submitting your application are:

- download the application form from the NWO web application ISAAC or from this NWO web page,
- complete the application form,
- save the application form in ISAAC as a PDF file and upload it with any compulsory annexes,
- fill in the requested information online in ISAAC.
Compulsary annexes:

- Budget form,
- Declaration of the applicant,
- In case of co-funding; A statement from the co-funder,
- In case it is needed (as specified in the Call for proposals): an embedding guarantee.
Annexes should be prepared in accordance with the template provided by NWO. Annexes should be uploaded separately from the application in ISAAC. All annexes, except the budget form, should be submitted as a pdf file (without security). The budget form should be submitted as an Excel file in ISAAC. Attachments other than those listed above are not allowed.

- It is mandatory to prepare your application in English,
- Submitting an application can only be done through the online application system ISAAC. Applications not submitted through ISAAC will not be considered,
- As the lead applicant, you are required to submit an application through your own personal ISAAC account.
It is important to begin your application in ISAAC in a timely manner:

- if you do not already have an ISAAC account, it should be created in time to avoid any application problems,
- new organizations may still need to be added to IAAC by NWO,
- You still need to enter data online.
