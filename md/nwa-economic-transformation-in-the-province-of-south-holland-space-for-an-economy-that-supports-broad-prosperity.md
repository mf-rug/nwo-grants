# NWA - Economic transformation in the Province of South Holland: space for an economy that supports broad prosperity

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/nwa-economic-transformation-in-the-province-of-south-holland-space-for-an-economy-that-supports-broad-prosperity

The province of South Holland is a highly innovative European region, but its growth rate lags behind similar areas, and not everyone benefits in terms of broad prosperity. At the same time, the province is facing challenges due to limited space, which necessitates careful consideration of economic, social and ecological interests. The purpose of this call is to develop integrated insights and concrete perspectives for action. The objective is to achieve a sustainable, future-proof and competitive economy in South Holland.

## Explore

### Purpose

In the province of South Holland is a need for insight and frameworks for action to accommodate economic structural change. Despite the province's status as one of Europe's most innovative economies, its growth rate lags behind similar areas. Moreover, not everyone benefits from the (broad) prosperity, and the distribution of scarce space in the province leads to complex trade-offs between economic, social and ecological interests. In order to work towards a sustainable, future-proof and competitive economy, South Holland faces the challenge of making tough choices at the intersection of economy, space and broad prosperity. The purpose of this call is to take an integrated approach to the issues and come to an actionable perspective on transformation that is widely supported.

### Who can apply

The consortium should consist of at least one main applicant, one co-applicant and one collaboration partner.

**Main applicant**
Researchers may act as main applicants if they are permanently employed (and therefore have a paid employment contract for an indefinite period of time*) or have a tenure track agreement with one of the research organisations listed in the Call for proposals.

**Co-applicants**
Two types of co-applicants are possible in this Call for proposals:

1. Co-applicants affiliated to research organisations;
2. Co-applicants affiliated to civil society organisations. Co-applicants linked to civil society organisations can only act as co-applicants under application of the de minimis regulation (see the Call for proposals, section 3.2).
**Cooperation partners**
Cooperation partners are mandatory as partners in the consortium within this Call for proposals, because active involvement of civil society stakeholders is of great importance in developing knowledge about challenges and possible solutions. Societal stakeholders include both public and private organisations, and where relevant also citizens or a representation thereof. The organisation of a societal stakeholder is not a main or co-applicant or co-financier.

**Co-funders**
Co-funding is possible but not mandatory within this Call for proposals. Co-financiers are organisations that participate in the consortium and contribute cash and/or in kind to the project.

**Assessment of research organisations**

In this Call, it is possible for atypical research organisations to participate as main applicants (for national knowledge institutions ’RKI’s’) or co-applicants (for RKI’s and other research organisations). An assessment is mandatory for this. More information can be found in the Call for proposals, section 3.2.

### Consortium building

This Call for proposals deals with complex and challenging societal issues. Research into answers to these issues requires collaboration, for example between different research disciplines, practical and fundamental research, and with civil society organisations and companies. NWO encourages and supports the formation of these collaborations by organising consortium formation meetings or workshops. In this Call for proposals, consortium formation is stimulated by organising a Sandpit.

The aim of a Sandpit is to establish cooperation in new collaborations and joint research proposals. Sandpits focus on collaboration in a single consortium consisting of the selected parties. During a Sandpit, the selected participants work jointly on site to develop a research project, under the guidance of external facilitators and content supporters.

For participation in a Sandpit, selection takes place through an Expression of Interest. Only individuals invited by the NWO Executive Board can participate in the Sandpit.

### What to apply for

Within this programme, a maximum of €2,800,000 of NWO grant can be requested for the proposal.

The maximum duration of the proposed project is 5 years. The applicant and co-applicants can include costs for personnel, equipment, investments and knowledge utilisation.

The available budget modules (including the maximum amounts) are listed in the Call for proposals, section 3.3. The rates and an explanation of the budget modules can be found in section 7.1.

### Assessment

### Criteria

**Substantive assessment criteria for Expressions of Interest**

Participants (researchers, civil society actors and companies) are subject to the evaluation criteria as specified below:

1. Scientific/ professional expertise (50%)
2. Cooperation skills (50%)
**Substantive assessment criteria Proposal**

The application submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Problem definition and analysis (20%),
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%),
3. Quality of the consortium (30%),
4. Quality of the research (30%).
More information on the substantive assessment criteria can be found in the Call for proposals.

**Assessment**

For this NWA Call for proposals, an external, independent assessment committee will be appointed by the NWO Executive Board. Its members will be selected from the scientific community and the entire knowledge chain, including societal stakeholders and representatives from the target group, with expertise in the field. The assessment committee's task is to assess the Expressions of Interest and the proposal submitted on the basis of the assessment criteria. Each Expression of Interest and the proposal will be assessed on its own merits.

### Procedure

The Sandpit method is applied in this Call for Proposals. To this end, interested individuals must submit an Expression of Interest. The Expressions of Interest will be assessed by the assessment committee, after which a decision on participation in the Sandpit will be taken by the NWO Executive Board. During the Sandpit, the participants will form a consortium and lay the foundations for the proposal. The proposal will be submitted to NWO and assessed by the assessment committee. An interview is part of the procedure.

### Write proposal

**Expression of Interest**

In an Expression of Interest, a potential participant in the Sandpit provides a concise description of their scientific/professional expertise and their competencies relevant to the Sandpit and the theme of the Call for Proposals. It is not required to formulate a research idea at this stage. The Expression of Interest will be assessed by the assessment committee.

**Proposal**

In the application, the consortium formulates the research proposal and the expected impact/route to impact using the Impact Plan Approach.

### Mandatory consortium-building activities

This programme includes a Sandpit workshop. The aim of the Sandpit is to establish a new partnership, resulting in a single joint proposal. The Sandpit emphasises collaboration in a consortium to be formed, consisting of participants selected on the basis of the Expressions of Interest submitted (see section 4.1). During the Sandpit, the participants work together on site to develop the application, under the guidance of facilitators and content support staff hired by NWO. Participation is mandatory for the main applicant of the proposal.
