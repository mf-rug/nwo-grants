# NWA Slavery pasts: (understudied forms of) impacts and perspectives

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/nwa-slavery-pasts-understudied-forms-of-impacts-and-perspectives

The influence of the Dutch slavery and colonial past is still noticeable in our society. More knowledge about it is needed to better understand its lasting and often profound effects. The NWA Call ‘Slavery pasts: (understudied forms of) impacts and perspectives’ aims to give impetus to scientific and applied research into underexposed repercussions of the Dutch colonial slavery past.

## Explore

### Purpose

The Call 'NWA Slavery pasts: (understudied forms of) impacts and perspectives’ aims to stimulate research into the often less-known consequences of the Dutch history of slavery. The programme seeks to contribute to the debate on how societies address injustices of the past and work towards a fairer future. It also aims to promote the development, sharing, and preservation of knowledge on this subject within the Kingdom of the Netherlands.

The research focuses on the economic, social, cultural, and administrative impacts of slavery over the years. Additionally, it examines how societies today deal with this past and its lasting effects. The programme emphasizes an open approach that allows for diverse perspectives. This includes the use of new and innovative research methods and disciplines to gain deeper insights into these complex issues.

### Who can apply

The consortium should consist of at least one lead applicant, co-applicant and collaboration partner.

**Main applicant**
Researchers may act as main applicants if they are permanently employed (and therefore have a paid employment contract for an indefinite period of time*) or have a tenure track agreement with one of the research organisations listed in the Call for proposals.

**Co-applicants**
Two types of co-applicants are possible in this Call for proposals:

1. Co-applicants affiliated to research organisations;
2. Co-applicants affiliated to civil society organisations. Co-applicants linked to civil society organisations can only act as co-applicants under application of the de minimis regulation (see the Call for proposals, section 3.1.1).
**Cooperation partners**
Cooperation partners are mandatory within this Call for proposals because active involvement of civil society stakeholders is of great importance in developing knowledge about challenges and possible solutions. Societal stakeholders include both public and private organisations, and where relevant also citizens or a representation thereof. The organisation of a societal stakeholder is not a main or co-applicant or co-financier.

**Co-funders**
Co-financing is not mandatory within this Call for proposals. Co-financiers are organisations that participate in the consortium and contribute cash and/or in kind to the project.

### Consortium building

This Call for proposals deals with complex and challenging societal issues. Research into answers to these issues requires collaboration, for example between different research disciplines, practical and fundamental research, and with civil society organisations and companies. NWO encourages and supports the formation of these collaborations by organising consortium formation meetings or workshops. These meetings and workshops can take different forms, depending on the specific objective, and are further explained in this Call for proposals.

In this Call for proposals, consortium formation is stimulated by deploying a Sandpit.

The aim of a Sandpit is to establish cooperation in new collaborations and joint research proposals. Sandpits focus on collaboration in a single consortium consisting of the selected parties. During a Sandpit, the selected participants work jointly on site to develop a research project, under the guidance of external facilitators and content supporters.

For participation in a Sandpit, selection takes place through an Expression of Interest. Only individuals invited by the NWO Executive Board can participate in the Sandpit.

### What to apply for

Within this programme, a maximum of €2,750,000 grant can be applied for one application.

The maximum duration of the proposed project is 5 years. The applicant and co-applicants can include costs for personnel, equipment, investments and knowledge utilisation.

The available budget modules (including the maximum amounts) are listed in the Call for proposals. The rates and an explanation of these budget modules can be found in section 7.1.

### Assessment

### Criteria

##### **Substantive assessment criteria for Expressions of Interest**

Participants (researchers, civil society actors and companies) are subject to the evaluation criteria as specified below:

1. Academic/professional attributes (40%),
2. Personal competences and motivation (60%).

##### Substantive assessment criteria Proposal

The application submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Problem definition and analysis (20%),
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%),
3. Quality of the consortium (30%),
4. Quality of the research (30%).
More information on the substantive assessment criteria can be found in the Call for proposals.

### Procedure

For this NWA Call for proposals, an assessment committee will be appointed by the NWO Executive Board. The assessment committee will have a broad composition. This means that not only scientific expertise will be represented in the assessment committee, but also expertise from the entire knowledge chain, including societal stakeholders who are highly familiar with the subject and representatives from the target group. The task of the assessment committee is to assess the applications and the relevant documents that are submitted, in conjunction with each other and with regard to both the respective merits of each application and the assessment criteria outlined in this Call for proposals.
