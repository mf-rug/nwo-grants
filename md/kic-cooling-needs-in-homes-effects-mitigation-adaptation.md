# KIC - Cooling needs in homes: effects, mitigation, adaptation

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/kic-cooling-needs-in-homes-effects-mitigation-adaptation

Climate change and urbanisation are causing increased indoor temperatures, which in turn increases cooling demand in homes. In this KIC programme, NWO encourages interdisciplinary research into local heat differences, the consequences of overheating and the increasing need for cooling in different types of homes. The goal of this call is to reduce this cooling demand and to meet the remaining demand to achieve a healthy and comfortable indoor climate.

## Explore

### Purpose

This Call for proposals addresses the current and future cooling demand in existing Dutch homes. The focus is on mapping this demand at the local level, identifying scalable, cost- and energy-efficient, and sustainable solutions to reduce cooling demand, and meeting the remaining demand. In doing so, the programme contributes to a comfortable and healthy indoor climate in homes. Research is needed into local heat variations, the effects of overheating, residents’ behaviour and differing cooling needs as well as the rising cooling demand across different housing types. To this end, sustainable heat mitigation and adaptation strategies for homes must be further developed and refined. An integrated approach is required, with the focus on residents, houses and the immediate surroundings. Researchers are invited to submit applications in collaboration with researchers from relevant disciplines and with relevant public and private parties.

### Who can apply

The main applicant and co-applicant(s) must carry out the requested research within a consortium that always includes two or more co-financiers in addition to themselves, possibly supplemented by one or more collaboration partners. All participants in the consortium must play an active role in formulating the research questions and in the design and implementation of the project. Professors, university (senior) lecturers, lecturers and other researchers with a comparable position may act as main applicant or co-applicant. For the list of eligible organisations and conditions for employment, see section 3.1 of the Call for proposals.

### Consortium building

To maximise the potential impact of the research, collaboration is required between researchers across disciplines combined with societal partners such as companies and civil society organisations. Within this Call for proposals, NWO supports consortium building by organising a matchmaking event, which takes place at the 25th of March 2026. It is encouraged to participate in the matchmaking event, however, this is not mandatory.

### What to apply for

The total budget for this Call for Proposals is € 7.000.000. For an application, a minimum of € 600.000 and a maximum of € 2.300.000 of NWO funding can be applied for. With this, NWO finances maximally 80% of the total project budget; the rest of the funding must be contributed by the compulsory co-funding (see Section 3.5.6). NWO never funds less than 50% of the total project budget. In addition, the proposed project has a duration of at most 6 years.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Problem definition and analysis (20%);
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%);
3. Quality of the consortium (30%);
4. Quality of the research (30%).
More information about the assessment criteria can be found in paragraph 4.3 in the Call for proposals.

### Procedure

The application procedure consists of the following steps:

- information session (optional);
- matchmaking (optional);
- submission of the proposal;
- admissibility of the proposal;
- peer review by referees;
- rebuttal;
- pre-advice assessment committee;
- interview selection;
- interview;
- assessment committee meeting;
- decision-making;
- An external, independent assessment committee will be assigned for this Call for proposals, consisting of representatives from the research field and society with knowledge of (partial aspects of) the topic.

### Write proposal

NWO will assess your application on all the conditions set out in this Call for proposals, including the conditions listed below. Your application will only be admitted to the assessment procedure if it meets these conditions. After submitting your application, NWO requests you to be available to implement any possible administrative corrections so that you can (still) meet the conditions for submission.

**These conditions are:**

- the main applicant and co-applicant(s) meet the conditions stated in Section 3.1 and Section 7.1.1
- the applying consortium contains two or more co-funders
- the application complies with the DORA guidelines as described in Section 4.1;
- the application is submitted via the main applicant’s ISAAC account;
- the application is received before the deadline;
- the application is written in English;
- the proposed project has a duration of at most 6 years;
- the budget in the application has been prepared according to the terms of this Call for proposals (using the format made available which includes the most recent rates);
- the required co-financing has been correctly and fully committed through co-financing statements (see sections 3.1.2 and 3.5.6);
- all of the required annexes and forms are, after a possible request to make additions or changes, have been completed and submitted completely and according to the instructions and in accordance with the terms of this Call for proposals.
