# KIC Adaptivity in transitions

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/kic-adaptivity-in-transitions

This call invites researchers, societal organizations, and companies to form a consortium to conduct research into increasing the adaptivity of actors and systems in transitions of the food system, toward sustainable mobility, and of the energy system in the built environment.

### Purpose

The objective of this Call for proposals is to develop insights, action perspectives and capacity for action that increase the adaptivity of systems and actors within transitions. Three transition contexts are linked to this central objective of the Call for proposals. Proposed projects must focus on at least one of these transition contexts; the focus may also be on *a specific component of a transition*context and/or on *intersections between these transitions*. Consortia must address both systemic adaptivity and actor-adaptivity, ensuring that actor-adaptivity is considered *in connection with system-level dynamics*. Consortia are also asked to clearly conceptualise and operationalise their approach to adaptivity, which underpins the proposed project.

### Who can apply

Full, associate and assistant professors, lectors and other researchers with a comparable position may act as main or co-applicants. Eligible organisations and other conditions can be found in paragraph 3.1.1 of the Call for proposals.

The main applicant and co-applicant(s) must carry out the proposed research within a consortium that always includes two or more co-funders in addition to themselves, possibly supplemented by one or more collaboration partners. All participants in the consortium must play an active role in formulating the research questions and in the design and implementation of the project. The main applicant (or their substitute) must have participated in both collaborative workshops (see also sections 3.3.1 and 4.2.2 of the Call for Proposals).

### Consortium building

To increase the likelihood of research impact, collaboration between researchers, between researchers from other disciplines, and with societal partners such as businesses and societal organisations, is essential. In this Call for proposals, NWO supports the formation of a consortium by organising a collaborative workshop. A brief description of this method can be found below. More information on consortium formation is available on the NWO website.

Participation in the collaborative workshops is mandatory for principal applicants (see also section 3.1 of the Call for Proposals).

### What to apply for

For an application in this Call for proposals, a minimum of €1,500,000 and a maximum of €2,000,000 of NWO funding can be applied for in. With this, NWO finances maximally 80% of the total project budget; the rest of the funding must be contributed by the compulsory co-funding (see Section 3.5.6). NWO never funds less than 50% of the total project budget.

The maximum duration of the proposed project is 6 years.

Further specific information can be found in Chapter 3 of the Call for proposals.

### Assessment

### Criteria

Proposals will be assessed by the following four criteria:

1. Problem definition and analysis (20%)
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%)
3. Quality of the consortium (30%)
4. Quality of the research (30%)
More information about the assessment criteria and sub-criteria can be found in paragraph 4.3 in the Call for proposals.

### Mandatory consortium-building activities

The objective of the collaborative workshops is to bring together potential applicants and their ideas, to encourage collaboration within broad, innovative consortia, and to limit the number of proposals. The programme of the workshops will be designed according to the theme(s) of the Call for proposals, the expertise of the participants and the expected contribution to impact. Two collaborative workshops will be organised. Although NWO supports mutual exploration, the parties ultimately decide for themselves whether and with whom they want to collaborate. During the workshops, ideas are exchanged, cooperation in consortia with other workshop participants is explored, and initial arrangements for the joint development of the proposal are made. After the workshops, the consortia further develop the proposals.
