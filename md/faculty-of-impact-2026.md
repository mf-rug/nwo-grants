# Faculty of Impact 2026

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/faculty-of-impact-2026

The Faculty of Impact programme is aimed at researchers who want to achieve impact with their research and are interested in developing entrepreneurial skills to set up their own business. These researchers can now submit a grant application to become a fellow at the Faculty of Impact. A maximum of €210,000 is available per application. The duration of this programme is two years.

## Explore

### Purpose

The aim of the Faculty of Impact is to offer talented researchers in all disciplines the opportunity to combine entrepreneurship with science. These researchers should have an innovative idea that can potentially create an impact on society. This can be a tool to stimulate behavioural change, a technological innovation, a new business model, et cetera. At this stage, however, there is no question of a functional product, service or policy, or of a commercial company. The idea must, however, have been validated by a pilot in a laboratory setting (corresponding to a Technology Readiness Level (TRL) of approx. 4) or by relevant stakeholders (corresponding to a Society Readiness Level (SRL) of approx. 4)[[1]](#_ftn1).

The fellows will be given the opportunity during a two-year support programme to further develop and scale up their ideas.

### Who can apply

- PhD, Engineering Doctorate (EngD) and Professional Doctorate (PD) students and Postdocs who have completed their PhD research, EngD programme, PD track or ongoing Postdoc project before the FoI programme start date (see section 4.2.8 for the start date).
or

- Researchers with positions of university (senior) lecturer, professor or other researchers with independent research experience.

### What to apply for

A maximum of € 210,000 subsidy can be applied for per project. The duration of the project is 24 months. The available budget modules are:

- Personnel: the applicant can declare own salary costs for 0.8 – 1.0 FTE.
- Material: financing can be requested for project-specific material costs. A maximum of €15,000 applies to these costs.
The explanation of the available budget modules, including rates, can be found in section 6.4 of the Call for proposals.

### When to apply

- The deadline for submitting full proposals is Tuesday 26 May 2026, before 14:00:00 CEST.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantively assessed based on the following criteria:

- Quality of the idea;
- Social impact for the chosen theme;
- Quality of the applicant.
Each of the three criteria is given equal weight and is given a score between 1 (excellent) and 9 (very poor). 
The quality requirement is then applied in this way: fellows are only eligible for funding when their score does not exceed 5.4 (good) for each of the criteria and the average of all criteria does not exceed 4.0 (very good).

### Procedure

The application procedure consists of the following steps:

- submission of the statement of intent;
- admissibility of the proposal;
- interview selection (*optional*);
- interview;
- assessment committee meeting;
- decision-making.
An external, independent assessment committee will be assigned for this Call for proposals, consisting of representatives from science and practice with knowledge of valorisation and entrepreneurship. Note: do not assume the assessment committee members to be experts in the scientific field of the applicant.

## Prepare

Applications must be submitted through the Isaac application and reporting system. If you do not have an Isaac account yet, then please create one at least one workday in advance. More information about writing a proposal and which forms you need to use can be found in Isaac. Please download and use the forms in Isaac. Below you will find examples of the required forms.

### Write proposal

In the application form, the applicant must describe his/her idea and its social impact on the chosen theme. In addition, the applicant must motivate why he or she wants to participate in the Faculty of Impact program.

### Fill in forms

In addition to a written application, you must also include a link to a downloadable SURFDrive of SURFFilesender video link containing the short video. 
The video must not be longer than 3 minutes. There is no need to hire professional equipment or help. The video is judged on its content, not its quality. A quality equivalent to the HD quality of a mobile phone is acceptable. The video must be recorded in English. Keep a broad audience in mind: your well-educated neighbours should be able to understand your story. Check whether audio files or visual materials used are royalty-free.

In addition, the applicant must upload four mandatory attachments to ISAAC in addition to the application itself:

- Budget;
- Letter of support from the applicant's supervisor;
- Letter of support from the organization's Knowledge Transfer Office;
- Letter of support from the department director.
