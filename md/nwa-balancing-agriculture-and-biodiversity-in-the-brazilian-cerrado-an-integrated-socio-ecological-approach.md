# NWA Balancing agriculture and biodiversity in the Brazilian Cerrado: an integrated socio-ecological approach

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/nwa-balancing-agriculture-and-biodiversity-in-the-brazilian-cerrado-an-integrated-socio-ecological-approach

With this Call for proposals, NWO and CNPq invite Dutch and Brazilian researchers and societal partners to form a consortium to conduct research into the balance between agriculture and biodiversity in the Brazilian Cerrado from a socio-ecological point of view. The biodiversity of the Cerrado is threatened by increasing deforestation for agricultural production. Which scenarios and practical solutions can safeguard agricultural production in the Cerrado while protecting and promoting biodiversity and mitigating climate change? That is the central question of this new Call within the Dutch Research Agenda (NWA).

### Purpose

Increasing deforestation rates driven by agricultural expansion and land use change are threatening the rich biodiversity of the Brazilian Cerrado, one of the world’s biodiversity hotspots. The Netherlands is a large importer of agricultural commodities from the Cerrado, while Brazilian climate and environmental policies and regulations are increasingly aimed at safeguarding the region’s rich biome. The aim of this Call for proposals is to develop strategies for a balanced approach towards conserving the biodiversity of the Cerrado, mitigating climate change and ensuring social justice, while at the same time driving food and nutrition security and economic development by safeguarding agricultural productivity. The Matopiba region in the northern part of the Cerrado, and its border areas with the Amazon, are the principal geographical focus of this Call for proposals.

In this Call, NWO and CNPq invite knowledge institutions and societal partners to form consortia that jointly develop scientific and practice-oriented research proposals. The consortia are required to work in an inter and transdisciplinary manner and should have a knowledge-chain-wide composition. Knowledge-chain-wide implies that proposals connect fundamental, applied and practice-oriented research and that connection is sought with the knowledge needs of societal parties.

The programme focuses on four themes, two of which should be addressed in the application:

- land-use design scenarios which support biodiversity and ecosystem services;
- social, economic and environmental impacts of land-use redesign strategies;
- developing agricultural systems more resilient to climate change while protecting biodiversity;
- co-creating governance approaches to support more sustainable land-use practices.

### Who can apply

A proposal must be submitted by a research consortium with participants from the Netherlands and Brazil who jointly develop the proposal. On both the Dutch and Brazilian sides, the consortium must consist of a main applicant and a co-applicant. On the Dutch side, the co-applicant must be affiliated with a different organisation than that of the main applicant. Furthermore, the consortium must include a cooperation partner from a practitioner organisation, such as a business, (local) government, or non-governmental organisation, on either the Brazilian or Dutch side.

For Dutch applicants, full, associate and assistant professors and other researchers with a comparable position may submit an application (i.e. participate in a consortium and request NWO funding) if they have a tenured position (and therefore a paid position for an indefinite period) or a tenure track agreement at one of the institutions listed in the Funding Organisation rules (NWO part) of the call for proposals.

Persons with a zero-hour employment agreement or with a contract for a limited period of time (other than a tenure track appointment) may not submit a proposal.

Researchers based in Brazil may be main applicants if they have their curriculum registered on the Lattes Platform, updated until the deadline for proposal submission and hold a doctoral degree. Applicants also need an employment contract (CLT) or statutory bond with the project executing institution or, if retired, prove that they maintain academic-scientific activity and present a declaration from the project executing institution agreeing with its execution.

Each main applicant and consortium can only submit one proposal. A main applicant cannot participate as a co-applicant in other proposals. A co-applicant can participate in a maximum of two proposals.

More information on the transnational eligibility criteria, can be found in the full Call for Proposals.

### Consortium building

NWO organises an online information and matchmaking event. The aim of this event is to provide information about the Call and application process, and bring together potential applicants, researchers, cooperation partners and stakeholders from various relevant fields and sectors. In addition, ideas for (or needs for) research related to the Call theme and the described impact can be exchanged. In this way, additional expertise needed for the consortium can be identified.

During the information and matchmaking event, participants are offered the opportunity to pitch their ideas and to network with other participants, in order to achieve the necessary breadth and higher quality of consortia and project proposals. It is up to the participants themselves to shape the follow-up to the results of the matchmaking. The matchmaking session will take place once.

Further information about the information and matchmaking meeting will be published on the programme page of this Call for proposals. Participation in the matchmaking is not mandatory.

### What to apply for

Per project, a grant amount of at most € 715,000 can be applied for from NWO and a maximum of R$ 1,000,000 can be applied for from CNPq.

The maximum duration of the proposed project is 4 years (i.e. 48 months). Reimbursable costs from NWO may include costs for personnel, equipment, investments, knowledge utilisation and project management. More detailed budget modules can be found in section 3.2.1 and Annex 7.1 of the Call for proposals.

70% of the CNPq budget of each project is allocated to the following scholarships modalities: Technological Development and Innovation in Brazil (DTI) and Abroad (DEJ and DES) from 1 to 12 months of duration each. The DEJ and DES are exclusively intended for Brazilian consortium members traveling to the Netherlands. A maximum of 30% of the CNPq budget can be allocated to Other Costs (please refer to section 3.2.1 of the Call for proposals for more information).

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following substantive assessment criteria:

1. Problem definition and analysis (20%)
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%)
3. Quality of the consortium (30%)
4. Quality of the research (30%)

### Procedure

Applications should be submitted to NWO (via [ISAAC](https://isaac.nwo.nl/auth/realms/ISAAC/login-actions/authenticate?client_id=isaac-webapp-client&tab_id=T6coQVPn07I)) only and the procedure is carried out through the NWO application system ISAAC.

The NWO main applicant and the CNPq main applicant are both responsible for sharing the reviewer reports, information regarding the rebuttal and any further information with the rest of the consortium.

The application procedure consists of the following steps:

- Submission of the proposal;
- Admissibility of the proposal;
- Peer review by referees;
- Rebuttal;
- Pre-advice of the International Assessment Committee (IAC);
- Meeting of the International Assessment Committee (IAC);
- Decision-making by the NWO Executive Board and the Executive Board of CNPq.
For this Call for proposals, an external IAC will be jointly appointed by NWO and CNPq. The assessment committee will have a broad composition. This means that not only scientific expertise will be represented in the assessment committee, but also expertise from the entire knowledge chain, including societal stakeholders who are highly familiar with the subject and representatives from the target group. The task of the assessment committee is to assess the applications and the relevant documents that are submitted, in conjunction with each other and with regard to both the respective merits of each application and the assessment criteria outlined in this Call for proposals.

Finally, the NWO Executive Board and the Executive Board of CNPq will assess the procedure followed as well as the advice from the assessment committee. They will subsequently determine the final qualifications and make a decision over awarding or rejecting the proposals. The decision is final when both organisations have come to the same decision.

The Brazilian main applicants (project leaders) of the awarded projects will be contacted by CNPq in order to take the procedures needed for contracting the project. That will include the submission of the proposal to Plataforma Carlos Chagas, informing in the electronic form the institutions and members involved, as well as the budget to be supported to CNPq.
