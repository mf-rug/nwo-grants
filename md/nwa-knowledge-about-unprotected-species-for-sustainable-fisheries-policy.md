### Purpose

The aim of this Call for proposals is to develop insights into the impact that the energy, food and nature transitions have on unprotected fish species in the North Sea. These insights are developed in cooperations with the fishing industry and form the basis for the establishment of a sustainable integrated North Sea policy, in which the developments in the North Sea and in the fishing industry go hand in hand. The programme should lead to insights that provide an initial framework for:

1. Policy options for the management and protection of unprotected species, nature conservation, and measures to benefit fisheries;
2. Insights that can help the fishing sector determine the direction in which it can develop to ensure sustainable and future-proof fishing;
3. Opportunities arising from the achievement of the objectives of 'The North Sea Agreement' to support sustainable fishing for unprotected species and to mitigate undesirable developments for these target species and for the fishing communities concerned.

### Who can apply

Proposals are to be submitted by the main applicant on behalf of the consortium. The main applicant has participated in both collaborative workshops (see Sections 2.4.1, 3.3.1 and 4.2.2 of the Call for proposals).

There are four categories of participants within a consortium:

1. Main applicant;
2. Co-applicant(s);
3. Cooperation partner(s);
4. Co-financier(s) (optional).
A consortium consist of at least a main applicant, co-applicant and collaboration partner. See section 3 of the Call for Proposals for conditions for consortium partners

### Consortium building

In this Call for proposals, consortium formation is encouraged through Collaborative workshops. The Collaborative workshops will take place on 8 and 29 October 2025. The two collaborative workshops build on each other in terms of content. Participation in both workshops is mandatory for lead applicants. If necessary, it is possible to designate a substitute for this. More information can be found in the Call for proposals.

The aim of collaborative workshops is to bring together ideas, encourage cooperation and submission of a limited number of research proposals by broad (innovative) consortia, on a predefined societal issue. It also offers participants insight into the field of forces surrounding their topic. During the workshop, interested parties (researchers and public and/or private societal parties) around a specific theme are brought into contact with each other. For more information see Section 4.2 of the Call for proposals..

Additional information about NWO's efforts in the field of consortium formation can be found on the website: [Consortium formation | NWO](/en/consortium-formation).

### What to apply for

A maximum grant amount of €4,780,000.‐ can be applied for. The applicant and co‐applicants may include costs for personnel, equipment, investments and knowledge utilisation among other things. Co-funding is not compulsory within this Call for proposals.

1. Registration for participation in the collaborative workshops is open until **1 October 2025**.

Participation in both collaborative workshops is mandatory for the main applicant or service representative. Participation in both Collaborative workshops is mandatory for the main applicant or its replacement.
2. The Collaborative workshops will take place on **8 and 29 October 2025**.
3. The deadline for submitting the proposal is **29 January 2026, before 14:00:00 CET**.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Problem posed and problem analysis (20%)
2. Expected impact and route to impact (20%)
3. Quality of the consortium (30%)
4. Quality of the research (30%)
For this NWA Call for proposals, an assessment committee will be appointed by the NWO Executive Board. The assessment committee will have a broad composition. This means that not only scientific expertise will be represented in the assessment committee, but also expertise from the entire knowledge chain, including societal stakeholders who are highly familiar with the subject and representatives from the target group. The task of the assessment committee is to assess the applications and the relevant documents that are submitted, in conjunction with each other and with regard to both the respective merits of each application and the assessment criteria outlined in this Call for proposals.

### Procedure

The application procedure consists of the following steps:

- Registering for collaborative workshops;
- Participation in two collaborative workshops (8 and 29 October 2025; mandatory for main applicants; see section 3.3.1);
- Submission of the proposal (main applicant; deadline 29 January 2026, 14:00:00 CET);
- Admissibility check of the proposal (Bureau NWA; within two weeks after submission);
- Administrative corrections (main applicant within 5 working days after admissibility check);
- Processing the application (Bureau NWA);
- Preliminary advice (assessment committee);
- Interview selection (assessment committee);
- Interview with consortium;
- Advice of the assessment committee;
- Decision-making (NWO Executive Board. Expected decision date 31 March 2026).
