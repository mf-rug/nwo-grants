## Explore

### Purpose

The European partnership ‘Rescuing Biodiversity to Safeguard Life on Earth’ (Biodiversa+) under Horizon Europe aims to contribute to bringing biodiversity in Europe back on a path of recovery by 2030. The partnership provides an overarching platform connecting national/local and European research and innovation programs and combines in-cash and in-kind resources, in line with the European Green Deal and the new EU Biodiversity Strategy for 2030.

The fourth joint call of the partnership focuses on the topic ‘Biodiversity and Transformative Change (BiodivTransform)’. The BiodivTransform Call aims at supporting interdisciplinary, transdisciplinary, and/or cross-sectoral research and innovation projects that will:

- Help understand trade-offs and cross-linkages between the worldwide crises of biodiversity loss, climate change and pollution.
- Identify, analyse, and comprehend transformation processes that may safeguard biodiversity by mitigating its threats and halt – even reverse – its decline, as well as the benefits it may provide to people.

### Who can apply

Applications need to be submitted by research consortia including teams from a minimum of three countries participating in the call (including a minimum of two from EU Member States or EU Associated Countries). See the Biodiversa+ Call Documents for details and participating countries.

Full, associate and assistant professors, lectors and other researchers with a comparable position may submit an application (i.e. participate in a consortium and request NWO funding) if they have a tenured position (and therefore a paid position for an indefinite period) or a tenure track agreement at one of the institutions listed in Funding Organisation rules (part NWO) of the call for proposals.

Persons with a zero-hour employment agreement or with a contract for a limited period of time (other than a tenure track appointment) may not submit a proposal.

An applicant may only request NWO funding for one project (part of a European consortium) in this call of the European Biodiversity Partnership.

More information on the transnational eligibility criteria, and the eligibility criteria of other countries can be found in the Biodiversa+ Call Documents and Funding Organisation Rules.

### What to apply for

For a research proposal in this round, a maximum of €325,000 can be applied for. The maximum project duration is 3 years. No limit has been set on the total international consortium size (budget or partners), the national funding agencies solely limit the budget that you can apply for at the national level. At NWO this maximum is €325,000. The budget modules that are available within this call for proposals are: Postdoc, Research leave, Personnel universities of applied sciences, Material Costs, Knowledge Utilisation and Internationalisation. The maximum amounts for each module are stated in the Funding Organisation rules (part NWO) in the Call Documents.

For the budget module “Postdoc”, a one-off individual bench fee of €5,000 is added on top of the salary costs to encourage the scientific career of the project employee funded by NWO.

An explanation of the budget modules can be found with the ‘Downloads for your application’ under the heading 'Prepare'.

### Assessment

### Criteria

**Pre-proposals will be reviewed on the basis of the following three criteria:**

- Fit to the scope of the call
- Novelty of the research
- Impact
**Full proposals will be reviewed on the basis of the following three criteria:**

- Excellence;
- Quality and efficiency of the implementation;
- Impact.
Please see the full Biodiversa+ Call Documents for the criteria including the sub-criteria.

### Procedure

The assessment in this call will be done in two stages. In the first stage consortia are invited to submit a pre-proposal. Only consortia that were successful in the first stage can submit a full proposal. Eligible proposals – both in the first and in the second stage – will be assessed by an independent Evaluation Committee consisting both of scientific and policy/management experts. The proposals will be ranked based on the criteria in the call text. Based on the ranking and the budget available, the national and regional funding agencies will take the funding decision. At NWO the Executive Board will take this decision.
