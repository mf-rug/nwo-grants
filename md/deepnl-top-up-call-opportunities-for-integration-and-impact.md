# DeepNL Top-up Call: Opportunities for Integration and Impact

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/deepnl-top-up-call-opportunities-for-integration-and-impact

Researchers who have previously received funding in the DeepNL programme can submit applications for small projects that build on previous DeepNL projects. They are encouraged to take concrete steps towards integration, upscaling, application and/or outreach, to increase the scientific and societal impact of the programme.

## Characteristics

**Status:** Open calls Continuous  
**Closing date full application:** 22 September 2026 14:00 hrs.  
**Budget:** €500,000  
**Research programme:** DeepNL  
**Upcoming submission date:** 7 May 2026 14:00 hrs.  

## Explore

### Purpose

This call aims to make targeted steps towards integration, upscaling, application and/or outreach, aimed at making results from the DeepNL programme accessible and usable for professionals and other knowledge users. Examples include tools for assessing or predicting subsurface properties, or activities that strengthen outreach to stakeholders and society. Projects can contribute to insights into gas production in Groningen or connect these to future mining and storage activities.

### Who can apply

Researchers are eligible if they are or were involved as (co-)project leader, postdoctoral researcher or PhD candidate in an ongoing or completed DeepNL project. Professors and researchers that have a PhD may submit an application if they have a paid position for at least the duration of the application process and the duration of the research project for which the grant is being requested at one of the organisations mentioned in the Call for proposals. Applications are submitted by one main applicant, and optionally with one or more co-applicants. An applicant may be involved in a maximum of two proposals, of which only one proposal in the role of main applicant.

### When to apply

Proposals can be submitted between **6 January 2026, 14:00:00 CET, and 22 September 2026, 14:00:00 CEST**, as long as the grant ceiling has not yet been reached. Within this period, there will be three intermediate submission deadlines for assessment:

- 10 February 2026, before 14:00:00 CET
- 7 May 2026, before 14:00:00 CEST
- 22 September 2026, before 14:00:00 CEST

### What to apply for

A maximum of €50,000 can be requested for each project for a period of 12 months. The total budget for this call is €500,000.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications will be assessed on the basis of two main criteria:

1. Alignment with the aim of the Call for proposals (weighting 50%)
2. Quality of the project plan (weighting 50%)

### Procedure

The application procedure consists of four steps:

1. admissibility of the proposal;
2. initial advice from the assessment committee;
3. assessment committee meeting;
4. decision-making.
NWO first assesses whether the application meets the administrative and technical requirements. Proposals are processed in order of receipt. The submission date and time of an application that meets all the administrative conditions is decisive in determining the order of applications to be awarded. An assessment committee draws up a written funding recommendation for each proposal, based on the assessment criteria. The board of the NWO Domain Science will take a decision about awarding or rejecting the proposals based on the advice from the assessment committee and the available budget.
