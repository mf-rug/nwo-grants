# Science for policy

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/science-for-policy

Knowledge from research is an important element of sound, effective policy. As practice has shown, however, it is not self-evident to use existing knowledge from research in policy or to generate new knowledge for policy. This NWA programme aims to contribute to improving the interaction between knowledge and policy. Improved interaction increases the likelihood of realising policy objectives, while enhancing both the legitimacy of implemented policies and trust in the government.

### Purpose

The objective of this NWA programme is to investigate how to optimise the government’s application of knowledge from research in order to improve understanding of complex societal challenges, how to address them more effectively and efficiently, and how to learn from these efforts. To this end, this programme considers current and possible new forms of interactions between the worlds of knowledge and policy, as well as the reinforcement of these forms of cooperation and what it is expected to yield: knowledge that can be utilised better and better knowledge utilisation. The insights and action perspectives that are needed in order to achieve this goal will be developed and tested through co-creation in the departmental *policy labs*. A policy lab is an experimental learning environment in which research proceeds parallel to the various phases of the policy cycle. Within this physical research environment, various parties (knowledge institutions, government bodies and civil society organisations) engage in co-creative collaboration to address a current departmental policy file.

### Who can apply

A consortium should consist of at least one main applicant, one co-applicant and one collaborative partner. Co-funding is not required.
Additional conditions:

- The main applicant may submit only one proposal in this call in the capacity of main applicant.
- The main applicant may additionally participate as a co-applicant in another consortium no more than once.
- The main applicant(or a designated substitute)has participated in all three collaborative workshops.
- A co-applicant may participate as a co-applicant in a maximum of two consortia in this call.
- Individuals on zero-hours employment contracts are excluded from submission as co-applicants.

### Consortium formation

NWO encourages and supports the formation of these collaborations by organising meetings or workshops for consortium formation. In this Call for proposals, consortium formation is encouraged through organising three collaborative workshops. The workshops offer the possibility to combine (research) ideas and to form broad innovative consortia (consolidation of strengths). It is subsequently up to the participants to enter into collaboration in inter- and transdisciplinary innovative consortia that span the entire knowledge chain, and to jointly develop the proposal. The main applicant (or a designated substitute) is required to participate in all three collaborative workshops.

### What to apply for

The maximum budget to be applied for is € 4,630,000. Within this Call for proposals it is expected that a maximum of one proposal will be awarded funding. The duration of the project is 48 to 60 months.  The applicant and co-applicants can request funding for the budget modules personnel, equipment, investments, knowledge utilisation, and project management.

### When to apply

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Problem definition and analysis,
2. Expected impact and route to impact,
3. Quality of the consortium,
4. Quality of the research.
The Call for proposals section 2.2.4 includes additional assessment criteria concerning the consortium, the *policy labs*, the supporting case studies, and the connectFing interdepartmental research.

### Procedure

The procedure consists of two phases:

1. Registration for and participation in the three collaborative workshops (mandatory for main applicants).
2. Submission of a proposal.
NWO will establish an assessment committee to assess the admissible proposals. No referees will be used for this funding round. NWO will compile a preliminary ranking based on the preliminary recommendations and the scores of the designated committee members. In principle, all consortia will be invited for an interview unless too many proposals have been submitted. In that case, a preselection will be made. At the end of the interviews, the committee will draw up a written recommendation on the quality and ranking of the proposals, which will be submitted to the Executive Board for approval.

### Write proposal

- This NWA programme invites the design and implementation of participatory and interactive action research. The proposal should address the following three components in full:
1. Departmental *policy lab*s
2. Supporting case studies
3. Connecting interdepartmental research
- The five departmental*policy labs* (at the Ministries of Defence; Justice and Security; Education, Culture and Science; and Housing and Spatial Planning/Infrastructure and Water Management) focus on existing files within the Dutch national government, in order to analyse the development and utilisation of knowledge, as well as to suggest and introduce improvements.
- The four supporting case studies can be from either within or outside the *policy labs*, but are focused on short-term policy initiatives and testing out smaller interventions.
- The connecting interdepartmental research brings together insights from the various *policy labs* and supporting case studies, and it provides a space within which the researchers, policy officers and other stakeholders can learn together and share experiences.

### Mandatory consortium formation activities

NWO is organizing three collaboration workshops. These workshops aim to bring together potential applicants and their ideas, encourage collaboration in broad, innovative consortia, and reduce the number of applications. The programme of each workshop will be shaped according to the theme of the call for proposals, the content descriptions of the *policy labs* and supporting cases, the expertise of the participants, and the expected impact of their contributions. While NWO supports mutual exploration, parties ultimately decide for themselves whether and with whom they wish to cooperate. During the workshops, participants exchange ideas, explore cooperation in consortia and make initial agreements for joint application development. Following the workshops, the consortia will further develop the applications.
