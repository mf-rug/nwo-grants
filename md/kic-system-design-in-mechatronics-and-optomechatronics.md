# KIC System design in mechatronics and optomechatronics

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/kic-system-design-in-mechatronics-and-optomechatronics

There is a wealth of expertise in the Netherlands in the fields of mechatronics and optomechatronics. These technologies are essential for building systems and machines for various sectors such as the semiconductor industry, medical technology, robotics, aerospace, and precision agriculture. This call invites knowledge institutions, industry, and societal stakeholders to form a consortium for research into the innovation and optimization of these technologies.

## Explore

### Purpose

This Call for proposals aims to stimulate the optimisation of the design of (opto)mechatronic (OMT) systems. These systems contribute to the development and construction of high-tech instruments and machines that can address numerous societal challenges, such as improving the quality of healthcare and the efficiency of production processes. Researchers are invited to submit proposals for interdisciplinary research that combines fundamental and practice-oriented approaches. Applicants are encouraged to form broad consortia with partners from various sectors, including government institutions, TO2 institutes, societal organizations, and companies. This collaboration enhances the societal relevance and applicability of the research.

### Who can apply

Proposals are submitted by the main applicant and one or more co-applicants. A proposal is written by a consortium in which besides the applicants other participants are involved as well. Four categories of participants can be distinguished in the consortium:

- Main applicant
- Co-applicant(s)
- Co-funder(s)
- Cooperation partner(s)
A consortium must consist of at least one main applicant, one co-applicant, and two co-funders, possibly supplemented by one or more cooperation partners. The conditions for each participant are explained in more detail in the following sections. All consortium partners must play an active role in formulating the research questions and in the design and implementation of the project.

### Consortium building

On 11 December 2025, NWO will organize a matchmaking event for this Call for Proposals. Participation in this activity is recommended but not mandatory

### Apply for

For a proposal submitted in this Call for proposals, applicants can request a minimum of €600,000 and a maximum of €3,200,000 in NWO funding. NWO will fund up to 80% of the total project budget; the remaining portion must be covered through mandatory co-funding (see section 3.5.6). NWO will never fund less than 50% of the total project budget. Applicants and co-applicants may include costs for personnel, materials, investments, knowledge utilization, and project management.

### When to apply

- The deadline for submitting proposals is 21 April 2026, before 14:00:00 CEST.
- The deadline for submitting a request for the assessment of foreign organizations (see section 7.1.1) is 7 April 2026, before 14:00:00 CEST.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The pre-proposals submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

- Problem definition and analysis (20%)
- Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%)
- Quality of the consortium (30%)
- Quality of the research (30%)

### Procedure

The application procedure consists of the following steps:

- matchmaking;
- submission of the proposal;
- admissibility of the proposal;
- peer review by referees;
- rebuttal;
- pre-advice assessment committee;
- interview selection;
- interview;
- assessment committee meeting;
- decision-making.
An external, independent assessment committee will be assigned for this Call for proposals, consisting of representatives from the research field and society with knowledge of (partial aspects of) the topic.

### Write proposal

These conditions are:

the main applicant and co-applicant(s) meet the conditions stated in Section 3.1 and Section 7.1.1;

- the applying consortium contains two or more co-funders;
- the application complies with the DORA guidelines as described in Section 4.1;
- the application is submitted via the main applicant’s ISAAC account;
- the application is received before the deadline;
- the application is written in English;
- the proposed project has a duration of at most 6 years;
- all of the required annexes and forms are, after a possible request to make additions or changes, have been completed and submitted completely and according to the instructions and in accordance with the terms of this Call for proposals;
- the budget in the application has been prepared according to the terms of this Call for proposals (using the format made available which includes the most recent rates);
- the required co-financing has been correctly and fully committed through co-financing statements.

### Fill in forms

Compulsory annexes:

budget form;

- declarations co-funding from co-funders (see Section 3.1.2 and 3.5.6);
- form ‘Statement and signature’.
- letter guaranteeing the continuity of project supervision (if applicable, see Section 3.1 of the Call for Proposals);
- confirmation of own contribution to investments (only if applicable, see section 7.1 of the Call for Proposals);
- Knowledge security statement.
The proposal and the appendix(es) must be drawn up in accordance with the template provided by NWO. Annexes must be uploaded in ISAAC separately from the application. All of the annexes, except for the budget, must be submitted as PDF files (without encryption). The budget must be submitted in ISAAC as an Excel file.

When submitting the proposal, the attached declarations co-funding should guarantee the full required amount of co-funding in line with the conditions of Section 3.5.6.

Any annexes other than those above-mentioned are not permitted.
