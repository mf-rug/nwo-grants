# ADM-LAB: Livings Labs for Adaptive Delta Management in Bangladesh

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/adm-lab-livings-labs-for-adaptive-delta-management-in-bangladesh

The Netherlands and Bangladesh, as densely populated delta countries, are susceptible to the effects of climate change. Effective natural resource management is crucial for inclusive development, prosperity, food security, and climate-resilient infrastructure. This programme contributes to sustainable and knowledgeinformed policy on adaptive delta management (ADM) and its implementation under the Bangladesh Delta Plan 2100.

## Explore

### Purpose

The ADM-LAB programme aims to enhance sustainable and knowledge-informed policies on Adaptive Delta Management (ADM) in Bangladesh under the Bangladesh Delta Plan 2100 (BDP2100). It seeks to secure past knowledge, integrate further knowledge development, and accelerate ADM implementation in both Bangladesh and the Netherlands. The programme focuses on delivering insights for policy and practitioners, creating an effective interface for knowledge interactions, and addressing challenges in ADM implementation. Furthermore, it emphasizes capacity building, interdisciplinary collaboration, and ownership in Bangladesh for effective ADM implementation.

### Who can apply

Applications in Phase I are submitted by at least one main applicant, a co-applicant, a collaboration partner and optional co-funders. Only persons working at a Bangladeshi university or research organisation based in Bangladesh can act as main applicant. Co-applicants play an active role in the project and each application must include at least one co-applicant based in the Kingdom of the Netherlands. Organizational eligibility is assessed beforehand. Cooperation partners are crucial for knowledge sharing but cannot request funding for personnel or research costs. Letters of commitment are required from cooperation partners.

### What to apply for

Applicants can apply for Living Labs (Phase I) and a Knowledge Management project (Phase II).   Phase I Living Lab applications can request up to €950,000, with a maximum project duration of 5 years. A minimum of €75,000 per living lab must be allocated towards the Knowledge Management project, facilitating research generalization and participation in Phase II.   Phase I involves three living lab projects focusing on four geographic (1. Coasts and Estuaries 2. Rivers 3. Urban 4. Haor and Wetlands) and thematic challenges within ADM.  Phase II allows for a maximum budget of €970,000, with a project duration of 4 years. Applicants should only request funding necessary for project realization, as detailed in the provided budget modules.  Phase II entails a knowledge management project to scale up insights from the living labs, facilitate stakeholder engagement, and ensure sustainability beyond the programme's timeframe. In Phase II, Phase I participants form a new consortium for a joint proposal for the overarching knowledge management project.  See the call text for details.

### Assessment

### Criteria

**Phase I:**

The applications submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Problem definition and analysis (20%)
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%)
3. Quality of the consortium (30%)
4. Quality of the research (30%)
**Phase II:**

The application submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Alignment with the objective of the programme (40%)
2. Quality of the consortium (30%)
3. Quality of the proposal (30%)
Each criterion consists of multiple sub-criteria which are listed in paragraph 4.3 of the call text.

### Procedure

The call consists of two phases. Proposals for living labs are submitted in Phase I. After assessment by NWO and an external committee Phase I participants form a new consortium for a joint proposal for the overarching knowledge management project in Phase II.  Details about the procedure are described in chapter 4 of the call text.
