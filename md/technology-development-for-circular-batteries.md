# Technology development for circular batteries

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/technology-development-for-circular-batteries

The call ‘Technology Development for Circular Batteries’ contributes to the National Growth Fund Programme ‘Material Independence & Circular Batteries’. This call aims at advancing the knowledge and technology development in the battery production and value chain - including recycling, new battery materials and battery systems for Long Duration Energy Storage applications. This call invites researchers to explore this in an interdisciplinary consortium.

### Purpose

The aim of this Call for Proposals is to contribute to the innovation programme of the NGF proposal ‘Material Independence and Circular Batteries’. This Call for Proposals is thus aimed at realising key technologies in the battery production and value chain, accelerating the energy transition, creating interdependence and increasing sustainable earning capacity. To achieve those key positions in the development of different parts of the battery value chain, this call comprises three separate programme lines:

| Program line 1: | Selective recovery of materials from lithium iron phosphate batteries and associated mixed waste streams. |
| --- | --- |
| Program line 2: | Development of sustainable next generation sodium-ion batteries with lower environmental impact and improved performance. |
| Program line 3: | Development of innovative manufacturing technologies for battery systems suitable for Long Duration Energy Storage applications. |

In addition, research on techno-economic feasibility, impact assessments and life-cycle analysis are integral parts of this programme.

### Who can apply

Researchers may act as main or co-applicants if they are employed by a Dutch research organisation such as universities, universities of applied sciences, TO2 institutes or other institutes. For research proposals, consortia must be formed consisting of at least one main applicant, one co-applicant and one cooperation partner. Cooperation partners can be other research institutions as well as companies or public organisations. The development of an impact plan is also a mandatory part of the proposals.

### Consortium building

The consortia envisaged here must consist of at least one main applicant, one co-applicant and one cooperation partner, where main and co-applicants should be affiliated with research institutions eligible for funding. As cooperation partners, other research institutions, companies or public organisations, for example, may participate in projects. To facilitate consortium formation, a one-day workshop will be organised, for which interested parties have to register. The registration form as well as more information about the procedure can be found on the corresponding website.

### What to apply for

This Call for Proposals includes three programme lines with their own grant budget:

- For programme line 1, a maximum of one application will be awarded for a maximum of €1,440,000 grant,
- For programme line 2, a maximum of one application will be awarded for a maximum of €8,000,000 grant,
- For programme line 3, a maximum of one application will be awarded for a maximum of €5,000,000 grant.
Subsidies can be applied for personnel, equipment, investments and knowledge utilisation. The intended duration of projects should be four years with a maximum of no more than six years.

### Assessment

### Criteria

Applications submitted within this Call for Proposals will be assessed according to the following criteria:

- Criterion 1: Scientific quality of the research,
- Criterion 2: Scientific impact and expected contribution to the development of battery technologies,
- Criterion 3: Quality of the consortium.

### Procedure

The application procedure consists of the following steps:

- registration for workshop,
- participation in workshop (mandatory for main applicant),
- submission of the proposal,
- admissibility of the proposal,
- pre-advice by member of expert commission,
- interview and assessment committee meeting,
- decision-making.

Applications must be submitted through the Isaac application and reporting system. If you do not have an Isaac account yet, then please create one at least one workday in advance. ### Write proposal

For this call, it is mandatory to prepare your proposal in English. To prepare your proposal, please go through the following steps:

- download the application form from the ISAAC online application system or from this website,
- fill in the application form,
- save the form as a pdf and submit it to ISAAC with any mandatory attachment(s),
- complete the information requested online in ISAAC.
The main applicant submits the application via ISAAC, NWO's electronic submission system. During the assessment process, NWO communicates with the main applicant.

Participation in the workshop for consortium formation is mandatory for main applicants or their substitutes.
