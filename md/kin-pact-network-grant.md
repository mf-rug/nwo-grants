# KIN Pact Network Grant

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/kin-pact-network-grant

Are you working with your network on a climate transition and are you looking for (financial) support to properly start or professionalize your network? Or would you like support in setting up a transdisciplinary climate transition network? This call might be for you!

## Characteristics

**Status:** Closed  
**Closing date full application:** 8 April 2025 14:00 hrs.

## Explore

### Purpose

The purpose of the KIN Pact Network grant is to support the creation of transdisciplinary networks focused on climate transitions. This applies both to the professionalisation of existing networks, and networks yet to be established. In this way, KIN aims to further stimulate the collaboration between different stakeholders and disciplines and to enhance the use of knowledge on climate transition themes.

### Who can apply

Employees of all legal entities established in the Kingdom of the Netherlands can submit an application. A condition for receiving a funding is that the main and co-applicants of the network must be affiliated with the KIN Pact when submitting an application. The network must be transdisciplinary and consist of at least one knowledge institution and at least two societal organizations; these form the main and co-applicants. Transdisciplinarity means that the network consists of more than one thematic discipline and more than one type of organisation (knowledge institution, social organisation, government, companies, etc.). The KIN Pact network subsidy is explicitly not intended for networks with a focus on a single area of ​​expertise (mono-disciplinary) or conducting research.

### What to apply for

A maximum of €25,000 in subsidy can be requested per application. Each network can submit one application. The subsidy must be used within one year after granting, this is not the same as the duration of the network.

Examples of activities eligible for funding include;

- Organizing or co-hosting events and meetings;
- Developing products or services focused on knowledge brokering.
- Organizing community engangement (e.g., educational events and participatory workshops; arts and culture initiatives);
- Capacity building (e.g. field trips, training);
- Analyses on system barriers that impede (participants in) the network in effectiveness;
- Organizational or digital support for the network.
No funding can be requested for scientific publications and activities that are already receiving funding (from KIN or elsewhere).

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this call will be assessed based on the following criteria:

1. Alignment with the mission of the KIN and KIN Pact (50%),
2. Appropriateness of activities and budget (50%).
