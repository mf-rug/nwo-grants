# KIC - Early detection of (emerging) outbreaks of infectious diseases

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/kic-early-detection-of-emerging-outbreaks-of-infectious-diseases

Alpha, gamma and beta researchers are invited to collaborate with industry and public and societal organisations in order to develop AI-based early detection. This should contribute to the early detection of emerging outbreaks of infectious diseases in the Netherlands and the prevention of disruptive epidemics.

## Explore

### Purpose

The aim of this programme is to develop and refine AI-driven early detection systems. The focus is on using and combining existing non-biomedical data sources for the early detection of infectious disease outbreaks. Combining these data sources with existing biomedical surveillance, such as data from the veterinary domain, general practitioners or screening, is an important innovation. This will enable early detection of infection hotspots in the Netherlands and prevent disruptive epidemics. Applicants are also recommended to seek alignment with existing national and international initiatives and/or networks. Applicants are encouraged to develop a plan for data infrastructure, revenue models and a response plan and/or integrate these into existing plans. Therapy and vaccine development are not covered by the Call for proposals.

### Who can apply

Proposals are submitted by the main applicant and one or more co-applicants. Researchers may submit an application if they have a tenured position (and therefore a paid position for an indefinite period) or a tenure track agreement at a research organisation. A proposal is written by a consortium in which besides the applicants two or more co-funders, and potentially one or more cooperation partners, are involved as well. All consortiumpartners should play an active role in the formulation of the research questions and in the design and implementation of the project.

For more information: see section 3.1 in the Call for Proposals.

### Consortium building

This Call for proposals stimulates the formation of consortiums by means of collaborative workshops. **Attending the collaborative workshops is mandatory for main applicants.**

The objective of the collaborative workshops is to bring together potential applicants and their ideas, to encourage collaboration within broad, innovative consortia, and to limit the number of proposals.

### What to apply for

For an application, a minimum of €2.000.000 and a maximum of €3.500.000 of NWO funding can be applied for. With this, NWO finances maximally 90% of the total project budget; the rest of the funding must be contributed by the compulsory co-funding (see Section 3.5.6). NWO never funds less than 50% of the total project budget.

The maximum duration of the proposed project is 6 years.

### When to apply

- The dates of the collaborative workshops are **19 March 2026 and 16 April 2026.**
- The deadline for submission of full applications is **6 October 2026, 14:00:00 CEST.**

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

- Problem definition and analysis (20%)
- Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%)
- Quality of the consortium (30%)
- Quality of the research (30%)

### More information

For this Call for proposals, attending the collaborative workshops by the main applicant (or substitute) is mandatory.

Full applications will be provided with peer review by referees. Applicants will be given the opportunity to submit a rebuttal to this. The applications will then be assessed by the assessment committee and and applicants will be given the opportunity to answer questions from the assessment committee during an interview. The assessment committee will present its advice to the NWO Executive Board for decision-making. In September 2027, the NWO Executive Board will adopt the final assessment of the applications.
