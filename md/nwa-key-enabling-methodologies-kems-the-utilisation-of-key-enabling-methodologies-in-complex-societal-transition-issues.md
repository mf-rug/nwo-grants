# NWA Key Enabling Methodologies (KEMs) - The utilisation of Key Enabling Methodologies in complex societal transition issues

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/nwa-key-enabling-methodologies-kems-the-utilisation-of-key-enabling-methodologies-in-complex-societal-transition-issues

This Call for proposals calls on knowledge institutions and societal stakeholders to jointly develop scientific and practice-oriented research proposals aimed at utilizing Key Enabling Methodologies (KEMs) in complex societal transition issues. The consortia work multi- and interdisciplinary and are knowledge chain-wide.

## Characteristics

**Status:** Closed  
**Closing date full application:** 12 June 2025 14:00 hrs.  
**Budget:** €3,050,000  
**Research programme:** Key Enabling Methodologies (KEMs) Support NWA-routes  

## Explore

### Purpose

The programme ‘NWA Key Enabling Methodologies (KEMs) - The utilisation of Key Enabling Methodologies in complex societal transition issues’ enables research on Key Enabling Methodologies and contributes to the development of a framework for the coherent deployment of these methods and techniques in policy development for complex transitions. By examining the deployment of KEMs in the context of departmental policy-making from different perspectives, the working principle of the KEMs is examined through systematic reflection, learning about usability and thus sharpening and developing the methods.

### Who can apply

An application must be submitted by a consortium. A consortium should consist of at least one main applicant, co-applicant and collaboration partner.

All participants should play an active role in formulating the research questions and in the design and implementation of the project.

The conditions per participant are further explained in the Call for proposals.

### Consortium building

This Call for proposals entails two phases: the submission of a research idea and participation in two collaborative workshops, and the full application submission phase. Following the submission of the research idea, two consecutive collaborative workshops will be organised by NWO. The person who submits the research idea will automatically be registered for the collaborative workshops.

The workshops will take place on Wednesday 5 March and 26 March 2025 in the afternoon in Utrecht. Participation in the workshops is mandatory for the main applicant who wants to submit an application in phase 2. Should this person be unable to attend, a replacement must be arranged, working within the same organisation.

All parties involved in a submitted research idea can apply for the workshops. In addition, the workshops are open to other interested parties. Parties not yet affiliated with a research idea before the workshop can view the submitted ideas on the NWO website. This allows them to determine whether affiliation with a research idea might be useful. Registration for the workshops is possible via the form on the programme page on the NWO website. Deadline for registration for the workshops is 20 February 2025 at 14:00:00 CET. Civil society organisations are specifically invited to submit a research idea and/or participate in the workshops.

### What to apply

A minimum of €2,850,000 and a maximum of €3,050,000 subsidy can be applied for per project. The maximum duration of the proposed project is 72 months/6 years. The applicant and co-applicants can include costs for personnel, equipment, investments and knowledge utilisation. The available budget modules (including maximum amounts) are listed in the Call for proposals. Apply only for what is essential to carry out the project. The rates and an explanation of these budget modules can be found in Annex 7.1 of the Call for proposals.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Problem posed and problem analysis, contribution to solution,
2. Expected impact and route to impact,
3. Quality of the consortium,
4. Quality of the research.
Criteria 1 and 2 each carry a weighting of 20% in the overall assessment, and criteria 3 and 4 each carry a weighting of 30%.

### Procedure

The deadline for submitting proposals is 12 June 2025, before 14:00:00 CEST. For the assessment of the proposals, the committee will make use of pre-advices and interviews will be conducted. A final decision on funding by the NWO Executive Board is expected in October 2025.
