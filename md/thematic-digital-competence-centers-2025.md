### Purpose

The aim of this instrument is to fund projects that will help to achieve the digitalization ambitions of the Thematic Digital Competence Centres (TDCCs) and the community they serve.

The ambitions are described by the TDCCs in the TDCC roadmaps, and cover data, software and computing.

They include ambitions such as:

- Improving the knowledge and skills of researchers and research support professionals (education and training);
- How to deal with data sharing bottlenecks (e.g., privacy laws);
- Establishing data sharing agreements (e.g., metadata standards);
- Knowledge of the use of software and platforms for sharing and analyzing data;
- Increasing knowledge of (the proper use of) available computing facilities.
In most cases, this is not about developing new scientific knowledge, but about finding and disseminating the right knowledge, and agreeing on its joint application. The projects resulting from this Call for proposals will therefore mainly focus on support and networking.

The project activities applied for under this Call for proposals should fit within the above ambitions and the definitions from the Community Framework for State aid for research, development and innovation. See section 2.1 in the Call for proposals for more information.

### Who can apply

Individuals may apply if they are permanently employed (and therefore have a remunerated employment contract for an indefinite period of time) or have a tenure track agreement with one of the research organisations listed below:

- Universities and colleges as referred to in Article 1.8(1) of the Higher Education and Scientific Research Act and the universities listed in the Policy Rule Universities in the Kingdom of the Netherlands;
- university medical centres, meaning academic hospitals as referred to in Article 1.13(1) of the Higher Education and Scientific Research Act;
- KNAW and NWO institutes;
- The Netherlands Cancer Institute;
- The Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics in Nijmegen;
- NCB Naturalis;
- Advanced Research Centre for NanoLithography (ARCNL);
- Princess Máxima Centre;
- TO2 federation: Deltares, Maritime Research Institute Netherlands (Marin), Netherlands Organisation for Applied Natural Science Research (TNO), Netherlands Aerospace Centre (NLR) and Wageningen University (WUR).
- National Institute of Public Health and the Environment (RIVM);
- Royal Netherlands Meteorological Institute (KNMI);
- IHE Delft Institute for Water Education;
- SURF;
- Netherlands eScience Center.
Persons on zero-hours contracts are excluded from submission.

It may happen that the applicant's tenure track agreement ends before the envisaged completion date of the project for which the grant is requested, or that the applicant's permanent employment ends before that date due to reaching retirement age. In such cases, the applicant shall attach a statement from his/her employer in which the research organisation concerned guarantees that the project and all persons working on the project for whom a grant is requested will be adequately supervised for the full duration of the project.

Applicants with part-time employment must guarantee adequate supervision of the project and of all persons working on the project for whom funding is requested.

### Additional requirements

Applicants affiliated to other research organisations as referred to in Article 1.1(4) of the NWO Grant Rules, the organisation must meet cumulative conditions, see Call for proposals section 3.1 for more information.

### What to apply for

A grant of minimum €50,000 and maximum of €400,000 can be applied for per project. The maximum duration of the proposed project is 24 months. The applicant and co-applicants can claim costs for personnel, equipment and knowledge utilisation. The available budget modules (including maximum amounts) with further explanation can be found in the Call for proposals. Apply only for what is essential to carry out the project.

### When to apply

- Applications can be submitted from 21 August 2025 until 20 November 2025, before 14:00:00 CET.

### Procedure

The application procedure consists of the following steps:

- submission of the application;
- processing the application;
- pre-advising the assessment committee;
- rebuttal;
- meeting of the assessment committee;
- decision-making.
For this call for proposals, an external, independent (inter)national assessment committee will be established, consisting of representatives from the scientific community and practitioners with knowledge of the field. The task of the assessment committee is to assess the submitted applications and related documents in relation to each other and on their own merits based on the given selection criteria in this Call for proposals.

Because of the expertise present in the assessment committee and because this Call for proposals is non-competitive and community-driven, NWO has decided not to use referees when evaluating applications.

See Call for proposals for further explanation of the procedure.

### Write proposal

Before you start preparing and submitting your application in the NWO online application system ISAAC, you should first contact the relevant TDCC (LSH, NES and SSH) about your project idea by sending an email via: [https://tdcc.nl/contact/](https://tdcc.nl/contact/)

To prepare your application, go through the following steps:

- download the application form from the online application system ISAAC or from the NWO website (on the website of the relevant funding instrument);
- fill in the application form;
- Save the form as a pdf and submit it with any mandatory attachment(s) in ISAAC;
- complete the data requested online in ISAAC.
Mandatory attachment(s):

- budget;
- statement of support signed by mandated TDCC representative(s);
- letter of guarantee own contribution.
The applicant consortium should provide its own cash or in-kind contribution of at least 25% of the total budget for the project application. One letter of guarantee per applicant institution should be attached to the application.

Each application must be accompanied by a letter of support signed by mandated representative(s) of the submitting TDCC domain.

NWO will review your application to check all the conditions of this Call for proposals. You are requested to be available after submitting an application to make any administrative corrections to meet the conditions for submission.

See Call for proposals for further details.
