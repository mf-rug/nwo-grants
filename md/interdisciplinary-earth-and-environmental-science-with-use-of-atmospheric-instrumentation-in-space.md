# Interdisciplinary Earth and Environmental Science with use of Atmospheric Instrumentation in Space

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/interdisciplinary-earth-and-environmental-science-with-use-of-atmospheric-instrumentation-in-space

With the thematic GO call “Interdisciplinary Earth and Environmental Science with use of Atmospheric Instrumentation in Space” NWO and the Netherlands Space Office (NSO) are looking for an interdisciplinary research proposal where researchers from multiple research areas within the Earth and environmental sciences, collaborate on research topics on the interface of their (sub)disciplines. Thereby the focus is on the use of data from atmospheric space instruments with strong Dutch involvement.

## Characteristics

**Status:** Closed  
**Closing date full application:** 6 March 2025 14:00 hrs.  
**For specific groups:** Interdisciplinary Collaboration  
**Budget:** €1,800,000  
**Research programme:** Use of space infrastructure for Earth observation and planetary research  

## Explore

### Purpose

This round of the GO programme focuses, within the framework of the general GO objective, on a defined theme within Earth observation research: the use of space-atmosphere instruments with significant Dutch involvement to address scientific issues that span multiple research areas (section  1.1.1 in the Call for proposals). Relative to the general objective of the GO programme (see section 1.1.1), the following are thus specified in this Call for proposals:

- infrastructure in space: these are atmospheric instruments with significant Dutch involvement;
- scientific research: it involves interdisciplinary scientific issues, which is understood to mean scientific issues at the interface between atmospheric research and one or more other (sub-)disciplines of Earth and environmental sciences such as land, water, oceans, ecology, ice, vegetation, soil.

### Who can apply

Researchers may submit an application if they have a tenured position (and therefore a paid position for an indefinite period) or a tenure track agreement at one of the following research organisations that are mentioned in section 3.1 of the Call for proposals. An applicatioon is submitted by a lead applicant and two or more co-applicants. Employees of organisations other than those listed above may be part of the consortium as collaborative partners but not as a lead or co-applicants, and cannot receive a grant under this Call for proposals. 
It could be the case that the applicant’s tenure track agreement ends before the intended completion date of the project for which funding is applied for, or that before that date, the applicant’s tenured contract ends due to the applicant reaching retirement age. In that case, the applicant needs to include a statement from their employer in which the research organisation concerned guarantees that the project and all project members for whom funding has been requested will receive adequate supervision for the full duration of the project.
NB: the details can be found in the Call for proposals.

### Consortium building

The preparatory workshops are designed to facilitate the creation of a joint research proposal from a submitting consortium (see section 2.1.2 in the Call for proposals). The preparatory workshops are planned for December 3, 2024 and January 23, 2025.

### What to apply for

Per project, a grant amount of at most €1.800.000 can be applied for. To promote interdisciplinary cooperation, a minimum of five scientific positions of PhD and/or postdoc type should be applied for per application, which should be appointed at a minimum of three different applicant research organisations participating in the consortium. Staff can only be appointed at institutions listed in section 3.1 in the Call for proposals.

### When to apply

- Deadline application: **March 6, 2025 at 2:00 pm CET**

### Assessment

### Criteria

1. Coherence and interdisciplinary nature of the proposed programme and alignment with the purpose of the Call for proposals (30%),
2. Scientific quality (25%),
3. Quality of the consortium (25%),
4. Scientific and/or societal impact (20%).

### Procedure

An external, independent assessment committee will be established for this Call for proposals, consisting of representatives from science with knowledge of the field. All applicant consortia are invited for an interview where they can present their research application to the assessment committee. In this manner, the principle of hearing and rebuttal is applied.  The interview is an important part of the assessment. Following the discussion, the committee draws up a written advice to the NWO Science Domain Board on the quality and ranking of the proposals. It bases this advice on the assessment criteria. The proposal as a whole must be rated at least 'very good' to be eligible for the grant.
