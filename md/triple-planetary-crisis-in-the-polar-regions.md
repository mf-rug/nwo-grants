# Triple Planetary Crisis in the Polar Regions

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/triple-planetary-crisis-in-the-polar-regions

Our world is facing a triple planetary crisis: climate change, biodiversity loss and pollution. These interconnected problems are hitting the polar regions particularly hard, as this region is warming up to four times faster than the global average. Each of these crises has been studied relatively well in its own right, but the interaction and cumulative effects – and the potential for action in policy, management and behaviour – present a challenge.

## Explore

### Purpose

This Call for Proposals invites research institutions and civil society organizations to jointly develop research proposals addressing the triple planetary crisis in the polar regions. This requires an interdisciplinary, knowledge-chain-wide approach based on the co-creation, co-design, and co-production of knowledge with rights-holders and stakeholders. This program has two main objectives, namely to:

1. generate new insights into the impacts of the interaction between the three planetary crises (climate change, biodiversity loss, and pollution) in the polar regions, as well as the accumulation of stressors and their potential consequences for polar life, the environment, and society.
2. use these insights as the basis for future scenarios and to generate perspectives for informed, integrated policy that reduces and prevents the single and multiple, cumulative, and interacting effects of pressure factors.
This Call for Proposals aims to strengthen the Dutch polar community by working toward a single joint consortium. International partners may be invited to further strengthen this consortium.

The method used in this research is Living Labs.

### Who can apply

This Call for Proposals consists of two phases. In phase 1, consortia submit a pre-application with an idea for a Living Lab. In phase 2, the applicants of the selected pre-proposals jointly develop the full proposal through synergy workshops, which is submitted by a main applicant chosen in phase 2 on behalf of the consortium.

There are three categories of participants within a consortium:

1. Main and co-applicants,
2. Collaboration partners,
3. Co-financier(s) (not mandatory).
The pre-proposal is submitted by a main applicant on behalf of the consortium. The consortium for a pre-proposal must consist of at least one main applicant and two co-applicants.

The full proposal is submitted by a main applicant on behalf of the consortium. The consortium for the full proposal must consist of one main applicant, six co-applicants and two cooperation partners per Living Lab. The involvement of co-applicants from foreign institutions is encouraged, in particular from Arctic communities (if the Living Lab is located in an inhabited part of the Arctic). The involvement of researchers from universities of applied sciences is also encouraged.

All participants must play an active role in formulating the research questions and in the design and implementation of the project. The conditions for each participant are explained in more detail in Chapter 3 of the Call for Proposals.

The early involvement of local rightsholders and stakeholders in the Arctic region for Arctic Living Lab ideas is recommended.

### Consortium building

NWO facilitates the formation of consortia with a matchmaking workshop and a maximum of three synergy workshops.

The goal of matchmaking (2 June 2026) is to bring together potential applicants, researchers, cooperation partners, and stakeholders from various fields and sectors to exchange ideas for, or needs for, research on the theme and impact described in the Call for proposals. During the one-time matchmaking meeting, participants will have the opportunity to exchange ideas, network, and identify any missing expertise. Participants will be responsible for shaping the next steps based on the matchmaking results. Participation in the matchmaking process is not mandatory

After the selection of pre-proposals, five consortia will in principle be invited to the synergy workshops (February-April 2027). The aim of the synergy workshops is to form a new interdisciplinary and transdisciplinary consortium that develops a single application based on different areas of knowledge and expertise in order to achieve maximum results and impact. Facilitators hired by NWO will guide the formation of the consortium and the integration of the pre-proposals into a single full proposal . Participation is mandatory for the main applicant.

### Procedure

#### Phase 1

- registration for and participation in the ideas workshop;
- submission of the preliminary application;
- processing of the preliminary application;
- interview selection (if applicable);
- interview;
- meeting of the assessment committee;
- decision on selection or rejection.
In principle, five preliminary applications, and thus five ideas for Living Labs, will be selected and invited to develop a full proposal in phase 2. The five consortia will together form one large consortium that will develop two or three Living Labs, at least one of which will be in the North Pole region and one in the South Pole region.

#### Phase 2

- participation in synergy workshops;
- submission of the application;
- processing of the application;
- preliminary advice from the assessment committee;
- interview;
- meeting of the assessment committee;
- recovery option (if applicable);
- decision-making.
Access to the fieldwork facilities of the British Antarctic Survey and the Dirck Gerritsz Lab requires approval of a so-called Pre-Award OSPQ

### Write proposal

#### Pre-proposal

A consortium for a pre-proposal must consist of:

- a lead applicant;
- at least two co-applicants;
- together, the lead and co-applicants must represent at least three different Dutch institutions;
- the consortium must have an interdisciplinary composition.
Collaborative partners and co-funders are not permitted in a pre-application.

The research proposal in the pre-application phase focuses on a knowledge chain-wide research idea that takes place in a living lab in the polar region. This proposal must encompass all three interactions between the three planetary crises.

#### Full proposal

After three Synergy workshops, for which five pre-registrations will be invited, a joint research proposal will be written that includes at least one Arctic and one Antarctic living lab. The research proposal must cover the entire knowledge chain and there must be sufficient integration for local stakeholders and rights holders.

The consortium must consist of:

- a lead applicant;
- at least six co-applicants;
- combined, the main and co-applicants need to represent at least five different Dutch organisations8;
- combined, the main and co-applicants must represent each pre-proposal of which the Living Lab is developed in the full proposal;
- at least two collaboration partners per detailed Living Lab;
- the consortium must have an interdisciplinary and knowledge-chain-wide composition.
The applicants of selected pre-proposals can jointly decide to invite new, not yet involved co-applicants to participate in the proposal.

### Mandatory consortium-building activities

In the consortium formation process, only the Synergy Workshops in phase 2 are mandatory for the main applicant. The matchmaking workshop in phase 1 is not mandatory, but participation is recommended.
