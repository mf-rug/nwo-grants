# Knowledge security: scientific research in a geopolitical context

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/knowledge-security-scientific-research-in-a-geopolitical-context

International research collaboration is of great societal and economic value, but at the same time makes the Dutch knowledge sector vulnerable to unwanted transfer, interference, and unethical use of scientific knowledge and research. This NWA programme promotes scientific and applied research into developments around knowledge security and inherent dilemmas related to knowledge security policies.

### Purpose

The NWA programme 'Knowledge security: scientific research in a geopolitical context' promotes research into developments around knowledge security and inherent dilemmas in the context of an evolving geopolitical landscape. The programme aims to contribute to the development of a conceptual framework for the study of knowledge security and yield insights into the international context and threats, the organisation and vulnerability of the knowledge sector, and the balance between opportunities and risks in knowledge security policies. These insights, together with tangible action perspectives, should help relevant stakeholders within the knowledge sector and government to develop balanced policies and deal with dilemmas. In this way, this programme aims to increase the resilience of the Dutch knowledge sector and bolster national security, in such a way that core academic values are being protected and promoted and society can continue to benefit from international cooperation.

### Who can apply

This Call for proposals consists of two phases. In phase 1, consortia submit a preproposal that addresses one of the four themes identified in the Call. In phase 2, the applicants of the selected preproposals jointly develop a proposal by participating in synergy workshops, after which the proposal is submitted by a main applicant who has been appointed in phase 2.

A preproposal and application have to be submitted by a main applicant, on behalf of a consortium. A consortium should involve at least a main applicant, co-applicant and cooperation partner.

All consortium members should play an active role in formulating the research questions and in preparing and executing the project. The requirements per consortium member are explained in further detail in paragraph 3.1 of the Call for proposals.

### Consortium building

In this Call for proposals, consortium building is encouraged through the use of synergy workshops. The aim of the synergy workshops is to encourage the formation of a new inter and transdisciplinary consortium that develops a single research project encompassing various areas of knowledge and expertise, thereby attaining maximum results and impact. To this end, different (existing or new) consortia and available knowledge on partial aspects of a complex issue, are brought together. Consortium building and the development of a joint proposal are done under the guidance of external facilitators. For participation in the synergy workshops, a selection is made on the basis of the preapplications, see sections 3.3 and 4.2 of the Call for proposals.

### What to apply for

Within this Call for proposals, a maximum of four preproposals will be selected. The preproposals consist of research proposals that address the four themes described in chapter 2. The selected applicants jointly submit one application, composed of an overarching work package and four thematic work packages. A total of €6,600,000 is available.

At least €2,000,000 is available for the overarching work package. A maximum of €1,150,000 is available for each thematic work package. Depending on the number of preproposals selected, any remaining budget will be added to the overarching work package (phase 2). The budget for the preproposals (phase 1) is released  once the research proposal of the application (phase 2) has been approved. The proposed project has a duration of a minimum of 48 months/4 years and a maximum of 72 months/6 years.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

#### Phase 1 – Preproposal:

1. Problem definition and analysis (40%),
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (40%),
3. Quality of the consortium (20%).

#### Phase 2 – Proposal

1. Problem definition and analysis  (20%),
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%),
3. Quality of the consortium (30%),
4. Quality of the research (30%).
For a detailed description of the specific aspects that are distinguished within these substantive assessment criteria, please refer to paragraph 4.3.1 of the Call for proposals.

### Write proposal

In this Call for proposals, four complementary themes have been identified within which knowledge gaps exist with regards to knowledge security:

1. International context and threats
2. Organisation and vulnerability of knowledge practice
3. Balance (between opportunities and risks) and policy
4. Action perspectives: increasing resilience
**In phase 1** of this Call for proposals, it is mandatory to submit a preproposal. A preproposal is a concise application addressing one of the four themes described above. The preproposal describes which knowledge questions the applicant and co-applicants want to address and how this contributes to the overall objectives and impact described in the Call for proposals. In addition, the preproposal describes which opportunities and leads the applicants anticipate for cooperation with the consortia working on the other themes.

**In phase 2**, one application is developed and submitted by a consortium consisting of parties that have submitted the selected preproposals. In this phase, the selected preproposals serve as starting point and are integrated into a single proposal. The proposal consists of an overarching work package in which an interdisciplinary conceptual framework is developed, which forms the basis to coherently conduct research within the four thematic work packages (preproposals).

### Mandatory consortium-building activities

NWO facilitates the design of the collaboration and integration of the selected preproposals by organising synergy workshops (phase 2). These consist of several sessions between November 2025 and January 2026 led by a facilitator appointed by NWO. It is mandatory for the main applicants of selected preproposals to participate in the synergy workshops in phase 2. It is possible to invite additional participants to the workshops, including stakeholders. The synergy workshops serve to support the joint writing of a single proposal that consists of an overarching work package and thematic work packages. Applicants are also expected to reserve sufficient time and capacity outside the workshops for further development of the research proposal, mutual coordination and writing of the application.
