# NWA Loneliness

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/nwa-loneliness

The Dutch Research Agenda thematic programme “NWA Loneliness” aims to contribute to contribute to a better understanding of the causes and nature of loneliness, contribute to the understanding of effective interventions to reduce loneliness, and contribute to the understanding of the possible taboo of loneliness in society.

## Characteristics

**Status:** Closed

## Explore

### Purpose

We know little about the different forms of loneliness experienced by different groups in society or the causes of loneliness and effective approaches for tackling it. Moreover, the translation of available knowledge into practice, the promotion of expertise among professionals and volunteers, and the development of policy in the field of loneliness are lagging behind. The NWA Loneliness programme aims to change all of this.

### Who can apply

Proposals should be submitted by a consortium in which the various types of research in the knowledge chain and civil society organisations must be represented.

For this call it is important that societal partners  participate in the consortium.

There are four categories of participant within a consortium:

1. Main applicant,
2. Co-applicant(s),
3. Cooperating partner(s),
4. Co-funder(s) (optional).
A consortium must consist of at least a main applicant, co-applicant and cooperating partner. More information about the four categories of participants as well as specific requirements regarding who can apply, can be found in section 3.1 of the call for proposals.

### What to apply for

For this call for proposals, a minimum of €800,000 and a maximum of €970,000 per target group project can be requested for an application in phase I. In phase II, a minimum of €700,000 and a maximum of €835,000 can be requested for the cross-cutting project.

- The maximum duration of a project in phase I is 54 months/4,5 years.
- The maximum duration of a project in phase II is 48 months/four years.
An explanation of the budget modules available can be found in paragraph 3.2 of this Call for proposals.

### When to apply

- The deadline for submitting proposals in phase I is **Thursday 29 June 2023, before 14:00:00 CEST**.
- The deadline for submitting the revised proposal in phase II is **Tuesday 14 October 2025, before 14:00:00 CEST**. **PLEASE NOTE:** This call is open in phase II only to the consortia already granted in phase I.

### Assessment

Proposals in phase I and II will be assessed against the following four criteria:

1. Problem definition and analysis, contribution to a solution (20%)
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%)
3. Quality of the consortium (30%)
4. Quality of the research (30%)
Each criteria consists of several subcriteria, which differ in phase I and phase II. The exact criteria can be found in paragraph 4.2 of the Call for proposals.

### **Procedure**

No referees will be used to evaluate the proposals.

The assessment committee will assess the applications according to the criteria for applications in section 4.2 of the Call for Proposals. During an interview, consortia will be given the opportunity to present their proposal and respond to questions and comments from the Assessment Committee.

The assessment committee discusses all proposals and interviews, assesses the proposals according to the assessment criteria and establishes a prioritisation for allocation within this call.
