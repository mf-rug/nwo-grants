# Senior Expert Programme (SEP) II - round 2

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/senior-expert-programme-sep-ii-round-2

The Netherlands (NL) – CGIAR Senior Expert Programme (SEP) aims to advance knowledge and enhance innovation (knowledge use) for global food and nutrition security through enhancing cooperation between the Netherlands and CGIAR. CGIAR is the largest global agricultural research organization. The call offers senior-level researchers from Dutch knowledge institutions the opportunity to dedicate part of their working time to contribute to one (or more) of the following CGIAR (Science) Programs/Accelerators for 1-3 years: Climate Action; Food Frontiers and Security; Policy Innovation; Scaling for Impact; Capacity Sharing; Digital Transformation; Gender Equality and Inclusion.

### Purpose

The overall goal of the Senior Expert Programme (SEP) is advancing knowledge and enhancing innovation (knowledge use) for global food and nutrition security (SDG2) through cooperation between the Netherlands and CGIAR.

The objectives are:

1. Leveraging Dutch  knowledge and expertise for performance and impact of selected CGIAR (Science) Programs and Accelerators,
2. Leveraging CGIAR expertise, science and networks for Dutch knowledge institutions,
3. Partnering in research and innovation, cooperating in knowledge sharing and capacity strengthening in areas of mutual interest in CGIAR and Dutch policy on global food and nutrition security.

### Who can apply

Senior Experts from Dutch knowledge institutes must comply with the following definition of Senior Expert:

- Senior Experts have at least a MSc/MA,
- Senior experts with a PhD should have at least ten years of demonstrable relevant working experience after the PhD. Reference date is 20 January 2026 so the PhD degree must be obtained no later than 20 January 2016,
- Senior experts with a MSc/MA should have at least fifteen years of demonstrable relevant working experience after the MSc/MA. Reference date is 20 January 2026 so the MSc/MA degree must be obtained no later than 20 January 2011.

### What to apply for

The Senior Expert Programme (SEP) II Round 2 provides the possibility to fund part of the salary costs of Senior Experts from Dutch research organisations in order to spend (additional) time on the selected CGIAR (Science) Program and/or Accelerator. This means that the Dutch research organisation is compensated for the time that the Senior Expert will engage with CGIAR; and the CGIAR organisation will host a (part-time) Senior Expert without (extra) personnel costs.

Per Senior Expert assignment, a grant amount of at most €550.000 for 3 years and for 0,5 fte (50%) can be applied for. The minimum duration of the proposed assignment is 1 year and the maximum duration of the proposed assignment is 3 years. The minimum number of fte that can be applied for is 0,2 (20%) and the maximum is 0,5 (50%). The applicant can include costs for salary, material, and knowledge utilisation.

### When to apply

- The deadline for submitting full proposals is **20 January 2026, before 14:00:00 CET**.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this Call for SEP candidates will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Appropriateness of the Senior Expert to the SEP II,
2. Quality of the proposed assignment,
3. Added value of the proposed assignment.
The criteria carry equal weight and each count for one-third of the final assessment. More information on the assessment criteria can be found in the Call for Proposals section 4.3.

### Procedure

The procedure consists of the following steps:

- submission of the proposal;
- admissibility of the proposal;
- Pre-assessment by the committee;
- Rebuttal;
- Assessment committee meeting;
- Decision by the NWO Executive Board.
An external, independent assessment committee will be assigned for this Call for SEP candidates, consisting of representatives from science and practice with knowledge of the field. Detailed information about the procedure can be found in the Call for Proposals, Section 4.2.

## Prepare

Applications must be submitted through the Isaac application and reporting system. If you do not have an Isaac account yet, then please create one at least one workday in advance. More information about writing a proposal and which forms you need to use can be found in Isaac. Please download and use the forms in Isaac. Below you will find examples of the required forms.

### Write proposals

The application complies with the formal submission conditions of this Call for SEP candidates and responds to the aims of the programme. Applicants have to make use of the instructions and explanatory notes in the application form.

### Fill in forms

The steps involved in writing your application are:

- download the application form from the NWO web application ISAAC or from the NWO web page (on the grant page of the funding instrument concerned);- complete the application form;- save the application form in ISAAC as a PDF file and upload it with any compulsory annexes;
- fill in the requested information online in ISAAC.
Compulsory annexes (please use the format provided on the grant page of the funding instrument on the NWO website):

- budget (needs to be submitted as an excel file in ISAAC);
- narrative CV of the Senior Expert;
- letter of support of the Dutch research organisation;
- letter of support of the hosting CGIAR Research Center. The host CGIAR Research Center depends on the assignment and is identified with the Science Director of the selected (Science) Program and/or Accelerator.
letter of support from the Director of the selected CGIAR (Science) Program(s) and/or Accelerator(s).
