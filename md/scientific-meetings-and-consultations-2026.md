# Scientific Meetings and Consultations 2026

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/scientific-meetings-and-consultations-2026

Full, associate and assistant professors, other researchers with a doctorate, postdocs and PhD candidates with an appointment for the duration of the meeting can submit an application for support for the organisation of scientific meetings or consultations. This funding instrument is open for applications with a research question within or that overlaps with the earth and environmental sciences, astronomy, chemistry, computer science, life sciences, physics and mathematics.

## Characteristics

**Status:** Open calls Continuous  
**For specific groups:** Interdisciplinary Collaboration International Collaboration Starting Researchers  
**Budget:** €600,000  
**Research programme:** Scientific Meetings and Consultations  
**Upcoming submission date:** 5 December 2026 14:00 hrs.  

## Explore

### Purpose

NWO perceives a duty to encourage national and international knowledge networks as well as to promote all forms of monodisciplinary, interdisciplinary and transdisciplinary collaboration, along with collaboration throughout the knowledge chain and knowledge-building, as also indicated in NWO’s 2023-2026 strategy, ‘Science Works!’. The aim of the Scientific Meetings and Consultations programme is to promote academic networking and collaboration for scientific research within the exact sciences and physics, in a national or international context. This may also be in collaboration with other scientific domains. The programme encourages an exchange of research and ideas, as well as the formation of research communities. Through this low-threshold scheme, researchers can apply for financial support to organise (cross-disciplinary) scientific meetings and consultations for the formation and development of new teams and consortia.

### Who can apply

Full, associate and assistant professors, other researchers with a comparable position, postdocs, and PhD candidates may submit an application if they have a paid position, for at least until the meeting or consultation for which funding is requested, at one of the following organisations (see extra conditions in section 3.1 of the Call for proposals):

- Universities as referred to in Article 1.8(1) of the Higher Education and Scientific Research Act and universities listed in the [Policy Rules for Universities in the Kingdom of the Netherlands](https://www.nwo.nl/sites/nwo/files/media-files/Beleidsregel%20Universiteiten%20in%20het%20Koninkrijk%20der%20Nederlanden-21%20februari%202024_0.pdf)
- University medical centres, by which is meant teaching hospitals as referred to in Article 1.13(1) of the Higher Education and Scientific Research Act
- Institutes affiliated with the Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences (KNAW) or NWO
- Netherlands Cancer Institute
- Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics in Nijmegen
- Naturalis Biodiversity Center
- Advanced Research Centre for NanoLithography (ARCNL)
- Princess Máxima Centre

### What to apply for

For a scientific meeting, a maximum contribution of € 15,000 is available. The maximum remuneration per participant is € 60. This must be matched by the organisers of the scientific meeting with at least € 25 per participant. For a scientific consultation, a maximum contribution of € 2,400 is available. The maximum remuneration per participant is € 60.

A maximum of €15,000 is available for a scientific meeting and a maximum of €2,400 is available for a scientific consultation, with a maximum grant amount per eligible participantof €60. The organisation of the scientific meeting or consultation must match the amount of NWO funding requested by at least €25 ‘in cash’ per eligible participant.

Participants eligible for funding are participants in the following categories:

-

- Academic institutions (full, associate and assistant professors, postdocs and PhD students)
- Universities of applied sciences
- Business community
- Government
- Civil society
The funding for both scientific meetings and consultations is intended for facilitation purposes only. This means that NWO will contribute only to costs relating to:

- Rental of facilities (room, studio, technical resources, computer, beamer)
- Accommodation of participants (coffee, tea, lunch, overnight stay)
- Travel and accommodation of guest speakers
- Setup and use of a digital platform on which all or part of the conference takes place, including recording costs

### Assessment

### Criteria

Proposals submitted within this Call for Proposals will be substantively assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

- Suitability to the NWO Domain Science- At least 50% of the intended and/or invited speakers are known*.
- At least 50% of the total intended and/or invited speakers work in one of the Science disciplines.
- At least 50% of the expected academic participants work in one of the Science disciplines.
- At least one applicant works in one of the Science disciplines.
- A scientific meeting or consultation should have a strong focus on Science research. The meeting or consultation’s main topic cannot be knowledge implementation for a patient population, diagnostics and health interventions, technology development or implementation, knowledge implementation in a social, cultural and societal context, or similar.
* This does not apply to speakers chosen through a selection procedure, such as a call for abstracts. This information is necessary to be able to assess Science suitability.

- Suitability of the scientific meeting or consultation to the aim of the call.

### Procedure

Applications can be submitted on a continuous basis. NWO reviews proposals that meet the formal requirements in order of receipt.

NWO will determine this based on various administrative and technical criteria. NWO can only take your proposal into consideration if it meets these conditions. You will be given one opportunity to make these corrections, and you will be given five working days to do so.

NWO does not seek external advice in relation to the assessment of a proposal for a scientific meeting or consultation. The office of the NWO Domain Science (ENW) assesses whether the proposal meets the assessment criteria and draws up advice on the basis of which a decision will be made. Next, the Board of the NWO Domain Science (ENW) reviews the procedure that has been followed and the advice of the office. Finally, it determines the final ratings and makes a decision about awarding or rejecting the proposals.

## Prepare

Applications must be submitted through the Isaac application and reporting system. If you do not have an Isaac account yet, then please create one at least one workday in advance.

### Write proposal

Download the application form and the mandatory form with event information and budget below and provide all of the necessary information. Among other information, the application form should include the (provisional) programme of the scientific meeting or consultation, a description of the extent to which a scientific meeting or consultation contributes to one or more Science disciplines.

If the proposal is multidisciplinary or interdisciplinary, the proposal form must include a description of the extent to which the meeting or consultation contributes to NWO’s other domains; Humanities and Social Sciences, Applied and Engineering Sciences, and Health Research and Development. Contributing to other domains is not required.

### Fill in forms

Save the application form as a pdf file and the budget form as an excel file and upload them both in ISAAC.
