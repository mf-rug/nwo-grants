# Future proof large rivers in the Netherlands

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/future-proof-large-rivers-in-the-netherlands

This call for proposals invites researchers as well as public and private parties to form a consortium to develop knowledge to support policy choices for the river system in the Netherlands. Specifically, the programme calls for research into the possible choices for (spatial) planning of the river system, the effects of these choices and how river functions relate to each other within those choices. It is important to consider the whole system of the river basin, the socio-economic effects of policy choices and the role of waterborne transport.

### Purpose

The goal of this programme is to provide perspectives for potential policy choices concerning the large Dutch rivers. Research financed by this program should investigate the impact which various policy choices might have and how river functions may play into these choices. As such this programme aims to contribute to possible answers to the question of how the Netherlands can best prepare itself for future challenges concerning river systems. These challenges concern the effects of climate change, such as rising sea levels and larger changes in river water levels and quality. In addition, aspects concerning the whole system of the river basin, the socio-economic effects of policy choices and the role of waterborne transport play a significant role.

The Directorate-General for Water and Soil Affairs of the Dutch Ministry of Infrastructure and Water Management is the initiator and is supported in this by the Directorate-General Rijkswaterstaat (DGRWS).

### Who can apply

The main applicant submits the application on behalf of the consortium.

There are four categories of participant within a consortium:

1. Main applicant;
2. Co-applicant(s);
3. Cooperating partner(s);
4. Co-funder(s) (optional).
This Call for proposals has three phases:

1. Registering for collaborative workshops (mandatory for main applicants)
2. Participating in collaborative workshops (mandatory for main applicants or substitution)
3. Submitting a proposal.
More information about the four categories of participants as well as specific requirements regarding who can apply, can be found in paragraph 3.1 of the call for proposals.

### Consortium building

For this programme NWO organizes two subsequent collaborative workshops.

- Attendance is mandatory for the main applicant or a replacement (see section 3.3.2 of the call for proposals for more information).
- Registration for the collaborative workshops is submitted via a form on the NWO website.
NWO explicitly invites both researchers and societal parties to participate in the workshops and to jointly develop proposals.

The aim of collaborative workshops is to bring together ideas, stimulate collaboration and submit a limited number of research proposals by broad (innovative) consortia. The workshops also give participants an overview of the forces at play around a topic. The expertise of the participants and their contribution to the impact on the call topic form the substantive basis of the workshops. The workshops consist of two consecutive meetings in which NWO actively supports the exploration of new collaborations, but the parties ultimately decide for themselves whether and with whom they wish to collaborate. After the workshops, participants will have insight into the possibilities and opportunities for clustering expertise and collaboration, and will further develop their proposals.

Further information about the collaborative workshops can be found here: [Collaborative Workshops ‘Future-proof large rivers in the Netherlands’ (NWA) | NWO](https://www.nwo.nl/en/meetings/collaborative-workshops-future-proof-large-rivers-in-the-netherlands-nwa)

The available budget modules (including maximum amounts) are listed in the Call for proposals. Apply only for what is essential to carry out the project. ### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Problem definition and analysis (20%),
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%),
3. Quality of the consortium (30%),
4. Quality of the research (30%).
Each assessment criterion consists of several subcriteria. More information the criteria can be found in paragraph 4.3 of the call for proposals.

### Procedure

The application procedure consists of the following steps:

- Registering for and participating in collaborative workshops;
- submission of the proposal;
- admissibility of the proposal;
- preliminary advice assessment committee;
- interview selection;
- interview;
- meeting of the assessment committee;
- decision-making.
Detailed information about the procedure can be found in the Call for Proposals, Section 4.2.
