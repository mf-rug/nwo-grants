# SSH Open Competition L 2025

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/ssh-open-competition-l-2025

The SSH Open Competition L funding instrument is open to research proposals with a research question and/or problem definition within the social sciences and humanities. Within this field the instrument has no further thematic limitations and in principle the applicant is free to choose the desired form of funding. The instrument is aimed at excellent disciplinary, interdisciplinary and multidisciplinary research. This certainly also includes fundamental scientific research.

### Purpose

The SSH Open Competition L funding instrument (hereinafter: SSH OC L) for thematically free research focuses on research projects with a primary social and/or humanities research question. Proposals with an interdisciplinary or cross-domain character can also be submitted to this instrument, provided they contain a clear social and/or humanities component.

### Who can apply

The SSH OC L target group consists of experienced researchers who obtained their doctorate at least fifteen years ago with a great fascination for conducting challenging and groundbreaking research. The focus is on curiosity-driven research. Given the aim of the programme, applicants must be able to independently formulate and supervise research.

When submitting your proposal, please ensure that it is submitted to the correct panel. Read more about [the six panels](https://www.nwo.nl/en/overview-panels-domain-social-sciences-and-humanities) within the Social Sciences and Humanities domain.

### What to apply for

This instrument offers the opportunity to apply for various types of personnel costs, material budget, investments in equipment or databases, and knowledge utilization. In addition, funding can also be requested for international cooperation through [Money Follows Corporation](https://www.nwo.nl/en/money-follows-cooperation). See the paragraphs 3.2.1.3 and 7.1.1 in the Call for Proposals.

The domain board expressly invites researchers to make the best possible use of the various possibilities offered by this funding instrument. It is up to the researchers themselves to determine which type of funding best suits their research ambitions.

For an application in this call for proposals, a maximum of 800,000 euros can be applied for. The maximum duration of the proposed project is six years. Request only what is essential to carry out the project. A further explanation of the budget modules can be found in the Call for proposals.

### Assessment

### Criteria

**The pre-proposals submitted within this Call for proposals are substantively assessed on the basis of the following criteria:**

1. Research idea (60%)
2. Appropriateness of the research team (40%)
**The full proposals submitted within this Call for proposals are substantively assessed on the basis of the following criteria:**

1. Research idea, design and impact (80%)
2. Team, embedding and budget (20%)
Please note that the abovementioned criteria also contain subcriteria. The full criteria including the sub criteria for both the pre-proposals as well as the full proposals can be found in the Call for proposals.

### Procedure

The assessment takes place in six disciplinary panels in both the pre-proposal and the full proposal phase of the round. In the pre-proposal phase, the assessment committee composes a written assessment in the form of a preliminary advice, after which the applicants are given the opportunity to respond to the assessments. The final advice on the pre-proposals is submitted to the Domain Board Social Sciences and Humanities, which then takes a decision on the proposals submitted in the pre-proposals phase. Based on this decision, the applicant may or may not submit a full application. In the full proposal phase, the applications are first assessed by external referees, after which the committee also provides the applications with a preliminary advice. The committee then discusses the applications, referee reports and preliminary advice in an assessment meeting. It then advises the domain board on the basis of a ranking of applications drawn up during the meeting. Finally, the domain board reviews the procedure followed and the advice of the assessment committee, determines the final qualifications and decides whether to grant or reject the applications. A specific timeline for the round can be found in the Call for proposals.

### Composition committee of the pre-proposals

Please note that it is possible that not all committee members assessed all proposals, because the NWO Code for Dealing with Personal Interests is applicable to all people involved in formal decisions. This concerns both personal interests as well as (appearance of) bias. Because of this, it can be decided that a committee member will not assess a proposal if there are personal interests or (appearance of) bias.

Please note that the composition of the assessment committee can change between the first and second phase of the assessment procedure. Because of this, committee members who assessed proposals in the first phase, might not participate in the committee in the second phase, and vice versa.

## Prepare

Applications must be submitted through the Isaac application and reporting system. If you do not have an Isaac account yet, then please create one at least one workday in advance. More information about writing a proposal and which forms you need to use can be found in Isaac. Please download and use the forms in Isaac.

### Write proposal

NWO will assess your application against the conditions listed below. Your application will only be admitted to the assessment procedure if it meets these conditions. After submitting your application, NWO requests you to be available to implement any possible administrative corrections so that you can (still) meet the conditions for submission.
These conditions are:

- the main applicant and possible co-applicant meet the conditions stated in Section 3.1;
- the application complies with the DORA guidelines as described in Section 4.1;
- the application form is, after a possible request to make additions or changes, complete and filled out according to the instructions;
- the application is submitted via the main applicant’s ISAAC account;
- the application is received before the deadline;
- the application is written in English;
- the application budget is drawn up in accordance with the conditions for this Call for proposals (budget form only to be submitted in the full proposal phase);
- the proposed project has a duration of at least one year and at most six years;
- all of the required annexes are, after a possible request to make additions or changes, complete and filled out according to the instructions.

### Fill in forms

###### The steps involved in writing your application are:

- download the application form from the NWO web application ISAAC or from the NWO web page (on the grant page of the funding instrument concerned);
- complete the application form;
- save the application form in ISAAC as a PDF file and upload it with any compulsory annexes;
- fill in the requested information online in ISAAC.

###### Compulsory annexe(s):

**Pre-proposal phase:**

- Statement appointment and project supervision (if applicable).
**Full proposal phase:**

- budget form;
- statement co-funding (if applicable).
In case NWO has made a template available, the annexes should be drawn up according to the NWO template. Annexes must be uploaded in ISAAC separately from the application. All of the annexes, except for the budget, must be submitted as PDF files (without encryption). The budget must be submitted in ISAAC as an Excel file. Any annexes other than those above-mentioned are not permitted.
