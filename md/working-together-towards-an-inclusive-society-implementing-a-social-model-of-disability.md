# Working Together Towards an Inclusive Society: Implementing a Social Model of Disability

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/working-together-towards-an-inclusive-society-implementing-a-social-model-of-disability

This Call for proposals calls for experts by experience, other societal stakeholders and knowledge institutions to jointly develop scientific and practice-oriented research proposals aimed at creating a more inclusive society for the diverse group of people with disabilities. The consortia are interdisciplinary and knowledge chain-wide in composition and work across disabilities.

### Purpose

This program aims to contribute to a more inclusive society that gives full and meaningful access to people with disabilities. This is based on a social perspective on disability, focusing on the design of society. Experiential knowledge, practical knowledge and scientific knowledge are linked. This identifies causes of exclusion, assigns responsibilities and formulates and tests possible solutions.

### Who can apply

An application must be submitted by a consortium. A consortium should consist of at least one main applicant, co-applicant and collaboration partner.

All participants should play an active role in formulating the research questions and in the design and implementation of the project. Ongoing input from experts by experience throughout the project is mandatory.

The conditions per participant are further explained in the Call for proposals.

### Consortium building

Prior to phase I, a matchmaking meeting is organised. On the one hand, this method contributes to bringing together both researchers (from different disciplines) and (representatives of) experts by experience and other societal stakeholders. On the other hand, it encourages to team up per benchmark and to think strategically about cooperation.

The matchmaking meeting will take place on Monday 27 October 2025.

### What to apply for

In phase I, €520,000 is available per benchmark project. In phase II, €785,000 is available for the overarching project.

The intended duration of all projects together (phase I and phase II) is 5 years. The maximum duration for projects in phase I is 4 years and the maximum duration for projects in phase II is 3 years. This means that phase I and phase II will overlap for two years, allowing for interaction between phases I and II.

The applicant and co-applicants can include costs for personnel, equipment, investments and knowledge utilisation. The available budget modules (including maximum amounts) are listed in the Call for proposals. Apply only for what is essential to carry out the project. The rates and an explanation of these budget modules are given in Annex 7.1 of the Call for proposals.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Problem posed and problem analysis
2. Expected impact and route to impact
3. Quality of the consortium
4. Quality of the research
Criteria 1 and 2 each carry a weighting of 20% in the overall assessment, and criteria 3 and 4 each carry a weighting of 30%.

### Procedure

The deadline for submitting proposals in phase I is Tuesday 10 February 2026, before 14:00:00 CEST. For the assessment of the proposals, the committee will make use of pre-advices and interviews will be conducted. A final decision on funding by the NWO Executive Board is expected in June 2026.

The deadline for submitting the overarching proposal is Tuesday 8 February 2028, before 14:00:00 CEST. For the assessment of the proposal, the committee will make use of pre-advices and an opportunity for response. A final decision on funding by the NWO Executive Board is expected in June 2028.

An application is prepared by a consortium that has the necessary expertise, experience and practical knowledge required to carry out the project.
