# Technology for innovative nuclear reactors and fuels

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/technology-for-innovative-nuclear-reactors-and-fuels

This call is part of the multi-year Dutch Nuclear Energy Research Programme aimed at strengthening and future-proofing the Dutch knowledge base regarding nuclear energy. The focus of this call lies specifically on nuclear reactor and fuel technology. Research organisations, companies, and other organisations are invited to work together on innovative scientific challenges on these themes.

## Explore

### Purpose

Nuclear energy is expected to play an important role in the energy system of the future as a stable, affordable and reliable supplement to wind and solar energy and as a low-carbon energy source. This call for proposals is part of the national nuclear energy research program and focuses on the development of Generation IV reactors, advanced modular reactors (AMR’s) and microreactors, and new fuel concepts. In addition, three cross-cutting themes are central to this call for proposal: reactor safety, materials research, and monitoring systems.

### Who can apply

For this call for proposals, one consortium submits a single joint application.

There are four different categories of participants for the consortium:

1. Main applicant
2. Co-applicant(s)
3. Co-funder(s)
4. Collaboration partner(s)
The main applicant and co-applicants may include professors, associate professors, professors of applied sciences, and other researchers with comparable positions from universities, universities of applied sciences, and TO2 institutions.

A co-funder is a party that participates in the consortium and contributes cash co-funding and/or in-kind support to the project. A collaboration partner is a party that does not receive funding and does not provide co-funding for the application but is closely involved in the execution of the research and/or knowledge utilization.

### Consortium building

Consortium building takes places during two two-day workshops. The workshops are mandatory for the main applicant of the consortium. Co-applicants, cofinanciers and collaborating partners preferably also take part in the workshops. Signing up for the workshop can be done via ISAAC.

### What to apply for

For the application under this call for proposals, a maximum of €6,100,000 can be requested from NWO. NWO funds up to 95% of the project. The remaining part of the project budget must be covered through mandatory co-funding, which can be provided either cash or in kind. Funding can be used for, among other things, personnel and material costs. The project may have a maximum duration of 6 years.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The proposal will be evaluated based on the following criteria:

- Problem definition and analysis (20%)
- Expected impact and pathway to impact (20%)
- Quality of the consortium (30%)
- Quality of the research (30%)

### Procedure

The procedure for this Call for Proposals consists of three steps:

1. Registration of organizations for the workshops (deadline: 29 January 2026, 14:00 CET)
2. Workshops for consortium formation (2–3 March 2026, 30–31 March 2026)
3. Preparation and submission of the application (deadline: 30 June 2026, 14:00 CEST)
After submission, the application will be evaluated by the assessment committee, which will advise the NWO executive Board on the approval or rejection of funding. The NWO executive Board will make a decision in the fourth quarter of 2026.

### Write proposal

Organisations who wish to participate in the application within this call for proposals are required to submit an Application form Workshops to participate in the procedure. For each organisation, a maximum of one Application form Workshops may be submitted. This means a maximum of one Application form Workshops for the entire organisation at the highest level (e.g. at university level and not at faculty or department level).

The main applicant submits the proposal via the NWO web application ISAAC. During the assessment process, NWO will communicate with the main applicant.

### Mandatory consortium-building activities

In this call for proposals casco-workshops are organised to guide the process of forming a consortium and defining the outline of the application. Participants do not attend the workshop on their own behave, but represent their organisation.
