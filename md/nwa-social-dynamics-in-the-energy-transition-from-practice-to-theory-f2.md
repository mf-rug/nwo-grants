# NWA Social dynamics in the energy transition. From practice to theory F2

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/nwa-social-dynamics-in-the-energy-transition-from-practice-to-theory-f2

This thematic NWA programme focuses on research into the behaviour of groups of people and the energy transition. The energy transition is urgent and developments in this field are in full swing. There is a need for practical, scientific tools that support policy development based on knowledge on social dynamics and help policymakers anticipate and respond to unpredictable or surprising behaviours.

## Explore

### Purpose

The knowledge this NWA programme seeks aims to develop must offer different levels of government ways of getting a grip on the unpredictability of such dynamics among end users, in order to better understand and hence better respond to them. If we want to accelerate the energy transition within the Dutch context – the ultimate urgent aim of this programme – we have a real need for practical hands-on knowledge about the exchange between, on the one hand, policy development and implementation by different levels of government (local, regional and national) and, on the other, the capacity of those in society to act.

The programme seeks to deliver practical usable insights and prospects for taking action that different levels of government can use to develop inclusive and equitable policies that take account of the interests and concerns of end users. The main thrust is to sponsor research into social dynamics within the context of the government’s ambitions for the energy transition that adds to existing knowledge about the individual behaviour of end users.

### What to apply for

For this Call for Proposals, a total of €2,855,000 is available.

A maximum of one application will be awarded within this Call for proposals. This Call for proposals has two phases:

1. Submitting a pre-proposal.
2. Submitting a full proposal.
For this call, a maximum of three pre-proposals will be selected. The pre-proposals must present practical case studies. The selected sub-consortia are required to submit a single full proposal, made up of an overarching research project and practical case studies.

For this Call for proposals, in phase 1, a maximum of €500,000 can be requested per sub-consortium for research on practical cases. For the overarching research project (phase 2), a minimum of €1,355,000 is available.

The three knowledge gaps identified by this Call are:

1. Drivers, catalysts and triggers of social dynamics
2. Involving end users in an inclusive energy transition
