# Venture Challenge Fall 2026

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/venture-challenge-fall-2026

The Venture Challenge helps starting entrepreneurs in life sciences & health to bring their service or product to the market. In a programme of eight to ten weeks, starters receive support in setting up a company and developing their idea into a solid business plan. Hereby, the Venture Challenge aims to shorten the time-to-market. This call concerns the spring edition 2026.

## Explore

### Purpose

Many scientific findings never reach the market. Researchers often do not have the experience and knowledge needed to set up a commercial organization and to come to a market-ready product or service. It is usually too early for private investors and experienced entrepreneurs to step in during this start-up phase, for example because the business case is not yet sufficiently clear to them. The Venture Challenge helps by developing a well-thought out business plan together with the researchers. This provides insight into what is needed to bring the invention to the market.

### Who can apply

The Venture Challenge is intended for teams of at least three and a maximum of four people who are planning to bring a product or service in the field of life sciences & health to the market as a new start-up. The product or service is based on a new technological discovery or a new application of existing technology, grounded in scientific research. More specifically, it concerns a new therapy or medicine, diagnostics, medical device or a technology for improving existing - or accelerating the development of new - therapies or medicines. The team consists of the person who made the scientific discovery and the other founders of the start-up. If required, it can be supplemented with relevant advisors. For example, this can be a business developer from an involved Knowledge Transfer Office.

### What to apply for

An edition consists of two three-day bootcamps and regular coaching sessions. Participants work under the supervision of experienced coaches, to set up or improve their business plan. The supervision can possibly be expanded with experts and entrepreneurs with a background in life sciences & health. The entire process takes eight to ten weeks. At the end, the participants pitch their plans in front of a jury, including experienced entrepreneurs and investors. The selected candidates pay a personal contribution and receive funding for participating in the Venture Challenge, to be spent in the upcoming edition. The personal contribution for participation per team is:

- 2,000 euros when 3 team members participate;
- 2,500 euros when 4 team members participate.
**The following dates are important:**

- 18-20 November 2026: First bootcamp;
- 20-22 January 2027: Second bootcamp;
- 10 February 2027: Pitches for the jury.

### When to apply

- Registration for the spring edition opens **5 May 2026 and closes on 2 July 2026 14:00:00 CET**.

### Assessment

### Criteria

- the commercial perspective of the new product or process or service;
- the innovative character of the new product or process or service;
- the perspective of the competitive position of the (future) Venture Challenge startup;
- the financial potential of the (future) Venture Challenge startup;
- the quality of the Venture Challenge team, considering the extent to which members complement each other, the members’ capacities and the allocation of roles within the team;
- the added value of participation in the Venture Challenge program for the (future) Venture Challenge startup.

### Procedure

If NWO takes your application into consideration, then an interview can be part of the application process. In that case, you will be given the opportunity to explain your proposal to the advisory committee which NWO appoints to assess the applications. Based on the committee's advice, NWO will decide which VC teams will be awarded a grant.

**The timetable for the procedure is as follows:**

- 2 July 2026 14:00:00 CET - Deadline full proposals
- 16-17 September 2026 - Interviews
- 16 October 2026 - Decision awards and rejections
- 21 October 2026 - Announcement awards

## Prepare

Applications must be submitted through the Isaac application and reporting system. If you do not have an Isaac account yet, then please create one at least one workday in advance. More information about writing a proposal and which forms you need to use can be found in Isaac. Please download and use the forms in Isaac. Below you will find examples of the required forms.

### Write proposal

In the application form you are asked about the goal of the startup, the proposition and to what extent it has been validated, a market analysis, competitive position, and the team.

### Fill in forms

Download the application form and complete the form. The maximum size of the application is 10 pages (excluding the ‘Innovatieadviesdienstverklaring’ mentioned below).

If the application is submitted by a start-up, then a so-called ‘Innovatieadviesdienstverklaring’ from the start-up should be appended to the application. If the application is submitted by a team without a start-up, then each team member should complete and add an innovation advisory service statement.

**Please note:** there are two different forms; which one you need to fill in depends on whether there is already a company established when you register for the Venture Challenge. You can read more in the ‘Toelichting’ (see the appendix to the form). Please note: The ‘innovatieadviesdienstveklaring’-forms are changed compared the last Venture Challenge round.
