# Tulip Fund

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/tulip-fund

Academic freedom is under mounting pressure on a global scale. The Tulip Fund offers international top researchers and top talents with their own line of research the opportunity to continue their research in the Netherlands.

## Characteristics

**Status:** Open calls Continuous  
**Budget:** €50,000,000  
**Research programme:** Tulip Fund  

## Explore

### Purpose

The objective of this fund is to enable research organisations to recruit top researchers and top talents from abroad who want to continue their research in the Netherlands due to growing threats to academic freedom.

This will both boost and reinforce the Netherlands’ position at the forefront of international scientific research. The research carried out by these researchers will lead to scientific and social strengthening of the Netherlands and Europe. The research may be fundamental, applied or practice-oriented. There are no restrictions on the content.

### Who can apply

Each of the research organisations in the Netherlands listed below may nominate a maximum of two candidates for each position via their board, for funding amounts of up to €1,000,000:

- public and private universities and the Open University;
- university medical centres;
- NWOi on behalf of NWO institutes (jointly);
- KNAW on behalf of institutes affiliated with KNAW (jointly).
Each of the research organisations listed below may nominate a maximum of one candidate for each position via their board, for funding amounts of up to €1,000,000:

- Netherlands Cancer Institute;
- NCB Naturalis (Naturalis Biodiversity Center).
The Netherlands Association of Universities of Applied Sciences can jointly nominate candidates for up to four positions, for funding amounts of up to €500,000.

The boards of the research institutions should confirm the nomination by means of the required letter of submission. The technical submission in ISAAC should be made by a board member of the board of the nominating research organization, or a person mendated by this member. This person is the first point of contact for the programme.

### What to apply for

A maximum of €1,000,000 in funding is available to the research organisation for each researcher; a maximum of €500,000 in funding is available to the research organisation for each researcher nominated by the Netherlands Association of Universities of Applied Sciences. The funding must be spent in five years. The funding must be used for the benefit of science in the Netherlands. The researcher enjoys broad freedom of spending, such as on personnel (scientific and non-scientific), equipment, investments, travel and accommodation expenses, knowledge utilization).

### When to apply

- The deadline for submitting nominations is provisionally set at **31 March 2026, 14:00:00 CEST**. Submissions are accepted continuously.

### Assessment

### Criteria

It should be clear from the nomination that the researcher fits within the defined target group. A top researcher is an established researcher (conducting fundamental, applied or practice-oriented research) at the stage of consolidation of their own research group. A top talent is a researcher who is ready to establish a research group or expand a recently established research group.

It should be clear from the nomination that the researcher’s planned research activities are aligned with relevant strategic knowledge agendas in the Netherlands and/or the EU such as – but not confined to – strategic priorities of research organisations, NWA or KIC.

### Procedure

The nomination procedure consists of the following steps:

- submission of the nomination;
- admissibility check of the nomination;
- assessment of the assessment committee;
- meeting of the assessment committee;
- rebuttal in case of a negative recommendation;
- decision making.
For this fund, an external, independent assessment committee will be installed, consisting of representatives from science with knowledge of diverse academic fields. The task given to this assessment committee is to assess the submitted nominations and thereto pertaining documents, in light of its individual merits, based on the assessment criteria outlined in the Call for proposals.

## Prepare

Nominations must be submitted through the Isaac application and reporting system. If you do not have an Isaac account yet, then please create one at least one workday in advance. More information about writing a nomination and which forms you need to use can be found in Isaac. Please download and use the forms in Isaac. Below you will find examples of the required forms.

### Write nomination

To write your proposal, you make use of the nomination form. This form can be downloaded from ISAAC or this webpage. The nomination form consists of (among others):

- A description of the quality of the researcher and their projected contribution to Dutch/European science (max. 3 pages);
- A description of the researcher’s 10 most important key outputs (max. 1000 words);
- The researcher’s CV (max. 4 pages).
Furthermore, you are required to attach a letter of submission of the board of the nominating research institute. If applicable, in this letter a mandate can be given to another person to serve as the first point of contact for the nomination.

Both the nomination and the letter of submission need to be written in English and make use of the NWO-supplied forms.
