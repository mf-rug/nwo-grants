# Treatments and adherence to treatment protocols (OH-TREAT)

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/treatments-and-adherence-to-treatment-protocols-oh-treat

OH-TREAT actively works to improve the treatment of bacterial and fungal infections by developing innovative therapies while simultaneously reducing the risk of microbial resistance.

## Explore

### Purpose

Drug-resistant infections are a global problem that lead to higher mortality, slower recovery, and reduced food productivity. This first Call for Proposals of the European Partnership OHAMR invites international consortia to contribute to the success rates of patients/animals/plants affected by bacterial or fungal infections by offering new treatment options and reducing the risk of resistance.

### Who can apply

Applications need to be submitted by research consortia including teams of at least a minimum of 3 countries participating in the call, including a minimum of two from EU Member States or EU Associated Countries. See the Call for proposals for details and participating countries.

Researchers may submit an application (i.e. participate in a consortium and request NWO funding) if they have a tenured position (and therefore a paid position for an indefinite period) or a tenure track agreement at one of the organisations listed in the national rules and requirements for NWO in the Call for proposals.

Persons with a zero-hour employment agreement or with a contract for a limited period of time (other than a tenure track appointment) may not submit a proposal.

An applicant may only request NWO funding for one project (part of a European consortium) in this call.

More information on the transnational eligibility criteria and the eligibility criteria of other countries can be found in the Call for Proposals.

For a proposal in this call, a maximum of 400,000 euro can be applied for at NWO. The maximum project duration is 3 years. ### Assessment

### Criteria

Pre-proposals and full proposals will be reviewed on the basis of the following 3 main criteria:

- Excellence
- Impact
- Quality and efficiency of the implementation
Please go to the Call for proposals for more information about the criteria.

### Procedure

The assessment in this call will be done in two stages. In the first stage consortia are invited to submit a preproposal. Only consortia that were successful in the first stage can submit a full proposal. Eligible full proposals will be assessed by external evaluators consisting both of scientific and policy/management experts. The proposals will be ranked based on the criteria in the call text. Based on the ranking and the budget available, the national and regional funding agencies will take the funding decision. Full proposals recommended for funding will undergo an ethics review organized by OHAMR. At NWO, the Executive Board will take the national funding decision.

### Write proposal

Proposals can be submitted under one of the three topics central to OH-TREAT:

- Identifying and developing new combination therapies using existing or innovative antimicrobial agents, or antimicrobial agents in combination with additional treatments to enhance drug efficacy and combat resistance.
- Developing tools and methods to improve adherence to treatment protocols.
- Assessing the impact of antimicrobial agents used in veterinary medicine and agriculture on the risk of AMR transfer to humans and the environment to develop policies for limiting certain antimicrobial agents for human use.
