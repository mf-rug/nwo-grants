# KIN Knowledge in Action - Watertransition Twente: transition towards a more resilient water system

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/kin-knowledge-in-action-watertransition-twente-transition-towards-a-more-resilient-water-system

Through its programmes, KIN mobilises knowledge and science to support transitions that will make the Netherlands climate-neutral and climate-resilient. Achieving this requires fundamental and long-term societal change that is fair and just for all.

The Knowledge-in-Action grant brings together (action)researchers and societal actors—such as entrepreneurs, practice pioneers, creative professionals and entrepreneurial policy-makers—to work jointly in co-creation on just climate transitions. Knowledge institutions and societal organisations submit joint applications for transformative, experimental intervention projects, in which practical experience and scientific knowledge and methods mutually reinforce one another.

Because in complex transition challenges it is not possible to determine in advance which interventions will be effective, or why, the Knowledge-in-Action grant explicitly creates space for experimentation with a strong focus on action. Through a cyclical and learning-oriented process of testing, researching and adapting—learning by doing and doing by learning—project partners collectively explore potential solution pathways, research questions and transition learning goals. Together, they develop relevant knowledge and concrete perspectives for action that contribute to accelerating just climate transitions.

In this Knowledge-in-Action grant, one urgent regional question is central: How can the water transition in Twente be designed in such a way that – given increasing drought, historical choices and other societal transitions – a resilient and equitable water system emerges?
