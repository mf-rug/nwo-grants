# KIC Nature-inclusive decision making in business

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/kic-nature-inclusive-decision-making-in-business

The role of biodiversity monitoring in the Agriculture, Horticulture, Water and Food Sectors

Researchers are invited to collaborate with businesses on an interdisciplinary research plan that links decision-making within the Agriculture, Horticulture, Water and Food (LWV) sectors to biodiversity monitoring. The interdisciplinary research plans enable companies to make better-informed decisions around measures that have an impact on biodiversity, or gain insight into maximum returns for investments in biodiversity.

### Purpose

Companies active within the Agriculture, Horticulture, Water and Food (LWV) sectors can only work on restoring biodiversity if they know the expected impact of measures. A good understanding of this impact gives companies a choice of available courses of action. This requires researchers to develop integrated monitoring techniques and data management for biodiversity, specifically for business within the LWV sectors. The aim of the call is form an interdisciplinary research plan in cooperation with the business community in which decision-making is linked to biodiversity monitoring. The expected economic impact of the research includes enabling nature-inclusive business practices and new business models around biodiversity monitoring.

### Who can apply

Pre-proposals and full proposals are submitted by the main applicant and one or more co-applicants. Full, associate and assistant professors, lectors and other researchers with similar positions may act as main or co-applicants if they have a tenured position (and therefore have a paid employment contract for an indefinite period of time) or have a tenure track agreement. A proposal  is written by a consortium in which besides the applicants co-funders are involved as well. Details on who may apply are described in section 3.1 of the call text.

### What to apply for

For an application in this Call for proposals, a minimum of €750,000 a maximum of €1,750,000 can be applied for. The grant ceiling for the call for proposals totals € 7.000.000. With this, NWO finances maximally 90% of the total project budget; the rest of the funding must be contributed by the compulsory co-funding. The maximum duration of the proposed project is 6 years.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The applications submitted within this Call for proposals will be substantially assessed on the basis of the following criteria:

1. Problem posed and problem analysis, contribution to solution
2. Expected impact and route to impact
3. Quality of the consortium
4. Quality of the research
Criteria 1 and 2 each carry a weighting of 20% in the overall assessment, and criteria 3 and 4 each carry a weighting of 30%.
