# NGF: Hydrogen & green electrons for carbon-based chemistry

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/ngf-hydrogen-green-electrons-for-carbon-based-chemistry

This call is part of the R&D programme GroenvermogenNL, financed by the National Growth Fund. This current Call for proposals concerns NGF: Hydrogen & green electrons for carbon-based chemistry, GroenvermogenNL WP4, and focuses on reducing the carbon footprint of the (petro)chemical industry through the use of hydrogen and green electrons.

## Explore

### Purpose

This Call for Proposals intends to come to a grant proposal for the realization of work package 4 of GroenvermogenNL: “NGF: Hydrogen & green electrons for carbon-based chemistry. Through workshops, a consortium will be formed which focusses on innovations to make the carbon-based chemical industry more sustainable. Due to the use of green hydrogen and green electrons the greenhouse gas emissions of the chemical industry will decline.

### Who can apply

1. Researchers from universities, universities of applied and TO2 institutions.
2. Employees of businesses and other public and private organisations provided that:- They have a tenured position for at least 0.6 fte;
- Their organisation has demonstrable Research & Development (R&D) activities in the Netherlands;
- They hold a master’s degree.

### What to apply for

In this Call for proposals, a maximum of €35,317,355 can be applied for. This amount will be supplemented by the businesses and civil society organisations in the consortium with ‘Funding other than that provided by NWO’.

**‘Funding other than that provided by NWO’ consists of:**

1. The own contribution, i.e. the non-fundable part of the eligible costs contributed by businesses and civil society organisations, plus;
2. Possible in cash (excl. Dutch VAT) or in-kind co-funding.
Financing can amongst other be used for personal and material costs.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The assessment of the full proposal is based on satisfying the ‘Framework for State aid for research and development and innovation’ and the GBER, as well as the quality of the consortium and the research plan.

### Procedure

**The procedure for this Call for proposals consists of 3 steps:**

1. Registration of organisations for the workshops (12 November 2024)
2. Workshops (December 2024/January 2025)
3. Developing grant proposal (deadline 25 March 2025)
After submission, the proposal will be assessed by the selection committee, which will advise the NWO Domain Science Board about whether or not to award funding (End of June 2025).
