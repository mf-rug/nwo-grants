# Isaac Newton Group Telescopes 26A

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/isaac-newton-group-telescopes-26a

Astronomers in the Netherlands are invited to apply for observation time at the Isaac Newton Group of Telescopes on La Palma (Canary Islands, Spain).

## Characteristics

**Status:** Closed  
**Closing date full application:** 2 October 2025 14:00 hrs.  
**Finance type:** Infrastructure  
**Research programme:** Observation time on the Isaac Newton Group of Telescopes  

## Explore

### Purpose

Astronomers can apply for observing time at the Isaac Newton Group of Telescopes (ING) on La Palma. The ING consists of the 4.2 m William Herschel Telescope (WHT) and the 2.5 m Isaac Newton Telescope (INT).

### Who can apply

Researchers in astronomy with a Dutch affiliation.

### What to apply for

Dutch observing time at WHT for observations with WEAVE (LIFU mode) to be carried out between 1 May and 31 October 2026.

Proposals are invited for observations with WEAVE@WHT’s large integral-field unit (LIFU) in low- and/or high-resolution modes. In semester 2026A it is also possible to apply for target-of-opportunity observations. The available Dutch observing time for open time proposals with WEAVE is approximately 100 hours per semester.

###### Cannot be applied for via this call:

1. Open-time observations with WEAVE in MOS mode. Once MOS mode is commissioned, ING may issue a special call for open-time proposals.
2. Observing time with the HARPS3 spectrograph at the INT. ING expects to offer HARPS3 in open time in semester 2026A or 2026B. A dedicated delta-call will be issued following successful commissioning and science verification of the instrument.
For more information, see [https://www.ing.iac.es/astronomy/instr.html](https://www.ing.iac.es/astronomy/instr.html) .

### When to apply

- The next deadline is 2 October 2025. This is the deadline for observing time with WEAVE (LIFU mode)@WHT to be carried out between 1 May and 31 October 2026.
- Each year, the deadline for submitting proposals for observing time in Semester A will be in the second half of September.
- Each year, the deadline for submitting proposals for observing time in Semester B will be in the second half of March.

### Assessment

### Criteria

Proposals will be assessed by the ING Programme Committee of NWO. Because proposals for observing time come from many different fields of research, we recommend you to keep in mind that not all members of the Programme Committee are experts in your specific area of research. Please include the necessary background information and clearly formulate the relevance of your science question and how the requested data will contribute to answering this science question.

### Procedure

The Programme Committee of NWO assesses proposals for Dutch observing time. International proposals are assessed by a different committee.

In general the telescope organisation will inform you on the success of your proposal approximately three months after submission. Around the same time, you will also receive feedback from the Programme Committee.
