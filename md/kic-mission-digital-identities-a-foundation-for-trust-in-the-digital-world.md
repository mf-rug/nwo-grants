# KIC Mission Digital Identities - A Foundation for Trust in the Digital World

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/kic-mission-digital-identities-a-foundation-for-trust-in-the-digital-world

This Call for proposals focuses on increasing digital trust with digital identities for individuals, legal entities and objects. We invite researchers to submit initiatives and proposals that they have formulated in partnership with researchers from other relevant disciplines and relevant organisations in society, including businesses. This will involve looking beyond the boundaries of scientific domains (humanities, exact sciences, social sciences).

## Explore

### Purpose

The proposed programme builds on the Breed Gedragen Programma Decentrale Technologie (Broadly Supported Decentralised Technology Programme) launched by the Top Sectors.  As cyber security and federative data systems are already covered by other research programmes, these elements are not included in this research programme.

The aim of this Call for proposals is to generate knowledge and develop the most important elements of digital identities for individuals, legal entities and objects. The objective is to establish and maintain digital trust across society by developing and standardising digital identities. This will involve tackling not just challenges related to technological development and standardisation, but also societal challenges related to regulation and social acceptance.

### Who can apply

Initiatives and proposals are submitted by the main applicant and one or more co-applicants. A proposal is written by a consortium in which besides the applicants other participants are involved as well.

Four categories of participants can be distinguished in the consortium:

1. Main applicant,
2. Co-applicant(s),
3. Co-funder(s),
4. Collaborating partner(s).
In this Call for proposals it is mandatory to have a minimum of two co-funders participate in the project. The co-funders jointly contribute a net minimum amount of 20% of the total budget for the proposal.

### What to apply for

The grant ceiling for the call for proposals totals € 7.000.000. For an application in this Call for proposals, a minimum of €600.000 and a maximum of €3.200.000 can be applied for in total. With this, NWO finances maximally 80% of the total project budget; the rest of the funding must be contributed by the compulsory co-funding. NWO never funds less than 50% of the total project budget.

### Assessment

### Criteria

1. Problem definition and analysis (20%),
2. Envisaged impact and route to impact (20%),
3. Quality of the consortium (30%),
4. Quality of the research (30%).

### Procedure

The submission of an initiative is mandatory for this Call for proposals. With an initiative you indicate that you have a potential contribution to or an idea for a (sub)project within this Call for proposals and intend to submit an application for this Call for proposals.

Applications will be provided with feedback by referees. Applicants will have the opportunity to submit a rebuttal to this. Subsequently, the applications are assessed by the assessment committee.

The assessment committee offers its recommendation to the NWO Executive Board for decision-making. In November 2025, the Executive Board determines the final assessment of the applications.
