# KIC Long-term programmes 2024

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/kic-long-term-programmes-2024

NWO invites researchers and partners to organise consortia that will set out to do innovative research and develop solutions for societal issues, while realising economic potential, to submit an application for a long-term programme. Long-term programmes aim towards impact throughout the course of the programme, as well as lasting beyond the funding period.

### Purpose

By funding the research projects that are the scientific foundation of the long-term programmes, NWO facilitates the development of strategic consortia consisting of researchers, public and private partners, that will set out or continue their collaboration throughout this ten-year programme. Tne ten-year collaboration within the long-term programme is the starting point of a long-term, forceful and sustainable stimulus of innovation and the national development of a scientific field in the Netherlands. A long-term programme is initiated with the aim and structure to continue after the initial funding of this LTP.

### Who can apply

Proposals are submitted by the main applicant and two or more co-applicants. Full, associate and assistant professors, lectors and other researchers with similar positions may act as main or co-applicants if they have a tenured position (and therefore have a paid employment contract for an indefinite period of time) or have a tenure track agreement. A proposal  is written by a consortium in which besides the applicants co-funders are involved as well.

Participation of research organisations is maximised, research organisations are allowed to apply as main applicant twice.

More details on who can apply and additional conditions for eligibility can be found in the call for proposals.

### What to apply for

For this call for proposals, a consortium can apply for a minimum of €9.000.000 and a maximum of €25.000.000 of NWO funding. NWO funds a maximum of 30% of the total LTP budget. The other 70% is contributed in joint funding by the consortium partners.

### When to apply

- The deadline for submission of an (obligatory) initiative was **15 April 2025, 14:00 hours**.
- The deadline for submission of an application is **14 October 2025, 14:00 hours**.
The deadline for submitting a (mandatory) initiative was 15 April 2025, 14:00. No more initiatives can be submitted now, but interested researchers can still join the consortia that registered before this deadline.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The application will be assessed on the criteria from the call for proposals:

1. Problem statement and analysis, and strategic importance
2. Expected impact and route to impact
3. Quality of the consortium
4. Quality of the research

### Procedure

The application procedure consists of the following steps:

- submission of a statement of intent;
- submission of the proposal;
- admissibility of the proposal;
- peer review by referees;
- rebuttal;
- pre-advice assessment committee;
- interview;
- assessment committee meeting;
- decision-making.

## Prepare

Applications must be submitted through the Isaac application and reporting system. If you do not have an Isaac account yet, then please create one at least one workday in advance. More information about writing a proposal and which forms you need to use can be found in Isaac. Please download and use the forms in Isaac.

To be eligible to submit an application, you have to register an initiative (statement of intent). This will be possible on the NWO website from January 2025, a weblink will be made available.

### Write proposal

Given the characteristic collaboration that is required in the LTP, we recommend that consortiumpartners are included and involved in the writing process of the application. Please also note that the application will have to adhere to additional terms, as well as those that are customary in all NWO KIC calls. For these, we refer to chapter 3.4 in the call for proposals, please inform yourself about these.
