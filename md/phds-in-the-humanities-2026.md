# PhDs in the Humanities 2026

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/phds-in-the-humanities-2026

The aim of the PhDs in the Humanities funding instrument is to provide research talent with the opportunity to carry out an independent PhD project.  The faculties of humanities at the respective universities are responsible for organizing the pre-selection for this programme.

## Characteristics

**Status:** Closed  
**Closing date full application:** 17 February 2026 14:00 hrs.  
**For specific groups:** Starting Researchers  
**Budget:** €4,000,000  
**Research programme:** PhDs in the Humanities  

## Explore

### Purpose

PhDs in the Humanities offers talented researchers a paid PhD position.

### Who can apply

Applications can only be submitted for candidates who have been selected by the deans of the humanities faculties from the following universities:

- Erasmus University Rotterdam
- Leiden University
- Maastricht University
- Open University
- Protestant Theological University
- Radboud University Nijmegen
- Theological University Apeldoorn
- Theological University Utrecht
- Tilburg University
- University of Amsterdam
- University of Aruba
- University of Curaçao
- University of Groningen
- University of Humanistic Studies
- University of St. Martin
- Utrecht University
- VU University Amsterdam
For further information about the pre-selection within the faculties, please consult the contact persons named in the call for proposals.

### What to apply for

A grant can be applied for to cover a standard PhD research project (4 years, 1 FTE or 5 years, 0.8 FTE). The grant covers the personnel costs of the PhD. Material costs for research are remunerated to a limited extent.

### When to apply

This funding round takes place once a year.

- The deadline for submitting an application is **17 February 2026 14:00 hrs**. Applications received after the deadline are automatically disqualified.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The assessment criteria for the proposals are:

- Quality of the PhD candidate (30%)
- Quality of the research proposal (70%).

### Procedure

The assessment of the proposals is done by one committee. Candidates are interviewed and assessed by committee members from their own discipline and neighbouring disciplines within the humanities.

- ### [Research data management](/en/research-data-management)

Responsible research data management is an essential component of good research practice. Prepare a data management plan after the grant is awarded and before the project starts.

## Prepare

Applications must be submitted through the Isaac application and reporting system. If you do not have an Isaac account yet, then please create one at least one workday in advance. More information about writing a proposal and which forms you need to use can be found in Isaac. Please download and use the forms in Isaac. Below you will find examples of the required forms.

### Write proposal

Only proposals of which the main discipline is part of the humanities are eligible for this grant. Apart from the requirement that the proposal must align with a main discipline within the humanities, the subject of the research project is entirely open. The evaluation of the proposal will be based on the criteria mentioned in section 4.3 of the Call for Proposals. The proposal must be written in English and should consist of the following components:

- (Academic) profile of the candidate ;
- Description of proposed research (total max. 3000 words) consisting of a) description of the research idea and b) a section on the anticipated scientific and/or societal impact (max. 750 of the 3000 words);
- Work plan (max. 1 A4);
- Description of institutional embedding and supervision (max. ½ A4);
- Budget section;
- Data management section.

### Fill in forms

For this Call, we ask you to fill in the application form as well as a budget form according to the provided template. 

If applicable, you are also required to submit a signed letter of confirmation from the master-thesis supervisor containing the expected graduation date of the candidate and/or a statement of appointment and project supervision.
