# Extreme Heat and Water Events: The Adverse Health Effects of Climate Change in Cities 2024

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/extreme-heat-and-water-events-the-adverse-health-effects-of-climate-change-in-cities-2024

This Call for proposals aims to fund collaborative research that investigates the health effects of climate change, specifically how extreme heat and/or water events impact human health and wellbeing in an urban context. The call ‘Extreme Heat and Water Events: The Adverse Health Effects of Climate Change in Cities 2024’ invites consortia with researchers from research institutions in both the Netherlands and Brazil to participate.

## Characteristics

**Status:** Closed

### Purpose

This Call for proposals aims to fund collaborative research that investigates the health effects of climate change; i.e., how extreme heat and/or water events affect human health and wellbeing in an urban context. Researchers can choose to focus their applications on either extreme heat- or water-related events or a combination of both.

The collaborative research should be disease-focused, encompass a broad perspective on the dynamics of cities, and advance scientific knowledge, as well as enhance knowledge exchange between researchers and stakeholders.

Projects should not only investigate the health impacts of extreme heat and/or water events, but aim for innovative solutions, paying special attention to the lives and environments of high-risk and vulnerable populations. They should ideally be relevant to both the Netherlands and Brazil.

### Who can apply

Consortia should be composed of:

- one main applicant based at an organisation in the (Kingdom of the) Netherlands, as listed in section 3.1.1 of the Call for proposals,
- one main applicant based in the State of São Paulo and,
- at least one collaboration partner from a public, semi-public or private practitioner organisation (for-profit or not-for-profit) from the Netherlands or from Brazil.
Proposals may also include one or more co-applicants based at an organisation in the Netherlands as listed in section 3.1.2 of the Call for proposals, or based in the State of São Paulo.

### What to apply for

Per project, a grant amount of at most € 700,000 can be applied for from NWO and a maximum of     R$ 4,000,000 can be applied for from FAPESP.

The maximum duration of the proposed project is 5 years. Reimbursable costs from NWO may include costs for personnel, equipment, knowledge utilisation and project management.

Funding and costs requested by the São Paulo researchers to FAPESP must be in accordance with FAPESP norms for (i) Thematic research project or (ii) Young Investigator Award.

### Assessment

### Criteria

The application will be assessed according to the following criteria:

1. Quality of the research,
2. Potential for societal impact,
3. Quality of the consortium.
All criteria carry equal weight.

### Procedure

Applications should be submitted to both NWO (via [ISAAC](https://isaac.nwo.nl/auth/realms/ISAAC/login-actions/authenticate?client_id=isaac-webapp-client&tab_id=T6coQVPn07I)) and FAPESP (via[SAGe](https://sage.fapesp.br/SAGe_WEB/jsp/loginAdm.jsp)). The procedure is carried out through the NWO application system ISAAC.

The NWO main applicant and the FAPESP main applicant are both responsible for sharing the reviewer reports, information regarding the rebuttal and any further information with the rest of the consortium.

The application procedure consists of the following steps:

- Submission of the proposal;
- Admissibility of the proposal;
- Peer review by referees;
- Rebuttal;
- Pre-advicing of the International Assessment Committee (IAC);
- Meeting of the International Assessment Committee (IAC);
- Decision-making by the Board of Trustees of FAPESP and, the NWO Executive Board.
The application, reviewer reports, and the consortium's rebuttal will be submitted to an International Assessment Committee (IAC) for evaluation. NWO and FAPESP will jointly compose this committee, with experts on the subject of the Call. The task of the committee is to assess the applications and the relevant documents that are submitted, in conjunction with each other and with regard to both the respective merits of each application and the assessment criteria outlined in this Call for proposals.

The NWO Executive Board and the Board of Trustees of FAPESP will assess the procedure followed as well as the advice from the committee and the advice of the FAPESP Executive Board. They will subsequently determine the final qualifications and make a decision over awarding or rejecting the proposals. The decision becomes definitive when the NWO Executive Board and the Board of Trustees of FAPESP have come to the same provisional decision.

### Write proposal

For the submission of the proposal, a standard form is available on the funding page of this Call for proposals on the NWO website. When writing the proposal, adhere to the questions stated on this form and the procedures explained in the explanatory notes. Please also adhere to the conditions for the maximum number of words and pages.

### Fill in forms

The application form consists of six sections. Each section contains further clarification and notes on how to fill out the related questions. When writing your proposal, please take into account that it will be assessed by both expert reviewers as well as the International Assessment Committee with a broad range of expertise. Please only include the annexes specified in the Call for proposals and application form. Additional annexes are not allowed, and will need to be removed from the application.

Documents are only available in English.
