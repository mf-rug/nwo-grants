# Use of space infrastructure for earth observation and planetary research (GO)

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/use-of-space-infrastructure-for-earth-observation-and-planetary-research-go

Through the programme Use of space infrastructure for Earth observation and planetary research (GO), NWO and the Netherlands Space Office (NSO) aim to support researchers making use of (international) space infrastructure. Researchers can apply for funding for a PhD student or postdoc who is going to work on an Earth observation or planetary research project for which space infrastructure (or the data thereof) is essential.

## Characteristics

**Status:** Closed

## Explore

### Purpose

This funding instrument is intended to support high-quality scientific research that makes substantial use of infrastructure in space. The infrastructure can be both (current or past) existing and planned infrastructure. Within this objective, the GO programme is open for scientific research in the area of Earth observation and planets (and other objects) within our solar system.

For this call, your proposal must fit within the objective of the GO programme. You should therefore consider in good time whether your proposal fits within this objective. If you have any doubts about this, please contact one of the contact persons for the programme well before the deadline. They can advise you regarding the connection of your proposal with this call.

### Who can apply

Researchers may submit an application if they have a tenured position (and therefore a paid position for an indefinite period) or a tenure track agreement at one of the following research organisations that are mentioned in section 3.1 of the Call for proposals.

It could be the case that the applicant’s tenure track agreement ends before the intended completion date of the project for which funding is applied for, or that before that date, the applicant’s tenured contract ends due to the applicant reaching retirement age. In that case, the applicant needs to include a statement from their employer in which the research organisation concerned guarantees that the project and all project members for whom funding has been requested will receive adequate supervision for the full duration of the project.

NB: the details can be found in the Call for proposals.

### What to apply for

A GO grant has a maximum amount of € 360,000. The grant must be used for a single temporary scientific position (a PhD student or postdoc) in combination with one of the other available budget modules.

### When to apply

- The deadline for submitting applications is **6 may 2023 at 14:00 hours CEST**

### Assessment

### Criteria

**Applications are assessed on the basis of the following three criteria:**

1. Scientific quality of the proposal – What? (weight 60%)
2. Appropriateness of the application to the GO programme (weight 10%)
3. Scientific and/or societal impact – Why (weight 30%)

### Procedure

Admissible applications are assessed by independent external experts (referees). The referees’ reports are sent to the applicants for a written rebuttal. The assessment committee ranks the applications on the basis of the assessment criteria and advises the Domain Science Board about the quality of the applications. Based on this advice and the available funding, the Domain Science Board decides whether to grant or reject the applications. The proposal as a whole and criteria 2 (appropriateness with the programme) must be rated at least 'very good' to be eligible for the grant.

Parallel assessment trajectories will be established for Earth observation and planetary research. When NWO appoints the members of the assessment committees, it will do its best to choose members whose expertise fits the applications.

### More information

Applications in the area of earth observation research and in the area of planetary research are assessed in parallel trajectories. The total grant ceiling for this Call for proposals is 3.24 million euros The available budget will be allocated across the two themes, with each theme having a set grant ceiling. A total of 2.16 million euros is available for the theme of Earth observation, and 1.08 million euros is available for the theme of planetary research.
The definition of ‘substantial use of space infrastructure’ is different for each theme. Please refer to the Call for proposals (see section 2.2).
