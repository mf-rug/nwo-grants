# Research Infrastructure (RI): national consortia

**Source:** https://www.nwo.nl/en/calls/research-infrastructure-ri-national-consortia-0

Good research infrastructure is essential for creating and maintaining a robust research system. Two preconditions of such infrastructure are wide accessibility and good structural funding. NWO stimulates the establishment and upgrades of largescale research facilities and research infrastructure that enable the Netherlands to capture and hold its prominent international position.

### Purpose

With this instrument, NWO aims to strengthen the RI available to the research community in the Netherlands. The instrument focuses on designing innovative RI that is of (inter)national importance, which can be used to achieve innovations and breakthroughs with a high value for science, society, and business. Proposals may be submitted by national consortia from all academic disciplines. A broad group of relevant representatives in the field from across the Netherlands must be involved at an early stage (when writing the proposal) and given a relevant role in governance.

### Who can apply

The main applicant submits the statement of intent and the completed proposal on behalf of the institution or consortium. There are four categories of participants for a consortium:

1. Main applicant
2. Co-applicant(s)
3. Partner(s) (optional)
4. Co-funder(s) (optional)
- Main applicants are employed by one of the following institutes:
universities as referred to in Article 1.8(1 of the Higher Education and Scientific Research Act and the universities listed in the Policy Rule Universities in the Kingdom of the Netherlands;
- university medical centres, meaning academic hospitals as referred to in Article 1.13(1) of the Higher Education and Scientific Research Act;
- institutes affiliated to the Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences (KNAW) or NWO;
- Netherlands Cancer Institute;
- the Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics in Nijmegen;
- Naturalis Biodiversity Center;
- Advanced Research Centre for NanoLithography (ARCNL);
- Princess Máxima Centre.
Co-applicants can be associated with research organisations mentioned above, and other research organisations mentioned in article 1.1, paragraph 4, of the NWO Grant Rules 2024 and that meet the cumulative conditions stated in the Call for proposals, section 3.1.1.

### Consortium building

For this call the forming of a consortium is necessary. Applicants are themselves responsible for finding partners. There are no obligatory meetings organised by NWO.

The submitted letters of intent are published on the NWO website. In the case you see the possibility to cooperate with another party after seeing their proposal, you can request their contact details at NWO.

### What to apply for

A minimum of € 1,500,000 and a maximum of € 10,000,000 (NWO contribution) can be applied for per RI project with a maximum project duration of 5 years. The own contribution of the applying consortium must be at least 25% of the total budget of the project application (NWO contribution + own contribution to the project). The total budget of a project application is therefore at least € 2,000,000.

Funding can be requested for the costs of the development, purchase, building or upgrade of an RI. This funding programme is not intended for financing the research to be conducted with the RI. Only project-specific costs are eligible for funding.

Capital investments can include the following types of costs:

- Personnel costs
- Investments
- Material costs
- IT costs

### When to apply

- The deadline for submitting letters of intent (required) is **2 December 2025, before 14:00:00 CET**.
- The deadline for applications is **24 March 2026, before 14:00:00 CET**.

### Assessment

### Criteria

Proposals are assessed against four criteria:

1. Scientific quality
2. Impact
3. Organisational and financial aspects
4. Technical aspects
The criteria have a weigh of 1/3, 1/3, 1/6, and 1/6, respectively.

### Procedure

The assessment procedure consists of the following steps:

- Submission of statements of intent, deadline 2 December 2025;
- Submission of a full proposal, deadline 24 March 2026;
- Peer review by referees, April 2026 - August 2026;
- Submission of a rebuttal, September-October 2026;
- First meeting Assessment Committee (site visit selection), November 2026;
- Site visits, end of November-December 2026;
- Second meeting Assessment Committee, January 2027;
- Decision by the Executive Board of NWO, March 2027.

## Prepare

Applications must be submitted through the Isaac application and reporting system. If you do not have an Isaac account yet, then please create one at least one workday in advance. More information about writing a proposal and which forms you need to use can be found in Isaac. Please download and use the forms in Isaac. Below you will find examples of the required forms.

### Write proposal

NWO will test your statement of intent and application for conditions set out in the Call for proposals. Only if this meets the conditions it will be processed. Submitting a statement of intent is a requirement to submit an application.

### Fill in forms

**Statement of intent**

Prior to submitting an application, the main applicant must announce the initiative digitally by submitting a statement of intent in ISAAC. The statement of intent is not assessed.

**Application**

For this call, we ask you to submit the following attachments in addition to filling in the application form. For all attachments, a mandatory format can be downloaded from this page or from the online application system ISAAC.

1. The completed budget format in Excel;
2. The completed and signed submission letter from the highest administrative body of the institution or organisation that acts as the official secretary of the consortium;
3. For each applying institution, a signed letter of guarantee confirming the own contribution;
4. For each co-funder, a Declaration of Co-funding;
5. if necessary, declaration of appointment and project supervision.
